> **TL;DR**
> 1. Residential service laterals are typically **4 in** PVC at a minimum slope of **2.0%** (1/4 in per ft). Commercial and multi-family laterals are **6 in** minimum.
> 2. Connect to the main with a **factory wye (tee)** installed during main construction or a **saddle tap** for after-the-fact connections. Saddle taps require a core drill and a gasketed fitting — never break into the main with a saw cut alone.
> 3. Provide a **cleanout** at the property line (or within 5 ft of the right-of-way line) and at every change in direction exceeding 45 degrees. Cleanout must be accessible from grade.
> 4. Maintain minimum **depth at the property line** of 3.0 ft (to finished grade) in most Indiana jurisdictions, with a target of 5 to 6 ft to serve basement plumbing.

## Lateral sizing

| Service type | Minimum diameter | Minimum slope | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-family residential | 4 in | 2.0% (1/4 in/ft) | SDR-35 PVC typical |
| Duplex / townhouse | 4 in | 2.0% | One lateral per unit, or 6 in shared |
| Multi-family (≥ 4 units) | 6 in | 1.5% | Confirm with local utility |
| Small commercial | 6 in | 1.5% | Size by fixture-unit count |
| Large commercial / institutional | 6 in to 8 in | per design | Grease interceptor may be required |
| Industrial | by design | by design | Pretreatment review required |

Ten States §34 sets 4 in as the minimum for a building sewer. Most Indiana utilities adopt this for single-family residential and require 6 in for anything with higher fixture counts. The lateral slope should produce a velocity of at least 2.0 ft/s at design flow; the 2.0% minimum for 4-in PVC exceeds this at any reasonable residential flow.

## Connection methods

### Factory wye or tee

The preferred method when laterals are installed at the same time as the main. A factory-molded wye branch is incorporated into the main pipe at the specified station during pipe laying. Advantages:

- Watertight factory joint with gasket.
- Correct alignment (45-degree wye angled in the direction of flow).
- No weakening of the main pipe wall.

Most Indiana plan reviews require wye locations to be shown on the construction plans, with stub-outs capped and marked with a stake or locator wire extending to finished grade.

### Saddle tap

Used for connections made after the main is installed. A saddle fitting clamps around the main pipe, and a core drill cuts a hole through the pipe wall. Key requirements:

- Use a gasketed, manufacturer-approved saddle fitting (ASTM D3034 for PVC-to-PVC, ASTM C425 for PVC-to-concrete).
- Core drill — never saw-cut. A saw cut creates an irregular opening that cannot be sealed.
- The hole must be drilled at the correct angle (typically 10 o'clock or 2 o'clock position on the pipe crown side, or at the springline). Never drill into the invert — flow will erode the joint.
- Saddle taps on mains 8 in to 12 in for a 4-in lateral are standard. Tapping a main smaller than twice the lateral diameter is not recommended — use a manhole or junction structure instead.

### Manhole connection

For laterals larger than half the main diameter, or when the main is 24 in or larger, connect the lateral through a manhole with a gasketed boot (ASTM C923). This gives a positive seal, allows inspection, and avoids weakening a large-diameter main with a saddle.

## Depth at the property line

The lateral must be deep enough at the property line (or the right-of-way line, whichever is applicable) to serve the lowest fixture in the building. In practice:

- **Basement plumbing** — the lateral must be below the basement floor elevation. For a typical 8-ft basement with a floor at about 7 ft below grade, the lateral invert at the building should be at least 8 ft deep. Working back at 2.0% slope to the main (which may be 50 to 150 ft away), the invert at the main may be 9 to 11 ft below grade.
- **Slab-on-grade buildings** — the lateral can be shallower, typically 3.0 to 5.0 ft at the property line.
- **Minimum cover** — 3.0 ft over the pipe crown to finished grade in trafficked areas. 18 in to 24 in in landscaped areas with no traffic (some jurisdictions).

If the main sewer is too shallow to serve basement plumbing by gravity, the building will require an ejector pump. Document this on the plat and in the plan narrative so that lot purchasers are informed.

## Cleanouts

A cleanout is a capped fitting at grade that provides access for cleaning and inspection equipment. Requirements:

- **At the property line** — required by most Indiana utilities. Located within 5 ft of the right-of-way line, inside the easement if the lateral is in an easement. Marked with a cast-iron frame-and-cover at grade.
- **At changes in direction** — a cleanout (or manhole for laterals 6 in and larger) at every change in horizontal direction exceeding 45 degrees.
- **At building entry** — a two-way cleanout where the lateral enters the building foundation is required by the Indiana Plumbing Code (675 IAC 16-5-3).
- **Maximum spacing** — 75 ft to 100 ft between cleanouts along a long lateral run (confirm with the local utility).

Cleanout risers are typically 4-in PVC with a threaded or gasketed cap. In landscaped areas, set the cleanout flush with grade in a valve box or with a surface-mounted cover.

## Backwater valves

Indiana building codes (675 IAC 16) require a backwater valve on any fixture below the next upstream manhole rim elevation, which in practice means all basement fixtures. The backwater valve prevents sewage from backing up through the lateral into the building during surcharge events.

The backwater valve is the property owner's responsibility, installed on the building sewer inside or near the building. The engineer/surveyor does not design it, but should note the requirement on the plans when basement connections are involved.

## Common review issues

- Lateral connected to the main at the invert (bottom) — connection should be at the springline or above to avoid invert erosion and backup.
- No cleanout shown at the property line — flagged.
- Saw-cut saddle tap specified instead of a core-drilled gasketed saddle — fail.
- Lateral slope less than 2.0% for 4-in — flagged for correction.
- Lateral stub-outs not shown on the plan view with station and offset — required for as-built records.
- No notation about basement service limitations where the main is shallow — add a plan note.
