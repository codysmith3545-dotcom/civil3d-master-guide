> **TL;DR**
> 1. Rule 13 (327 IAC 15-13) is Indiana's implementation of the federal **NPDES Phase II** stormwater program for **Municipal Separate Storm Sewer Systems (MS4s)**. It applies to urbanized areas and designated communities.
> 2. MS4 entities must implement **six minimum control measures** (MCMs): public education, public involvement, illicit discharge detection, construction-site runoff control, post-construction stormwater management, and good housekeeping.
> 3. For land developers and engineers, the most directly impactful MCMs are **MCM 4** (construction-site runoff, which links to [Rule 5](idem-rule-5.md)) and **MCM 5** (post-construction BMPs, which links to [water-quality BMPs](water-quality-bmps.md)).
> 4. MS4 entities submit an **annual report** to IDEM documenting compliance. Failure to comply can result in IDEM enforcement or federal Clean Water Act penalties.

## What an MS4 is

A Municipal Separate Storm Sewer System (MS4) is a publicly owned conveyance system (storm sewers, ditches, channels, retention/detention basins) that discharges to waters of the United States and is not part of a combined sewer system (CSS) or a publicly owned treatment works (POTW). The term "municipal" is broad — it includes cities, towns, counties, townships, state agencies (INDOT), universities, military installations, and other public entities.

Under the Clean Water Act §402(p) and 40 CFR 122.34, operators of "small MS4s" (serving populations under 100,000 in urbanized areas) must obtain NPDES permit coverage and implement a stormwater quality program. In Indiana this is accomplished through Rule 13.

## Which Indiana communities are MS4s

IDEM maintains the list of designated MS4 entities. As of the current permit cycle, most Indiana communities within U.S. Census-designated urbanized areas are MS4s. In the Indianapolis metropolitan area and surrounding counties:

- **Marion County**: City of Indianapolis, all included towns, and Marion County government (all operated through Indianapolis DPW / Citizens Energy for stormwater).
- **Hamilton County**: Carmel, Fishers, Westfield, Noblesville, Hamilton County government, plus smaller designated areas.
- **Boone County**: Zionsville, Whitestown, portions of Lebanon.
- **Hendricks County**: Plainfield, Avon, Brownsburg, Hendricks County government.
- **Johnson County**: Greenwood, portions of Johnson County.
- **Hancock County**: portions near the Indianapolis urbanized area.
- **Morgan County**: portions near the Indianapolis urbanized area.
- **Shelby County**: portions near the Indianapolis urbanized area.
- **State agencies**: INDOT, state universities (Purdue, IU), military installations (Camp Atterbury).

Communities outside urbanized areas may be designated by IDEM if they discharge to impaired waters or have water quality concerns.

## Six minimum control measures (MCMs)

### MCM 1 — Public education and outreach

The MS4 entity must educate the public about the impacts of stormwater discharges and actions residents can take (picking up pet waste, reducing fertilizer use, proper disposal of household chemicals).

### MCM 2 — Public involvement and participation

The MS4 must provide opportunities for public input on the stormwater management program — public meetings, advisory committees, volunteer monitoring, stream cleanups.

### MCM 3 — Illicit discharge detection and elimination (IDDE)

The MS4 must map its storm sewer system (outfalls, pipes, and connections), adopt an ordinance prohibiting illicit discharges, and implement a detection and elimination program (dry-weather outfall screening, dye testing, smoke testing).

### MCM 4 — Construction-site runoff control

The MS4 must adopt an ordinance requiring erosion and sediment control on construction sites and enforce it through plan review and site inspections. This is the local implementation of [IDEM Rule 5](idem-rule-5.md). For engineers and surveyors, MCM 4 means:

- The SWPPP and erosion-control plan must be reviewed and approved by the MS4 entity (city, county, or SWCD) before construction begins.
- The MS4 inspector conducts periodic site inspections in addition to the operator's self-inspections.
- Violations can result in stop-work orders, fines, and referral to IDEM.

### MCM 5 — Post-construction stormwater management

The MS4 must adopt an ordinance requiring permanent stormwater quality BMPs on new development and redevelopment sites that disturb 1.0 acre or more. For engineers and surveyors, MCM 5 means:

- Post-construction BMP design must meet the local technical standards (water-quality volume, TSS removal targets, drawdown times). See [water-quality BMPs](water-quality-bmps.md).
- A recorded maintenance agreement is required before the final plat is approved or the occupancy permit is issued.
- The MS4 entity inspects post-construction BMPs periodically (typically annually or biennially) and can require the owner to perform maintenance.

### MCM 6 — Good housekeeping for municipal operations

The MS4 must manage its own facilities and operations (streets, parks, fleet maintenance, salt storage) to minimize stormwater pollution. This includes street sweeping, catch-basin cleaning, salt/sand storage management, and stormwater infrastructure maintenance.

## Co-permittee arrangements

Many Indiana MS4 communities operate as **co-permittees**, sharing responsibilities under a joint permit or a cooperative agreement. For example:

- A county government and its municipalities may file a joint NOI and share program elements (mapping, IDDE, education).
- A county SWCD may administer MCM 4 (construction-site plan review and inspection) on behalf of multiple municipalities.
- INDOT is a separate MS4 permittee for state highway right-of-way, but coordinates with local MS4s at shared discharge points.

When a development project straddles jurisdictional boundaries (county road with city drainage, or a state highway frontage), determine which MS4 entity reviews the stormwater plan and whether multiple approvals are needed.

## Annual reporting

Each MS4 entity submits an **annual report** to IDEM documenting compliance with each MCM. The report covers:

- Actions taken during the reporting year (educational events, ordinance updates, inspections conducted, illicit discharges found and eliminated, BMPs installed and inspected).
- Measurable goals and whether they were met.
- Changes to the stormwater management plan.
- Total acreage of land disturbance permitted under MCM 4.
- Number and type of post-construction BMPs installed and inspected under MCM 5.

IDEM reviews the annual reports and may audit the MS4's program. Audit findings can lead to required corrective actions or enforcement.

## How Rule 13 affects site plan review

For the surveyor or engineer submitting a site development plan in an MS4 community:

1. **Check whether the site is in an MS4 area.** If it is, post-construction BMPs are required on any project disturbing 1.0 acre or more.
2. **Obtain the local stormwater technical standards.** Every MS4 community publishes (or should publish) a technical standards manual that specifies the water-quality storm depth, acceptable BMP types, sizing methods, maintenance requirements, and plan-submittal format.
3. **Submit the stormwater management plan** to the MS4 entity for review. This is typically a separate submittal from the Rule 5 / SWPPP and may be reviewed by a different department (engineering vs. SWCD).
4. **Obtain approval before recording the plat or obtaining a building permit.** The MS4 entity's approval of the post-construction BMP plan is a prerequisite for final plat or site-plan approval in most Indiana communities.
5. **Record the maintenance agreement.** The recorded instrument is a condition of approval.

## Common review issues

- Site plan submitted without a post-construction BMP plan in an MS4 area — returned for revision.
- BMP design references a standard from a different MS4 community — use the standards of the community where the site is located.
- No maintenance agreement submitted — required before final plat approval.
- Stormwater plan addresses detention (quantity) but not water quality — both are required; they are independent design criteria.
- Project straddles a city/county boundary and only one MS4 entity was consulted — both may need to review.
