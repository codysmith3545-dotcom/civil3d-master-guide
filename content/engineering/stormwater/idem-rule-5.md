> **TL;DR**
> 1. Rule 5 (327 IAC 15-5) is Indiana's NPDES construction stormwater general permit. It applies to any land disturbance of **1.0 acre or more**, including projects that are part of a larger common plan of development that totals ≥ 1 acre.
> 2. The owner/operator submits a **Notice of Intent (NOI)** at least **48 hours before** earth-disturbing activity, posts the NOI letter on site, and implements a **Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)** approved by the local MS4 (or by IDEM if the site is in a non-MS4 area).
> 3. Inspections are required weekly and within 24 hours after a storm event of 0.5 in or more. Records are retained for 3 years after Notice of Termination (NOT).
> 4. When ownership changes or construction completes, file the appropriate **Notice of Transfer** or **Notice of Termination** to close the permit.

## Authority and scope

Rule 5 is codified at 327 IAC 15-5 and is administered by the Indiana Department of Environmental Management (IDEM) Office of Water Quality. It is a general permit issued under the federal NPDES program (Clean Water Act §402) and Indiana's delegated authority. Rule 5 covers stormwater discharges from construction sites; the post-construction MS4 program lives in Rule 13 (327 IAC 15-13).

## When Rule 5 applies

Coverage is required when **all** of the following are true:

- Land disturbance ≥ 1.0 acre (cumulatively, including off-site borrow and spoil areas), or
- Disturbance is part of a larger common plan of development or sale that totals ≥ 1.0 acre even if the individual phase is smaller.
- The project will, or could, discharge stormwater off-site or to waters of the U.S. or waters of the State.

Some single-family lots within a permitted subdivision are covered by the master Rule 5 permit and do not need a separate NOI; the homebuilder still has site-level erosion-control responsibilities. Linear projects, agricultural land disturbance, and routine maintenance are governed by separate provisions or exclusions in 327 IAC 15-5-3.

## Notice of Intent (NOI)

The NOI is the formal request for permit coverage. It is filed with IDEM and copied to the local Soil and Water Conservation District (SWCD) and the MS4 entity that has jurisdiction. Required elements:

- Project name, location, and lat/long.
- Owner / developer / operator information.
- Acreage of disturbance.
- Receiving waters and watershed (HUC-12).
- A complete SWPPP attached to or incorporated by reference in the submittal.
- A signed certification by an authorized representative.

Submit at least **48 hours before** earth-disturbing activity begins. IDEM provides paper and electronic forms; many MS4 communities run a separate local plan review and approval process that is the practical gate.

## Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)

The SWPPP is the working document on site. Required content per 327 IAC 15-5-7:

1. **Site description** — existing conditions, soils, slopes, receiving waters, predevelopment land use.
2. **Project description** — proposed conditions, sequence of construction, schedule.
3. **Site map** — limits of disturbance, drainage patterns existing and proposed, BMP locations, soil stockpiles, concrete washout, fueling areas.
4. **Calculations** — sediment basin sizing (when required), sediment trap sizing, channel and inlet protection.
5. **BMP details and specifications** — silt fence, inlet protection, stabilized construction entrance, sediment basins/traps, dust control, dewatering, concrete washout, vehicle wash, fueling, hazardous materials, dewatering filter bags, slope stabilization, channel stabilization, riprap aprons, soil stockpile management, landscape stabilization.
6. **Inspection and maintenance plan** — frequency, responsible person, log forms.
7. **Spill prevention and emergency response procedures.**
8. **Post-construction stormwater quality measures** — required by Rule 13 but typically incorporated in the Rule 5 SWPPP.
9. **Record drawings of all BMPs and post-construction features** — kept current as construction proceeds.

The Indiana Stormwater Quality Manual (the "ISQM") published by IDEM and the SWCDs is the standard reference for BMP design details. Many Indiana cities have additional requirements layered on top.

## Inspections and recordkeeping

The permittee is required to:

- Inspect all BMPs and disturbed areas **at least once per week** and **within 24 hours after a storm event of 0.5 in or more** (per 327 IAC 15-5-7.5).
- Document each inspection — date, weather, BMP condition, deficiencies, corrective actions, and the inspector's name and qualifications. The qualified inspector must be a "trained individual" as defined in the rule.
- Take corrective action within 7 days for non-functional BMPs (faster if a discharge is occurring).
- Keep all inspection records on site and available to IDEM, the SWCD, and the MS4 inspector for 3 years after NOT.

Some MS4 communities (including most Hamilton County cities) require that the inspection log be submitted to the city monthly or be available on site for unannounced audits.

## Transfer and termination

- **Notice of Transfer (NOT-T)** — when ownership or operational control changes, the new owner files a Notice of Transfer to assume coverage. Until the transfer is filed and accepted, the prior owner remains responsible.
- **Notice of Termination (NOT)** — file when **all** disturbed areas are stabilized (typically 70% perennial vegetation cover or equivalent), all temporary BMPs are removed, and post-construction stormwater quality measures are operating per design. Premature NOT is a common enforcement issue — IDEM and MS4s look for the perennial vegetation criterion specifically.

## Common enforcement issues

- NOI filed less than 48 hours before earthwork (or not at all).
- SWPPP map does not match field conditions — BMPs missing or in wrong locations.
- No inspection log or log not kept current.
- Discharge of sediment-laden water from a site without a sediment basin or trap when one is required (≥ 10 ac drainage to a single discharge point usually triggers a basin).
- Concrete washout located where it can discharge to a storm inlet.
- Track-out from the construction entrance onto a public street.
- NOT filed before stabilization is achieved.
