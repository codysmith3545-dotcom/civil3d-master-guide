> **TL;DR**
> 1. Any Indiana construction project disturbing **≥ 1 acre** (or a smaller site in a common plan of development totaling ≥ 1 acre) is regulated by the Indiana Construction General Permit (CGP) and must submit a **Notice of Intent (NOI)** with a complete Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) at least **48 hours before earth disturbance**.
> 2. Self-inspections are required **at least once every 7 calendar days** and **within 24 hours of a 0.5-in or greater rainfall event** (Indiana CGP, EPA 2022 CGP).
> 3. Project closure requires final stabilization (70% perennial vegetative cover or equivalent), a **Notice of Termination (NOT)**, and post-construction BMPs operational and accepted by the MS4 or property owner.

## What triggers a SWPPP

The CGP applies to:

- Any construction activity disturbing ≥ 1 acre of land.
- Smaller activities that are part of a **larger common plan of development or sale** when the total project exceeds 1 acre.
- Activities that discharge into impaired waters (303(d) listed) even if smaller, at IDEM discretion.

The trigger is **soil disturbance**, not project value. A 1.0-ac parking lot resurface that includes regrading triggers; a 1.0-ac re-asphalt over an existing intact subbase typically does not.

## Required SWPPP / ESCP contents (Indiana CGP)

Indiana uses "ESCP" (Erosion and Sediment Control Plan) for the plan-set portion and "SWPPP" for the broader operational document. Both must contain:

1. **Site description** — boundaries, total area, area to be disturbed, existing land cover, soils, slopes, drainage patterns, receiving waters (stream name + 303(d) status).
2. **Sequencing** — phasing schedule showing the order in which BMPs are installed, soil is disturbed, and stabilization is achieved. Required by Indiana CGP §15-5-7.
3. **BMP design and detail** — for every selected BMP: location on the site plan, design calculation (for sized BMPs), construction detail, maintenance frequency.
4. **Map** — site map at appropriate scale (typically 1" = 50' or 1" = 100') showing existing topography, proposed grading, all BMPs, drainage subbasins, and receiving water discharge points.
5. **Pollution prevention** — concrete washout, fuel storage, dumpster placement, dewatering, sanitary facility locations.
6. **Operator contact** — names of permitted operator, qualified inspector, owner.
7. **Inspection schedule and log forms** — template forms for the inspections required during construction.
8. **Final stabilization plan** — seeding mix, mulch, blanket selection, dates of seasonal closure.

INDOT IDM Chapter 205 expands on these for state highway projects with INDOT-specific BMP details.

## Submittal: NOI and timing

Indiana NOI is filed online through IDEM's Virtual File Cabinet (VFC). Process:

1. Operator completes the Indiana CGP NOI form, attaches the SWPPP/ESCP package.
2. Most counties also require a **county-level review** (by the SWCD or county engineer); some delegate inspection authority entirely to IDEM, others retain it.
3. **48-hour minimum** between NOI acceptance and beginning of construction. Many MS4 communities require pre-construction meeting after NOI but before grade-break.

## Inspection cadence

Indiana CGP §15-5-8:

- **Every 7 calendar days minimum.**
- **Within 24 hours of a precipitation event of 0.5 in or more in 24 hr.**

EPA 2022 CGP allows monthly inspections during temporary stabilization (after a site is dormant and 70%+ vegetated for ≥ 14 days); Indiana CGP has not formally adopted this option as of 2026 — confirm in the current IDEM publication.

Each inspection requires:

- Inspector's name and qualification (certified Indiana CISEC / CESCO or equivalent).
- Date, time, weather, recent rainfall.
- Per-BMP condition and corrective action required.
- Photographs (recommended; required by many MS4s).
- Signed report retained on site, available to IDEM / MS4 on request.

Corrective actions must be completed **within 7 calendar days** of identification.

## Final stabilization and NOT

A site is "finally stabilized" when:

- All soil-disturbing activities are complete.
- A uniform perennial vegetative cover of at least **70% density** is established on all unpaved areas, **or** an equivalent permanent stabilization (mulch, rolled blankets, riprap, pavement) is in place.
- All temporary BMPs (silt fence, inlet protection, sediment basins beyond their permanent function) have been removed and the disturbed footprint of removal has been stabilized.

Submit Notice of Termination (NOT). The CGP closes when IDEM acknowledges the NOT.

## Common review / inspection findings

- SWPPP map shows existing topography only; current grading is not reflected. Update at each major sequence step.
- Inspector certification expired. Renew prior to next inspection.
- 0.5-in rainfall on Wednesday, inspection log shows weekly inspection only. Missing the 24-hr post-rain inspection.
- Silt fence J-hooks omitted at low points on long slopes (see [silt-fence-installation](silt-fence-installation.md)).
- 70% vegetative cover claimed but visual estimate is closer to 40%. Re-seed or extend the schedule.

## Worked example: NOI timeline

50-ac subdivision in Hamilton County, planned grade-break May 1.

- Mar 15 — SWPPP/ESCP submitted to Hamilton County SWCD for review.
- Apr 1 — SWCD review complete, no comments.
- Apr 5 — NOI filed via IDEM VFC, fee paid.
- Apr 8 — NOI accepted by IDEM, project assigned permit tracking number.
- Apr 15 — Pre-construction meeting with county and contractor.
- Apr 27 — Contractor installs perimeter BMPs (silt fence, inlets, construction entrance) and stabilized construction entrance.
- Apr 29 — Initial inspection by qualified inspector documenting BMPs in place. Photos taken.
- May 1 — Grade-break begins.
- Weekly inspections + 24-hr post-rain inspections continue throughout construction.
- Final stabilization achieved, NOT submitted at the end of the project.
