> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana CGP "Phase 2" covers the **post-construction stormwater controls** — typically the permanent BMPs that handle the water-quality volume (WQv) and any required quantity-control basins after the site is built out.
> 2. Hand-off to the MS4 (or to the property HOA where the MS4 does not take over) requires: as-built drawings, **operation & maintenance (O&M) manual**, recorded **drainage easements** over every BMP, and a long-term **maintenance covenant** or agreement.
> 3. The Notice of Termination cannot be approved until the post-construction BMPs are **installed, functional, and accepted by the responsible entity** (MS4, HOA, owner) per Indiana CGP §15-5-7.

## What Phase 2 covers

Indiana's CGP has historically distinguished:

- **Phase 1 / construction phase BMPs:** temporary controls during earth disturbance (silt fence, check dams, sediment basins acting as forebays).
- **Phase 2 / post-construction BMPs:** permanent controls in service after construction — typically the bioretention, wet pond, sand filter, underground detention system, or treatment device that achieves the water-quality TSS reduction target (typically 80%).

The CGP requires both, but the regulatory hand-off differs: construction BMPs are removed at site closure; post-construction BMPs persist and become the responsibility of the MS4 community, HOA, or property owner.

## Required deliverables

At project closeout, the post-construction package typically includes:

1. **As-built drawings** of every post-construction BMP — bioretention media depth and gradation, basin top of berm, outlet structure elevations, underdrain alignment, etc. Indiana practice: as-builts certified by a P.E. and accepted by the MS4.
2. **O&M manual** specific to each BMP, including:
    - Maintenance frequency and tasks (e.g., bioretention mulch refresh annually, sediment removal at 6 in accumulation, vegetation replacement seasonally).
    - Trigger conditions (e.g., ponding > 48 hr indicates underdrain failure).
    - Equipment and access requirements.
    - Estimated annual maintenance budget.
3. **Drainage easements** over each BMP and over access routes, recorded with the county.
4. **Maintenance covenant** or O&M agreement — usually a recorded document that binds successor owners to the maintenance obligation. Indianapolis DPW and Hamilton County require this for HOA-maintained BMPs.
5. **Final stabilization certification** of the BMP and contributing areas.
6. **Notice of Termination (NOT)** with the post-construction documentation attached.

## MS4 hand-off vs HOA hand-off

Indiana MS4 communities vary:

- **MS4 takes ownership** of post-construction BMPs in the public right-of-way or in dedicated MS4 tracts. The MS4 inspects and maintains under its own CGP-15-13 permit.
- **MS4 retains oversight, HOA maintains** in subdivisions where BMPs lie within common areas. The MS4 inspects periodically (annual or biennial); the HOA performs routine maintenance. Most Indiana subdivisions in Hamilton, Hancock, Hendricks counties.
- **Private owner maintains** for commercial / industrial sites. The MS4 enforces via the local stormwater ordinance and annual inspection.

## Inspection frequency post-NOT

Per Indiana MS4 General Permit (327 IAC 15-13) and most MS4 ordinances:

| BMP type | Routine inspection | After major storm (≥ 2-yr event) |
|---|---|---|
| Wet pond / wet ED pond | Annual | After |
| Bioretention | Annual + after each season | After |
| Sand filter | Annual | After |
| Underground detention | Annual + every 5-yr CCTV | After |
| Manufactured treatment device | Per manufacturer (typ. quarterly) | After |
| Permeable pavement | Annual + every 2-yr vacuum sweep | After |

## Easement language

Drainage easements for BMPs should:

- Cover the BMP plus a minimum **15-ft maintenance access** around the perimeter (Indianapolis DPW requires 20 ft for any basin > 0.5 ac surface area).
- Be **non-exclusive** to allow MS4 access regardless of who maintains.
- Cite the recorded O&M agreement by instrument number.
- Be drawn to the **top of berm + 5-ft buffer** on detention basins (not just the high-water mark), so equipment access on the dry side is included.

## Worked example: Phase 2 hand-off for a 75-lot subdivision

Carmel, IN. Single bioretention cell at the rear of the subdivision sized for WQv = 12,000 cf and 1-yr / 10-yr / 100-yr stage-storage control.

Closeout package:

- As-built: media gradation lab report, surface elevation 814.50, underdrain invert 812.00, IE of outlet 815.50 (WQv elevation), outlet 100-yr crest 818.00, top of berm 820.00. P.E. stamp.
- O&M manual: 12 pages, including: annual mulch refresh (3-in cedar bark), semi-annual ponding-time test (must drain WQv in ≤ 48 hr), 5-yr media replacement, vegetation maintenance list (no broad-leaf herbicides, mowing 4-in minimum).
- Easement: 20-ft buffer around the basin extent, recorded on plat as drainage and utility easement, instrument 2026-12345.
- Maintenance covenant: HOA responsible for annual O&M; Carmel inspects annually.
- Final stabilization: 80% perennial cover verified by Carmel inspector on date Y.
- NOT submitted with above documentation; IDEM closes the CGP file.

## Common review / hand-off problems

- As-built shows the bioretention surface elevation 0.5 ft higher than design — reduces ponding storage; correct or revise calcs.
- O&M manual is a generic template with no site-specific values — rejected by reviewer.
- Easement covers only the basin extent, no maintenance access — rejected.
- Underdrain cleanout omitted at the upstream end of the perforated pipe — adds to maintenance cost forever.
- HOA documents reference a maintenance obligation but the actual instrument is not recorded — fails Indianapolis DPW acceptance.
