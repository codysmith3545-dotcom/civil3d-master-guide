> **TL;DR**
> 1. The **ESCP (Erosion and Sediment Control Plan)** is the plan sheet showing BMP locations, limits of disturbance, and construction sequence. The **SWPPP (Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan)** is the project binder kept on site with the NOI, inspection logs, and corrective actions.
> 2. Every project disturbing 1 acre or more (or part of a common plan of development that does) needs both an ESCP and a SWPPP in Indiana.
> 3. The ESCP is a design document (drawn by the engineer). The SWPPP is an operational document (maintained by the contractor during construction).

## ESCP plan sheet contents

The ESCP is typically one or more sheets in the plan set showing:

### Required elements

- **Existing contours** — showing pre-construction topography and drainage patterns.
- **Proposed contours** — showing the finished-grade topography.
- **Limits of disturbance (LOD)** — the maximum extent of grading, clearing, and construction activity. Drawn as a heavy line.
- **BMP locations** — every erosion and sediment control measure shown with standard symbols: silt fence, inlet protection, sediment basins/traps, construction entrance, check dams, diversions, erosion blankets, and temporary seeding areas.
- **Construction sequence notes** — numbered steps describing the order of BMP installation, earthwork, utility installation, paving, and final stabilization.
- **Stabilization schedule** — identifies which areas receive temporary stabilization and when (within 14 days of final grade in Indiana for areas not actively being worked).
- **BMP detail references** — callouts to standard details (INDOT or local standards) for silt fence installation, inlet protection types, construction entrance, etc.
- **Drainage area map** — showing subcatchments, flow arrows, and the receiving waters.
- **Legend and notes** — BMP symbols, responsible parties, inspection frequency, maintenance notes.

### Optional but recommended

- **Phasing plan** — separate sheets for each construction phase (initial clearing, rough grading, utility installation, fine grading, paving, final stabilization).
- **Dewatering plan** — if excavation will encounter groundwater.
- **Winter construction plan** — if work continues through the non-growing season.

## SWPPP binder contents

The SWPPP is a site-specific document kept on site (or readily accessible) during construction. Required contents:

1. **Copy of the NOI** — the Notice of Intent filed with IDEM (Indiana) or EPA.
2. **NOI acknowledgment letter** — IDEM's confirmation of coverage.
3. **Site map** — typically a copy of the ESCP plan sheet.
4. **Description of construction activity** — project narrative, schedule, phasing.
5. **Description of BMPs** — installation, maintenance, and removal procedures for each BMP type used.
6. **Inspection log** — records of every inspection (at least every 7 days and within 24 hours of each 0.5-inch rain event).
7. **Rain log** — daily precipitation record (from on-site gauge or nearby NOAA station).
8. **Corrective action log** — documentation of BMP failures and the repairs made.
9. **Contractor certifications** — signed acknowledgments by the contractor and any co-permittees.
10. **Amendments** — changes to BMPs, phasing, or responsible parties documented as amendments to the SWPPP.

The SWPPP must be retained for at least 3 years after the NOT (Notice of Termination) is filed.

## ESCP design process

1. Delineate the limits of disturbance.
2. Identify all discharge points from the site and the receiving waters.
3. Calculate the disturbed area within each subcatchment.
4. Size perimeter controls (silt fence for sheet flow; sediment basins for concentrated flow or large disturbed areas).
5. Place inlet protection at every storm inlet within or adjacent to the disturbed area.
6. Design the stabilized construction entrance (minimum 50 ft long, 20 ft wide, 6-inch stone depth).
7. Show temporary stabilization areas and permanent stabilization areas.
8. Write the construction sequence.
