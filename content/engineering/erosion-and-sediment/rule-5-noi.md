> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana Rule 5 (327 IAC 15-5) requires a stormwater permit (NOI filing) for any construction activity that disturbs **1 acre or more** of land, or is part of a common plan of development that will ultimately disturb 1 acre or more.
> 2. The NOI must be filed with IDEM **at least 48 hours before** construction begins. Filing is done online through the IDEM Virtual File Cabinet (VFC) / e-Reporting system.
> 3. Both the **owner and the contractor** are co-permittees — both are legally responsible for compliance.

## Rule 5 triggers

The permit requirement applies when:

- The project will disturb 1 acre or more of land, OR
- The project is part of a larger common plan of development or sale that will cumulatively disturb 1 acre or more (e.g., a 0.5-acre lot in a 20-acre subdivision).

"Disturbance" includes clearing, grading, excavation, stockpiling, filling, and demolition. It does not include agricultural tillage.

## NOI filing process

1. **Prepare the SWPPP** — the SWPPP must be complete before the NOI is filed.
2. **File the NOI online** — through IDEM's e-Reporting portal. The NOI includes:
   - Project name, location, county, and receiving water body.
   - Total project area and area to be disturbed.
   - Estimated start and end dates.
   - Owner and operator (contractor) information.
   - Name of the certified SWPPP designer.
3. **Pay the fee** — the filing fee is set by IDEM (currently in the range of $100-$250 depending on project size; confirm current fee schedule on IDEM's website).
4. **Receive acknowledgment** — IDEM assigns a permit number. Coverage is effective 48 hours after filing (not upon acknowledgment).
5. **Post the NOI** — a copy of the NOI and permit number must be posted at the site in a visible location.

## Co-permittees

Both the **land owner** and the **contractor performing the work** are co-permittees. If additional contractors perform land disturbance, they must also be added as co-permittees (by filing a co-permittee NOI). Each co-permittee is independently liable for violations.

This means the owner cannot disclaim responsibility by hiring a contractor, and the contractor cannot claim the owner is solely responsible.

## Requirements during construction

Once the NOI is active:

- Implement the ESCP and maintain all BMPs per the SWPPP.
- Conduct inspections per the required schedule (see [Inspections](inspections.md)).
- Temporarily stabilize areas not actively worked within 14 days.
- Keep the SWPPP binder on site (or readily available) and update it with inspection reports and amendments.
- Report any non-compliance to IDEM (spills, BMP failures that result in off-site sediment discharge).

## Notice of Termination (NOT)

The permit remains active until a NOT is filed. The NOT can be filed when:

- All construction activity is complete.
- **Final stabilization** has been achieved: 70% vegetative cover on all disturbed areas, or equivalent permanent stabilization (riprap, pavement, buildings) — see [Stabilization](stabilization.md).
- All temporary BMPs have been removed.
- The SWPPP has been updated and archived.

File the NOT through the same IDEM online portal. Retain the SWPPP records for at least 3 years after the NOT.

## Penalties

IDEM can issue Notices of Violation (NOVs), Agreed Orders, and civil penalties for Rule 5 violations. Penalties can reach $25,000 per day per violation under the Indiana Environmental Management Act. Common violations:

- Failure to file the NOI before beginning work.
- Missing or incomplete SWPPP.
- Failed or missing BMPs (silt fence down, no inlet protection, no construction entrance).
- Failure to conduct required inspections.
- Sediment discharge to waters of the state.

## Local MS4 programs

In the Indianapolis metropolitan area and other MS4 communities, local jurisdictions (cities, counties) may have additional requirements layered on top of Rule 5. These may include plan review fees, local inspection programs, and additional BMP standards. Always check the local MS4 stormwater ordinance.
