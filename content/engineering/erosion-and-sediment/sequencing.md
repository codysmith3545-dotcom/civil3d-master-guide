> **TL;DR**
> 1. Erosion controls must be installed **before** land disturbance begins — perimeter controls first, then proceed with clearing and grading.
> 2. Areas not actively being worked must be temporarily stabilized within **14 days** of reaching final grade (Indiana Rule 5). The EPA CGP also requires stabilization within 14 days.
> 3. Construction phasing limits the area of exposed soil at any one time, reducing both erosion risk and BMP maintenance burden.

## Typical construction sequence

The ESCP plan sheet should include a numbered construction sequence. A standard sequence for a site-development project:

### Phase 1 — Initial controls

1. Hold a pre-construction meeting with the contractor, owner, and inspector. Review the SWPPP.
2. Install perimeter erosion controls: silt fence around the downslope boundary, sediment traps/basins at concentrated discharge points.
3. Install the stabilized construction entrance at every site access point.
4. Install inlet protection on all existing storm inlets within or adjacent to the disturbance limits.
5. Mark clearing limits and tree-protection fencing.

### Phase 2 — Clearing and rough grading

6. Clear and grub within the approved limits of disturbance only.
7. Begin rough grading. Install check dams in temporary ditches and swales.
8. Construct sediment basins (if not already built in Phase 1) before upstream areas are graded.
9. Install diversion berms to direct runoff toward sediment-trapping BMPs.

### Phase 3 — Utility and structure construction

10. Install underground utilities (storm, sanitary, water).
11. Construct building pads, retaining walls, and other structures.
12. Maintain all BMPs — repair silt fence, clean inlet protection, remove accumulated sediment from basins.
13. Temporarily stabilize (seed + mulch or erosion blanket) any areas at final grade that will not be actively worked for 14+ days.

### Phase 4 — Fine grading and paving

14. Fine-grade roadways, parking lots, and building pads.
15. Install curb, gutter, and storm sewer.
16. Pave roadways and parking areas.
17. Transition inlet protection from construction-type to post-construction type as needed.

### Phase 5 — Final stabilization

18. Place topsoil and fine-grade all unpaved areas.
19. Seed or sod all disturbed areas.
20. Install permanent erosion protection (riprap, permanent channels, outlet protection).
21. Remove temporary BMPs only after upstream areas achieve 70% vegetative cover.
22. File the Notice of Termination (NOT) with IDEM once final stabilization is achieved site-wide.

## Phasing to limit exposed area

For large projects (subdivisions, commercial campuses, linear projects), limit the area of concurrent disturbance:

- Phase clearing by lot block or street segment.
- Stabilize completed phases before starting new ones.
- Some jurisdictions cap concurrent disturbance (e.g., 20-30 acres at a time for large developments).

Phasing reduces the total sediment load leaving the site and keeps BMP sizes manageable.

## Seasonal considerations

In Indiana, the primary seeding season runs from March 15 through June 15 and August 15 through October 15. Outside these windows:

- Dormant seeding (November through February) may be acceptable but must be mulched or blanketed.
- During the non-growing season, temporary stabilization with mulch alone (at 2 tons/acre) or erosion blanket may be used until seeding can occur.
- Winter construction requires additional attention to frozen ground, snowmelt erosion, and dewatering.
