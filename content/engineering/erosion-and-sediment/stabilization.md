> **TL;DR**
> 1. **Temporary stabilization**: seed + mulch or erosion blanket within **14 days** of reaching final grade on areas not being actively worked (Indiana Rule 5 and EPA CGP).
> 2. **Final stabilization**: **70% vegetative cover** over all disturbed areas, or equivalent permanent stabilization (riprap, pavement, buildings, sod). This is the trigger for filing the NOT and closing the permit.
> 3. In Indiana, the primary seeding seasons are **March 15 - June 15** and **August 15 - October 15**. Outside these windows, use dormant seeding with mulch/blanket or alternative stabilization.

## Temporary stabilization

Temporary stabilization prevents erosion on disturbed areas during construction. Required when:

- An area has reached interim or final grade and will not be actively worked for 14 or more days.
- The EPA CGP and Indiana Rule 5 both use the 14-day threshold.

### Methods

| Method | Description | Slope limit |
|---|---|---|
| Seed + straw mulch | Broadcast seed with straw mulch at 2 tons/acre | Up to 3:1 |
| Seed + crimped straw | Straw mulch anchored by crimping or tackifier | Up to 3:1 |
| Seed + erosion blanket | Rolled erosion control product (straw, coir, excelsior) | Up to 2:1 |
| Mulch only (no seed) | Straw mulch at 2 tons/acre, anchored | Off-season when seed won't germinate |
| Sod | Immediate coverage | Critical areas, channels, steep slopes |
| Gravel/stone | Temporary stone cover | Staging areas, construction roads |

### Temporary seed mixes (Indiana)

INDOT temporary seed mix (for quick cover):

- Annual ryegrass: 40 lb/acre (spring/summer)
- Cereal rye: 100 lb/acre (fall/winter dormant seeding)
- Oats: 64 lb/acre (spring/summer alternative)

These are nurse crops that establish quickly but are not permanent. Permanent seed must be applied before final stabilization.

## Final stabilization

Final stabilization is the condition under which the construction stormwater permit can be terminated. IDEM Rule 5 defines final stabilization as:

- **Vegetative cover**: 70% of the pre-disturbance vegetative cover density on all disturbed areas, established with permanent species.
- **Equivalent stabilization**: areas covered by permanent hard surfaces (pavement, buildings, concrete) or permanent erosion protection (riprap, gabions, geosynthetic armoring).
- **Individual residential lots**: final stabilization on individual lots that have been transferred to homeowners requires either sod or seed with temporary stabilization measures in place.

## Permanent seed mixes (Indiana)

INDOT publishes permanent seed mixes in the Standard Specifications. A common mix for upland areas:

| Species | Rate (PLS lb/acre) |
|---|---|
| Tall fescue | 40-60 |
| Kentucky bluegrass | 10-20 |
| Perennial ryegrass | 10-20 |
| White clover | 2-4 |

For wet areas, drainage channels, and bioswales, use appropriate wetland or riparian seed mixes. Native-species mixes are increasingly required by local jurisdictions for stormwater quality BMPs (rain gardens, bioretention).

## Seeding seasons

| Season | Dates (Indiana) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Spring | March 15 - June 15 | Best for warm-season and cool-season grasses |
| Fall | August 15 - October 15 | Best for cool-season grasses (fescue, bluegrass) |
| Dormant | November 1 - March 1 | Seed placed on prepared soil; germinates in spring. Must be mulched or blanketed. |
| Summer (not recommended) | June 16 - August 14 | Heat stress and drought make establishment difficult. Irrigate if used. |

## Slopes and channels

- Slopes steeper than 3:1 should receive erosion blanket in addition to seed and mulch.
- Slopes steeper than 2:1 should receive turf reinforcement mat (TRM) or engineered stabilization.
- Drainage channels with design velocities above 4 ft/s should receive permanent armoring (riprap, TRM, or concrete) rather than relying on vegetation alone.

## Alternative permanent stabilization

Not all areas need vegetation. Acceptable permanent stabilization includes:

- **Riprap** — at pipe outfalls, channel transitions, and steep slopes.
- **Pavement and buildings** — impervious surfaces count as final stabilization.
- **Sod** — provides immediate stabilization; required in some critical areas.
- **Aggregate/gravel** — for maintenance access roads and utility corridors.
