> **TL;DR**
> 1. Inspections are required **at least every 7 calendar days** and **within 24 hours after each rainfall event of 0.5 inches or more**.
> 2. The inspection report must document every BMP on the site: is it in place, functioning, and maintained? Note any damage, sediment accumulation, or off-site discharges.
> 3. Corrective actions for failed BMPs must begin **within 24 hours** of discovery and be completed as soon as practicable. Document everything in the SWPPP binder.

## Inspection frequency

| Trigger | Inspection deadline |
|---|---|
| Routine | At least once every 7 calendar days |
| Rainfall event >= 0.5 inches | Within 24 hours of the end of the event |
| Both triggers in same week | Both inspections must occur (the rain-event inspection does not replace the weekly inspection unless it falls on the same day) |

Some local MS4 programs require more frequent inspections (e.g., every 5 days, or after every 0.25-inch event). Check the local ordinance.

## Who can inspect

Indiana Rule 5 requires inspections by a "qualified person." This does not require a licensed professional engineer or surveyor. A qualified person is someone who has received training in erosion and sediment control and understands the SWPPP for the project. Many Indiana MS4 communities accept:

- CISEC (Certified Inspector of Sediment and Erosion Control) certification.
- CPESC (Certified Professional in Erosion and Sediment Control).
- CPSWQ (Certified Professional in Storm Water Quality).
- Local training programs offered by Soil and Water Conservation Districts.

The inspector can be the contractor's employee, a third-party consultant, or the owner's representative.

## Inspection report contents

Each inspection report must include:

1. **Date and time** of the inspection.
2. **Inspector name** and qualifications.
3. **Weather conditions** — precipitation since last inspection, current conditions.
4. **BMP inventory** — status of each BMP on the site:
   - Is it in place and functioning?
   - Is sediment accumulation below the maintenance threshold (e.g., 1/3 of silt fence height)?
   - Has the BMP been damaged, undermined, or flanked?
5. **Discharge points** — visual assessment of any discharge leaving the site. Note turbidity, color, odor, or visible sediment.
6. **Areas needing stabilization** — identify areas at final grade that have not been stabilized within the required timeframe.
7. **Corrective actions needed** — describe the deficiency and the planned repair.
8. **Corrective actions completed** — document repairs made since the last inspection.
9. **Signature** of the inspector.

## Corrective action timeline

- Begin corrective action within **24 hours** of identifying the deficiency.
- Complete the repair as soon as practicable — for simple repairs (re-staking silt fence, adding stone to inlet protection), the same day is expected.
- If the deficiency resulted in off-site sediment discharge, report to IDEM and the local MS4 authority as required by the permit.
- Document the corrective action in the SWPPP binder with dates and photos.

## Common inspection findings

| Finding | Corrective action |
|---|---|
| Silt fence down or undercut | Re-install: re-trench, re-stake, replace fabric if torn |
| Sediment > 1/3 fence height | Remove accumulated sediment, dispose properly |
| Inlet protection displaced or clogged | Clean or replace; ensure drainage is maintained |
| Construction entrance deteriorated | Add stone, re-grade; sweep public road if mud tracking |
| Unstabilized areas past 14 days | Seed and mulch/blanket immediately |
| Sediment basin full | Dewater cleanly, remove accumulated sediment |
| Off-site sediment discharge | Report to IDEM, implement additional BMPs, clean up |

## Record keeping

All inspection reports, rain logs, and corrective action records must be:

- Kept in the SWPPP binder on site (or readily available electronically).
- Retained for at least 3 years after the NOT is filed.
- Available for review by IDEM, the local MS4, or EPA at any time during construction.
