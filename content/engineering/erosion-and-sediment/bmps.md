> **TL;DR**
> 1. **Silt fence** is the most common perimeter BMP — it intercepts sheet flow only (not concentrated flow). Install with the fabric trenched 6-8 inches deep on the downslope side of disturbed areas. Maximum drainage area: 0.25 acres per 100 ft of fence.
> 2. **Sediment basins** are required when 10 or more acres drain to a single discharge point (EPA CGP). Size at 3,600 cubic feet per acre of disturbed drainage area.
> 3. **Construction entrances** must be at least 50 ft long, 20 ft wide, and 6 inches deep with clean stone to prevent mud tracking onto public roads.

## Silt fence

Silt fence is a geotextile fabric supported by posts, installed along the contour to intercept sheet flow and trap sediment.

**When to use:** Perimeter of disturbed areas where runoff leaves the site as sheet flow. Not effective for concentrated flow, channel flow, or slopes steeper than 2:1.

**Installation:**
- Trench the bottom of the fabric 6-8 inches into the ground and backfill with compacted soil.
- Posts spaced 6-8 ft apart (closer on steeper slopes), driven 18-24 inches deep.
- Maximum slope length above the fence: 100 ft.
- Maximum drainage area: 0.25 acres per 100 linear ft.
- Do not place in streams or concentrated-flow channels.

**Maintenance:** Inspect after every rain event. Remove accumulated sediment when it reaches one-third of the fence height. Repair any undermining, tears, or leaning posts immediately.

## Inlet protection

Protects storm sewer inlets from sediment during construction.

| Type | Description | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Type A | Fabric barrier around the inlet with wire mesh support | Paved areas with low flow |
| Type B | Stone (INDOT #2 or #5) placed around the inlet | Unpaved areas, higher flow |
| Type C | Stone bag barrier or filter fabric dam | Curb inlets on grades |
| Manufactured | Drop-in filters, foam inserts | Quick deployment, paved areas |

Inlet protection must be maintained throughout construction and removed only after the upstream area is fully stabilized.

## Construction entrance

A stabilized pad of clean stone at every point where construction traffic exits the site onto a public road.

**Minimum dimensions (INDOT):**
- Length: 50 ft minimum (100 ft on state routes)
- Width: 20 ft minimum (full width of the access drive)
- Stone depth: 6 inches
- Stone size: INDOT #2 or #53

**Additional measures:** Wheel wash stations may be required on large or sensitive sites. Street sweeping of the adjacent public road may be necessary.

## Sediment basin

A temporary impoundment that detains sediment-laden runoff and allows sediment to settle.

**When required:** EPA CGP requires a sediment basin when 10 or more acres drain to a single discharge point and it is feasible to install one. Indiana Rule 5 follows the same threshold.

**Sizing:** 3,600 cubic feet per acre of drainage area (equivalent to approximately 1 inch of runoff from the disturbed area). The basin should have a minimum 2:1 length-to-width ratio and a stable outlet (principal spillway pipe with a trash rack and an emergency spillway for the 25-year storm).

**Dewatering device:** A skimmer or perforated riser that draws water from the surface (where it is cleanest) rather than from the bottom.

## Erosion control blankets

Temporary rolled erosion control products (RECPs) that protect bare soil from raindrop impact and overland flow erosion until vegetation is established.

| Type | Material | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Straw blanket | Straw fibers in degradable netting | Slopes up to 3:1, channels up to 5 ft/s |
| Coconut (coir) blanket | Coconut fiber | Slopes up to 2:1, channels up to 8 ft/s |
| Excelsior blanket | Curled wood fibers | Slopes up to 2:1 |
| Turf reinforcement mat (TRM) | Permanent synthetic matrix | Steep slopes, high-velocity channels |

Install blankets by rolling down the slope (not across), stapling at 3-ft intervals, and overlapping seams by 4-6 inches.

## Check dams

Small barriers placed across drainage ditches and swales to slow flow velocity and trap sediment.

**Materials:** Rock (INDOT #1 or #2 stone), straw bales (less effective, not recommended by many agencies), or manufactured devices.

**Spacing:** The crest of the downstream check dam should be at the toe elevation of the upstream dam (approximately 2-4 ft of drop between dams, depending on channel slope).

**Maximum height:** 2-3 ft.

## Diversion berms and swales

Temporary earthen berms or swales that redirect runoff away from disturbed areas or toward sediment-trapping BMPs. Typically 18-24 inches high with a 2% minimum grade to a stable outlet.
