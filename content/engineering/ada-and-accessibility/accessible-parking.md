> **TL;DR**
> 1. Required number of accessible spaces is based on total parking count (ADA Table 208.2): 1 accessible space for 1-25 total, 2 for 26-50, scaling up. At least **1 in every 6** accessible spaces must be van-accessible.
> 2. Access aisle minimum width is **60 inches** (standard) or **96 inches** (van-accessible). Slope in the space and aisle must not exceed **2.0% in any direction**.
> 3. Signage: international symbol of accessibility, mounted **60 inches minimum** to the bottom of the sign, "Van Accessible" designation on van spaces. Indiana law (IC 5-16-9) adds specific state signage and penalty requirements.

## Required number of accessible spaces

ADA Table 208.2:

| Total parking spaces | Minimum accessible spaces |
|---|---|
| 1 to 25 | 1 |
| 26 to 50 | 2 |
| 51 to 75 | 3 |
| 76 to 100 | 4 |
| 101 to 150 | 5 |
| 151 to 200 | 6 |
| 201 to 300 | 7 |
| 301 to 400 | 8 |
| 401 to 500 | 9 |
| 501 to 1000 | 2% of total |
| 1001 and over | 20, plus 1 for each 100 over 1000 |

### Van-accessible requirement

At least 1 in every 6 accessible spaces (or fraction thereof) must be van-accessible. A parking lot with 3 accessible spaces needs at least 1 van-accessible space.

## Space and aisle dimensions

### Standard accessible space

- Width: 96 in. (8 ft) minimum.
- Access aisle: 60 in. (5 ft) minimum, adjacent to the space.
- The access aisle may be shared between two adjacent accessible spaces (one aisle serves the space on each side).

### Van-accessible space

Two options:

1. **Wide space:** 132 in. (11 ft) space + 60 in. (5 ft) access aisle.
2. **Standard space with wide aisle:** 96 in. (8 ft) space + 96 in. (8 ft) access aisle.

The wide aisle accommodates side-mounted wheelchair lifts on vans.

### Slope

The parking space and access aisle surface must not exceed **2.0% slope in any direction**. This is one of the strictest slope requirements in the ADA standards. It means the accessible spaces should be on the flattest part of the lot. A parking lot with a 3% drain slope across the driving lanes will need a level pad at the accessible spaces.

### Vertical clearance

Van-accessible spaces and their access aisles must have a minimum vertical clearance of **98 inches** (8 ft-2 in.) along the vehicular route to the space. This prevents high-roof vans with roof-mounted lifts from being excluded.

## Location

- Accessible spaces must be on the **shortest accessible route** to the accessible building entrance they serve.
- Where a site has multiple buildings or multiple entrances, accessible spaces should be dispersed to serve each accessible entrance.
- In parking structures, accessible spaces should be on the level closest to the accessible entrance.

## Signage

### ADA requirements

- Each accessible space must be identified by a sign showing the international symbol of accessibility.
- Van-accessible spaces must have an additional "Van Accessible" designation.
- Signs must be mounted so that the **bottom of the sign is at least 60 inches above the parking surface**. This ensures visibility when a vehicle is parked in the space.

### Indiana requirements (IC 5-16-9)

Indiana law adds:

- Signs must include the penalty for unauthorized use (fine amount per current Indiana statute).
- Pavement markings (the international symbol painted on the space surface) are required in addition to the post-mounted sign in most municipal standards.
- Indiana law enforcement can cite vehicles without a disability plate or placard parked in accessible spaces.

## Access aisle markings

The access aisle should be marked with diagonal stripes or cross-hatching to discourage parking within the aisle. Striping color varies — blue or white are most common. The aisle must be at grade with the parking space (no curb between the space and the aisle).

The access aisle must connect to the accessible route. This connection point is where many designs fail — the aisle ends at a curb with no ramp or flush transition.
