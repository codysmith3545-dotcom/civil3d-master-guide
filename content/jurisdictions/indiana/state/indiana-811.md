> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana law (**IC 8-1-26**) requires anyone who excavates or demolishes to notify Indiana 811 at least **2 full working days** (not counting the day of the call, weekends, or legal holidays) before digging.
> 2. Tickets are filed online at [indiana811.org](https://indiana811.org/) or by calling **811**. Design tickets (for pre-design utility mapping) and construction tickets (for active excavation) are separate request types.
> 3. The excavator must maintain the marks, hand-dig or use vacuum excavation within **24 inches (610 mm)** of a marked facility (the tolerance zone), and re-notify if work extends beyond the ticket's valid period (**20 calendar days**).

## Statutory framework

IC 8-1-26, the Underground Plant Protection Act, governs the relationship between excavators and underground-facility operators. Key provisions:

- **IC 8-1-26-14** — Excavator must provide notice to the association (Indiana 811) at least 2 full working days before beginning excavation.
- **IC 8-1-26-15** — Operator must mark facilities within 2 full working days of receiving the notice, using APWA uniform color code.
- **IC 8-1-26-18** — Excavator must use reasonable care within the tolerance zone (24 inches (610 mm) measured horizontally from the outer edge of the underground facility).
- **IC 8-1-26-19** — Liability provisions for damage caused by failure to comply.
- **IC 8-1-26-23** — Emergency excavation: notice is still required, but work may begin immediately to address the emergency, and the excavator must call as soon as practicable.

## Filing a locate ticket

### Construction tickets

Used when excavation will occur. Filed at [indiana811.org](https://indiana811.org/) or by calling 811.

1. Provide the **project location** (address, intersection, or GPS coordinates), a description of the work, and the planned excavation dates.
2. **White-line the dig area** on the ground before the locate crew arrives. This is required by Indiana 811 best practice and avoids marking entire properties.
3. Wait at least **2 full working days** (the "start date" on the ticket) before digging.
4. Ticket is valid for **20 calendar days** from the start date. If work continues beyond 20 days, file a renewal or new ticket.
5. If a utility operator fails to mark or reports "no conflict" and a facility is struck, liability shifts per IC 8-1-26-19.

### Design tickets

Used for pre-design utility investigation (e.g., SUE quality level B or A, topographic survey). Design tickets inform utilities that a surveyor or engineer needs facility locations for planning purposes; they are not authorization to excavate. Operators may respond with as-built records, GIS data, or field locates depending on the utility's practices.

File design tickets well in advance of the design deadline — utility response time on design tickets is not statutorily guaranteed and may be slower than for construction tickets.

## APWA uniform color code

Utility markings follow the APWA (American Public Works Association) color code:

| Color | Facility |
|---|---|
| Red | Electric power, conduit, cables |
| Yellow | Gas, oil, steam, petroleum |
| Orange | Communications, alarm, signal, fiber |
| Blue | Potable water |
| Green | Sewer, storm drain |
| Purple | Reclaimed water, irrigation |
| Pink | Temporary survey markings |
| White | Proposed excavation (placed by the excavator) |

## Excavator responsibilities

- **Maintain marks.** If marks are destroyed by weather or traffic before excavation is complete, request a re-mark.
- **Tolerance zone.** Hand-dig or use vacuum excavation within 24 inches (610 mm) of a marked facility. Mechanical excavation is not permitted within this zone.
- **Exposed facilities.** Support and protect exposed facilities for the duration of the excavation. Backfill carefully.
- **Damage reporting.** If an underground facility is damaged, notify the facility operator and Indiana 811 immediately, regardless of whether the damage appears to be serious.
- **Emergency.** A damaged gas line or other life-safety situation requires calling 911 first, then 811.

## Utility operator responsibilities

- **Mark within 2 full working days** of receiving the notice, or communicate a "no conflict" response.
- **Mark accurately.** Marks must be within 24 inches (610 mm) of the actual facility location (horizontal).
- **Maintain records.** Operators must maintain as-built records of their facilities and provide them when requested for design purposes.
