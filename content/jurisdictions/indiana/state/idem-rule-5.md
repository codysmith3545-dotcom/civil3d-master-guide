> **TL;DR**
> 1. **327 IAC 15-5 (Rule 5)** requires a Notice of Intent (NOI) and a Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) for any construction activity disturbing **1 acre or more** of land (or less than 1 acre if part of a common plan of development that exceeds 1 acre).
> 2. The NOI must be filed with IDEM **at least 15 days** before land disturbance begins; coverage under the Construction Stormwater General Permit (CSGP) lasts until a Notice of Termination (NOT) is filed after final stabilization.
> 3. The SWPPP must be on-site, updated as conditions change, and inspected at a minimum of **once per 7 calendar days** and **within 24 hours after a rainfall event of 0.5 inches or more** in 24 hours.

## When Rule 5 applies

Rule 5 applies when construction activity will disturb 1 acre or more of land. "Construction activity" includes clearing, grading, excavating, stockpiling, and demolition. Even if a project is smaller than 1 acre, Rule 5 applies if the project is part of a larger common plan of development or sale that will collectively disturb 1 acre or more.

Projects that only pave an existing gravel surface with no grading, or that are routine maintenance of existing infrastructure, are typically exempt, but confirm with IDEM if there is any ambiguity.

## NOI filing process

1. **Prepare the SWPPP** before filing the NOI. The SWPPP must be substantially complete at the time the NOI is submitted.
2. **File the NOI** through IDEM's online portal (e-NOI system). The filing fee is set by IDEM and updated periodically — verify the current amount at the source link above.
3. **Wait 15 days** after IDEM receives the complete NOI before beginning land disturbance. IDEM may issue the permit before the 15-day window closes.
4. **Post the NOI permit number** on site in a visible location.

If ownership or the responsible party changes during the project, a transfer or new NOI is required.

## SWPPP requirements

The SWPPP is the operational document that governs erosion and sediment control for the life of the project. It must include:

- **Site description.** Location, acreage of disturbance, nature of construction activity, receiving waters.
- **Erosion-control plan.** Measures to prevent detachment: phased clearing, temporary seeding, mulching, erosion-control blankets, diversion swales.
- **Sediment-control plan.** Measures to trap sediment before it leaves the site: silt fence, sediment basins (required when 10 acres or more drain to a common point), inlet protection, check dams.
- **Post-construction stormwater controls.** Permanent BMPs that will function after stabilization (detention, retention, bioswales, permeable pavement) as required by the local MS4 or project design.
- **Pollution-prevention measures.** Controls for concrete washout, fuel storage, hazardous materials, dewatering discharge.
- **Inspection and maintenance schedule.** See below.
- **Site map.** Showing limits of disturbance, BMP locations, drainage patterns, receiving waters, and buffer zones.

The SWPPP must be kept on site or be accessible within a reasonable time and updated whenever there is a change in design, construction, operation, or maintenance that materially affects stormwater discharge.

## Inspection frequency

- **Routine inspections:** at least once every 7 calendar days.
- **Post-storm inspections:** within 24 hours after any rainfall event producing 0.5 inches (13 mm) or more in 24 hours.
- **Inspector qualifications:** Indiana does not currently require a specific certification, but IDEM expects inspectors to be knowledgeable in SWPPP implementation. Many MS4 communities within the 8-county area require the inspector to hold a certification (e.g., CESSWI or similar).

Document each inspection in writing. The inspection report must note the condition of every BMP, identify deficiencies, and record corrective actions and deadlines.

## Notice of Termination (NOT)

The project sponsor files a NOT when:

1. All construction activity is complete.
2. **Final stabilization** has been achieved — meaning all disturbed areas have either a uniform perennial vegetative cover with a density of at least 70 percent of pre-disturbance levels, or equivalent permanent stabilization (riprap, pavement, structures).
3. All temporary BMPs have been removed (or, if converted to permanent, are functioning as designed).

Until the NOT is filed and accepted by IDEM, the permit holder remains responsible for SWPPP compliance, inspection, and maintenance.

## Interaction with local MS4 programs

In MS4 communities (most of the urbanized areas in the 8-county area — see [Rule 13](idem-rule-13.md)), the local MS4 operator may impose additional requirements beyond Rule 5:

- Stricter erosion-control specifications (e.g., minimum lengths of silt fence, basin sizing criteria).
- Plan-review fees and erosion-control plan approval before the NOI is filed.
- Local inspection schedules that are more frequent than state minimums.
- Financial surety (bond or letter of credit) for BMP performance.

Always check with the municipality or county before filing the NOI.

## Common mistakes

- Filing the NOI but not having a site-specific SWPPP ready before disturbance begins.
- Using the same generic SWPPP across multiple projects without updating site-specific details.
- Failing to update the SWPPP when site conditions change (e.g., adding a phase, relocating a basin).
- Letting the NOT lapse — the permit holder remains liable for SWPPP compliance until IDEM accepts the NOT.
- Assuming that a project under 1 acre is exempt without confirming it is not part of a larger common plan.
