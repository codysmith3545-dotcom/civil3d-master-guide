> **TL;DR**
> 1. The Indiana Design Manual (IDM) is INDOT's controlling document for roadway, bridge, hydraulic, traffic, and survey design on state-maintained highways. It supersedes or modifies the AASHTO Green Book and other national standards where Indiana-specific policy applies.
> 2. Key chapters for civil/survey work: **Ch. 29** (small structures), **Ch. 30** (large structures), **Ch. 36** (survey), **Ch. 40** (traffic), **Ch. 203** (hydrology and hydraulics).
> 3. The IDM is published online at no cost and updated periodically; always confirm you are reading the current version at the link above.

## What the IDM is

The Indiana Design Manual is a multi-part reference that INDOT uses to govern the design of state highway projects. Consultants performing INDOT-let or locally-administered federal-aid work are bound by it. Municipalities and counties may adopt it by reference in their own ordinances, but it is not automatically controlling on non-INDOT projects unless the local authority says so.

The IDM adopts national standards (AASHTO Green Book, Roadside Design Guide, MUTCD, LRFD, MASH) and then modifies or tightens them for Indiana conditions. When INDOT guidance and a national standard conflict, the IDM governs for INDOT projects.

## Chapter-by-chapter summary

The following is a summary of major parts and chapters. Chapter numbers follow the IDM's own numbering as of 2026. Verify against the current online edition — INDOT may reorganize chapters between updates.

### Part 1 — General information

- **Ch. 1 — INDOT project development.** Project phases, letting process, consultant agreements.
- **Ch. 2 — Design criteria.** Design speed selection, functional classification, level of service, design-year traffic.

### Part 2 — Roadway design

- **Ch. 40 — Traffic design.** Signal warrants, turn-lane warrants, channelization, roundabout design, signing, pavement marking, traffic studies, and intersection sight-distance requirements.
- **Ch. 44 — Roadway design elements.** Cross sections, lane and shoulder width, superelevation, vertical and horizontal alignment, grades, and design exceptions.
- **Ch. 45 — Clear zone and roadside safety.** Clear-zone widths (ties to the AASHTO Roadside Design Guide), barrier warrants, end treatments, and MASH compliance.
- **Ch. 46 — Intersection and interchange design.** Geometric layout, acceleration and deceleration lanes, ramp design.
- **Ch. 49 — Pedestrian and bicycle facilities.** Sidewalks, shared-use paths, ADA-compliant curb ramps.

### Part 3 — Drainage and hydraulics

- **Ch. 200 — Drainage policy.** General drainage requirements, design-storm frequencies, agency responsibilities.
- **Ch. 201 — Hydrology.** Rational method, unit-hydrograph methods, TR-55, regression equations for Indiana streams.
- **Ch. 203 — Hydrology and hydraulics (applied).** Bridge waterway adequacy, scour, backwater, floodplain encroachment analysis (ties to FEMA/IDNR). This chapter is the primary reference for hydraulic computations on INDOT projects.
- **Ch. 204 — Storm drainage systems.** Inlet spacing, pipe sizing (Manning's equation), junction losses, energy-grade-line computations.
- **Ch. 205 — Erosion control.** Temporary and permanent erosion-control measures, coordination with IDEM [Rule 5](idem-rule-5.md).

### Part 4 — Structures

- **Ch. 29 — Small structures.** Pipes, box culverts, and minor drainage structures. Covers hydraulic design, structural design, end treatments, and standard sizes.
- **Ch. 30 — Large structures.** Bridges and major culverts. LRFD-based design, load ratings, scour countermeasures. References the AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications with Indiana modifications.

### Part 5 — Survey

- **Ch. 36 — Survey.** Coordinate systems (State Plane Indiana East/West, NAD83(2011)), control-survey accuracy requirements, GPS/GNSS procedures, level-loop tolerances, right-of-way survey standards, PLSS corner perpetuation. This chapter is essential for surveyors doing INDOT-funded work.

### Part 6 — Right-of-way and utilities

- **Ch. 53 — Right-of-way acquisition.** Appraisal, negotiation, condemnation, relocation assistance.
- **Ch. 54 — Utility accommodation.** Longitudinal and transverse utility encroachment in INDOT ROW, relocation procedures, utility agreements.

### Part 7 — Traffic operations

- **Ch. 59 — Work-zone traffic control.** Temporary traffic control plans, lane-closure procedures, flagging, MUTCD Part 6 compliance.

## How to cite

Reference by chapter and section number, for example: "IDM Ch. 203, §203-3.02(02)" for a specific hydraulic calculation procedure. Include the access date because the manual is updated in place.
