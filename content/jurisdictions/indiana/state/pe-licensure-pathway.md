> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana requires a **Professional Engineer (PE)** license to offer or perform engineering services to the public. The pathway is: ABET-accredited engineering degree, **FE exam**, **4 years of progressive experience** under a licensed PE, **PE exam**, and the **Indiana law and ethics component**.
> 2. Continuing education: **30 professional development hours (PDH)** per 2-year renewal cycle, including at least 1 hour in Indiana engineering law and rules.
> 3. Reciprocity (comity) is available for PEs licensed in other states who meet Indiana's requirements.

## Education requirements

- A 4-year degree from an ABET-accredited engineering program is the standard path.
- Graduates of ABET-accredited engineering technology programs may qualify with additional experience (typically 6 years total instead of 4); confirm with the Board.
- Non-ABET graduates may petition for equivalency evaluation.

## Examination sequence

### 1. Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam

- Administered by NCEES.
- A computer-based, multiple-choice exam covering mathematics, sciences, engineering fundamentals, and ethics.
- Typically taken during the final year of the undergraduate program.
- Passing the FE exam qualifies the candidate as an **Engineer Intern (EI)** in Indiana.

### 2. Experience requirement

- **4 years of progressive engineering experience** under the supervision of a licensed PE.
- Experience must demonstrate increasing responsibility in engineering analysis, design, and decision-making.
- The supervising PE must verify the experience on the application.
- Graduate education may be credited toward up to 1 year of experience, subject to Board approval.

### 3. PE exam

- Administered by NCEES.
- For civil engineering, the exam is the **PE Civil** exam, offered in the following depth areas: construction, geotechnical, structural, transportation, and water resources and environmental.
- The exam is computer-based.

### 4. Indiana law and ethics

- Indiana requires knowledge of Indiana engineering statutes (IC 25-31.5) and professional conduct rules.
- This is evaluated as part of the application process; the Board may require completion of a law/ethics course or examination component.

## Reciprocity (comity)

A PE licensed in another state may apply for Indiana licensure by comity if:

- The applicant holds an active, unrestricted license in good standing in the other state.
- The applicant's education and experience meet or exceed Indiana's requirements.
- The applicant has passed the NCEES FE and PE exams (or equivalent).

Indiana participates in the NCEES Model Law Engineer framework. Applicants who hold the NCEES Model Law Engineer designation typically receive expedited processing.

## Continuing education

- **30 PDH per 2-year renewal cycle.**
- At least **1 PDH** must cover Indiana engineering law and rules.
- Acceptable activities: seminars, workshops, college courses, published papers, patents, active participation in professional engineering organizations.
- 1 college semester credit hour = 45 PDH; 1 college quarter credit hour = 30 PDH.
- Renewal is biennial; the Board notifies licensees of the renewal deadline.

## Scope of practice notes

- Indiana does not have a statutory "industrial exemption" as broad as some states; work offered to the public or affecting public safety requires a PE.
- Plans, specifications, and reports prepared by a PE must bear the PE's seal, signature, and date.
- A PE license does not authorize the practice of land surveying (and vice versa). Boundary surveys, legal descriptions, and the setting of monuments for property corners require a Professional Surveyor license. See [surveyor licensure pathway](surveyor-licensure-pathway.md).
