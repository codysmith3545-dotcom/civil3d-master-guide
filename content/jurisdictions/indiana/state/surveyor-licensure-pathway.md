> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana requires a **Professional Surveyor (PS)** license to practice land surveying. The pathway is: ABET-accredited degree (or equivalent), **FS exam**, **4 years of progressive experience** under a licensed PS, **PS exam**, and the **Indiana State-Specific Exam (SSE)**.
> 2. Continuing education: **24 hours per 2-year renewal cycle**, including at least 2 hours in Indiana standards of practice (865 IAC 1-12).
> 3. Reciprocity (comity) is available for surveyors licensed in other states who meet Indiana's education and experience requirements and pass the Indiana SSE.

## Education requirements

Indiana requires a degree from an ABET-accredited program in surveying, geomatics, or a closely related field. Acceptable degree paths include:

- A 4-year ABET-accredited surveying/geomatics degree (the most straightforward path).
- A 4-year ABET-accredited engineering degree combined with additional surveying coursework meeting Indiana Board requirements.
- For candidates who began practice before current education requirements took effect, the Board may evaluate combinations of education and extended experience; consult the PLA for current equivalency rules.

The Board reviews transcripts and may require additional coursework if the applicant's program does not cover boundary law, public land survey system, geodesy, and legal descriptions.

## Examination sequence

### 1. Fundamentals of Surveying (FS) exam

- Administered by NCEES.
- A multiple-choice exam covering mathematics, measurement, geodesy, mapping, legal principles, and professional practice.
- Typically taken during the final year of the degree program or shortly after graduation.
- Passing the FS exam qualifies the candidate as a **Surveyor Intern (SI)** in Indiana.

### 2. Experience requirement

- **4 years of progressive experience** in land surveying under the direct supervision of a licensed Professional Surveyor.
- Experience must include boundary surveys, legal description preparation, and monumentation — not solely construction staking or mapping.
- The supervising PS must certify the experience on the application.

### 3. Principles and Practice of Surveying (PS) exam

- Administered by NCEES.
- A 6-hour exam (morning and afternoon sessions) covering boundary law, boundary analysis, public land survey system, geodetic surveying, legal descriptions, and professional practice.

### 4. Indiana State-Specific Exam (SSE)

- Administered by the Indiana Board of Registration for Professional Surveyors.
- Covers Indiana-specific statutes, rules, and practice requirements: IC 36-2-12 (county surveyor), IC 25-21.5 (surveyor licensing statute), 865 IAC 1-12 (standards of practice), Indiana case law affecting boundary, and Indiana State Plane coordinate systems.
- Required for all applicants, including comity (reciprocity) applicants from other states.

## Reciprocity (comity)

A surveyor licensed in another state may apply for an Indiana license by comity if:

- The applicant holds an active license in good standing in the other state.
- The other state's requirements are substantially equivalent to Indiana's (education, experience, and NCEES exams).
- The applicant passes the Indiana SSE.

The Board evaluates each application individually. Applicants from states that do not require a 4-year surveying degree may need to demonstrate equivalent education.

## Continuing education

- **24 hours** of professional development per 2-year renewal cycle.
- At least **2 hours** must cover Indiana standards of practice (865 IAC 1-12) or Indiana surveying law.
- Acceptable activities: seminars, workshops, college courses, published papers, active participation in professional surveying organizations.
- Renewal is biennial; the Board notifies licensees of the renewal deadline.

## Key Indiana practice rules (865 IAC 1-12)

The Indiana Standards of Practice govern the minimum requirements for surveying work performed in the state, including:

- Plat requirements (minimum content, seal and signature, basis of bearings, closure precision).
- Monument requirements (type, material, marking).
- Boundary survey procedures (research, evidence hierarchy, monumentation).
- Minimum accuracy standards by class of survey.
