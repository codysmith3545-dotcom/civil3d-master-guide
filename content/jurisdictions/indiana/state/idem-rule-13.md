> **TL;DR**
> 1. **327 IAC 15-13 (Rule 13)** is Indiana's implementation of the federal NPDES Phase II MS4 stormwater program (40 CFR 122.26). It requires designated small Municipal Separate Storm Sewer System (MS4) operators to obtain a general permit and implement six minimum control measures.
> 2. All 8 counties in this guide (Marion, Hamilton, Hancock, Shelby, Johnson, Morgan, Hendricks, Boone) contain designated MS4 entities — primarily the urbanized municipalities and the counties themselves.
> 3. For site-development work, Rule 13 means the local MS4 reviews and approves stormwater management plans and may impose post-construction standards that exceed state minimums.

## What an MS4 is

A Municipal Separate Storm Sewer System (MS4) is a publicly owned system of conveyances — streets, gutters, ditches, storm sewers, channels — that discharges to waters of the state without passing through a wastewater treatment plant. MS4 status is designated by IDEM based on U.S. Census Bureau urbanized-area boundaries and population thresholds.

MS4 status is not voluntary. When a community is designated, it must apply for a general permit, develop a stormwater quality management plan (SWQMP), and implement the six minimum control measures on an ongoing five-year permit cycle.

## Six minimum control measures

The federal Clean Water Act and Indiana Rule 13 require every MS4 to implement:

1. **Public education and outreach.** Informing the public about the impacts of stormwater discharge and how to reduce pollutant loading.
2. **Public involvement and participation.** Opportunities for the public to participate in the SWQMP development and implementation (public hearings, volunteer monitoring, storm-drain marking).
3. **Illicit discharge detection and elimination (IDDE).** Mapping the storm sewer system, conducting dry-weather screening, tracing and eliminating non-stormwater discharges.
4. **Construction site stormwater runoff control.** Requiring erosion-and-sediment-control plans for construction activities, reviewing plans, inspecting sites, and enforcing compliance. This is where Rule 13 intersects with [Rule 5](idem-rule-5.md) — the MS4 serves as the local enforcement layer.
5. **Post-construction stormwater management.** Requiring permanent stormwater controls on new development and redevelopment. Many MS4s in the 8-county area require detention/retention sizing to control the 1-year through 100-year storms, water-quality BMPs treating the first 1.0 inch (25 mm) of runoff, and maintenance agreements recorded against the property.
6. **Pollution prevention and good housekeeping for municipal operations.** Training municipal staff, maintaining municipal stormwater infrastructure, managing salt storage, and preventing pollution from municipal operations.

## Co-permittee obligations

Where a county and one or more municipalities within it are separately designated MS4 entities, they may operate as **co-permittees** under a single general permit or maintain separate permits. Co-permittees share some administrative functions (mapping, IDDE coordination, public education) while each entity retains its own inspection and enforcement authority.

For a consultant or developer, the practical effect is that a project may trigger review by both the county MS4 and the municipal MS4. Confirm which entity reviews the stormwater management plan and which conducts inspections.

## How Rule 13 affects site plan review

When designing a site in an MS4 community, expect these requirements beyond Rule 5:

- **Pre-development and post-development runoff comparison.** Many MS4 ordinances require that post-development peak discharge does not exceed pre-development for the 1-year, 10-year, and 100-year storms, and some require volume control.
- **Water-quality BMP.** Treat the water-quality volume (commonly defined as the runoff from the first 1.0 inch (25 mm) of rainfall over the new impervious area) before discharge.
- **Stormwater management plan.** A formal plan submitted to the MS4 for review and approval before construction. The plan includes hydrologic/hydraulic calculations, BMP sizing, drainage area maps, and a maintenance plan.
- **Recorded maintenance agreement.** A covenant recorded against the property requiring the owner to maintain post-construction BMPs in perpetuity.
- **As-built certification.** After construction, a licensed engineer or surveyor certifies that BMPs were installed per the approved plan.
