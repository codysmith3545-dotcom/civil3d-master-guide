> **TL;DR**
> 1. Greenwood is the largest city in Johnson County and runs its own engineering, planning, and building review; do not assume Johnson County process inside the corporate limits.
> 2. The Greenwood stormwater technical standards govern drainage design within the city (verify current title and edition in current revision).
> 3. Greenwood operates Greenwood Sanitation for sanitary sewer service; water service in the city is split between Indiana American Water and other providers depending on location (verify in current revision).

## Authorities

- **Department of Engineering** — construction plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.greenwood.in.gov/department/?fDD=11-0
- **Department of Community Development Services / Planning** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA. https://www.greenwood.in.gov/department/?fDD=10-0
- **Building Department** — improvement location, building permits, inspections.
- **Greenwood Sanitation Department** — sanitary sewer service. https://www.greenwood.in.gov/department/?fDD=22-0
- **Indiana American Water** — water in much of the service area. https://www.amwater.com/inaw
- **Greenwood Fire Department** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, and access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Greenwood Code of Ordinances** — hosted on American Legal / Municode (verify current host in current revision).
- **Greenwood Unified Development Ordinance / Zoning and Subdivision Control Ordinance** (verify combined or separate documents in current revision).
- **Greenwood Stormwater Technical Standards** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Greenwood Standard Construction Details / Standard Specifications** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA, sanitary (verify in current revision).
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Community Development and Engineering.
2. TAC review of the concept package.
3. Plan Commission action on primary plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Sanitation, the applicable water provider, and Fire.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the city standards.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and utilities.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Greenwood standards.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Greenwood standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from Greenwood Sanitation and the water provider.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the development ordinance.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- The city sanitation department is its own utility; coordinate sanitary review separately from county-administered systems.
- Water service area is fragmented; confirm provider for the specific parcel before producing plans or will-serve requests.
- Greenwood MS4 program is operated by the city; the Johnson County Surveyor still has jurisdiction over regulated drains that cross the city.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
