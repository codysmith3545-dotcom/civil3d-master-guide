> **TL;DR**
> 1. Zionsville is a reorganized town that absorbed Eagle and Union Townships; town jurisdiction now extends well beyond the historic village, so confirm whether a parcel is inside corporate limits before assuming Boone County process.
> 2. The Zionsville development standards and stormwater technical standards govern design within the corporate limits (verify current titles and editions in current revision).
> 3. Sanitary sewer service is provided by the town (Zionsville Sewer Department) over much of the area; water is provided by Indiana American Water or other providers depending on location (verify provider for the parcel in current revision).

## Authorities

- **Engineering Department / Department of Public Works** — construction plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.zionsville-in.gov/government/departments/public_works/index.php
- **Planning and Economic Development** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA. https://www.zionsville-in.gov/government/departments/planning___economic_development/index.php
- **Building Permits and Inspections** — improvement location and building permits.
- **Zionsville Sewer Department** — sanitary sewer over much of the town. https://www.zionsville-in.gov/government/departments/sewer_utility/index.php
- **Indiana American Water** — water service in much of the area. https://www.amwater.com/inaw
- **Zionsville Fire Department** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Zionsville Code of Ordinances** — hosted on American Legal / Municode (verify current host in current revision).
- **Zionsville Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Control Ordinance** — administered by Planning (verify combined or separate documents in current revision).
- **Zionsville Stormwater Technical Standards** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Zionsville Standard Construction Details / Standard Specifications** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA, sanitary (verify in current revision).
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Planning and Engineering.
2. TAC review of the concept package.
3. Plan Commission action on plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Sewer Department, the water provider, and Fire.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the town standards.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and the utilities.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Zionsville standards.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Zionsville standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from the Sewer Department and the water provider.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the zoning ordinance, with attention to Zionsville's strong rural-character preservation language.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- Reorganization (2010) means much of the geography that older maps label as Eagle or Union Township is inside the town now; verify corporate limits early.
- Historic village area has additional design overlay standards that affect site, signage, and building elevations (verify current overlays in current revision).
- The Boone County Surveyor retains jurisdiction over regulated drains that cross the town.
- Zionsville MS4 program is operated by the town.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
