> **TL;DR**
> 1. Lebanon is the Boone County seat and runs its own engineering, planning, and building review; the LEAP innovation district has driven a rapid expansion of utility and stormwater infrastructure on the city's south side.
> 2. The Lebanon stormwater technical standards govern drainage design within the corporate limits (verify current title and edition in current revision).
> 3. Lebanon Utilities (a separate utility entity owned by the city) provides electric, water, sewer, and broadband service for much of the city.

## Authorities

- **Engineering Department** — construction plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.lebanon.in.gov/engineering
- **Department of Planning and Development** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA. https://www.lebanon.in.gov/planning
- **Building Department** — improvement location, building permits, inspections.
- **Lebanon Utilities** — water and sanitary sewer (and electric/broadband). https://www.lebanonutilities.com/
- **Lebanon Fire Department** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Lebanon Code of Ordinances** — hosted on American Legal / Municode (verify current host in current revision).
- **Lebanon Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Control Ordinance** — administered by Planning (verify combined or separate documents in current revision).
- **Lebanon Stormwater Technical Standards** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Lebanon Standard Construction Details / Standard Specifications** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA, sanitary, water (verify in current revision).
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Planning and Engineering.
2. TAC review of the concept package.
3. Plan Commission or Common Council action on plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Lebanon Utilities, and Fire.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the city standards.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and Lebanon Utilities.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Lebanon standards.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Lebanon standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from Lebanon Utilities for water and sanitary, with capacity confirmation for large industrial users.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the zoning ordinance.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded; large LEAP-area projects routinely require one (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- The LEAP Lebanon Innovation District on the south side of the city has driven new water-supply, sanitary, and stormwater infrastructure; coordinate early with Lebanon Utilities for capacity and service-area questions.
- Lebanon Utilities is a single multi-utility, simplifying coordination compared with cities that split water and sewer between providers.
- The Boone County Surveyor retains jurisdiction over regulated drains that cross the city.
- Lebanon MS4 program is operated by the city.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
