> **TL;DR**
> 1. Fishers became a second-class city in 2015 and runs its own engineering, planning, and building review; do not assume Hamilton County process inside the corporate limits.
> 2. The City of Fishers Stormwater Technical Standards Manual governs drainage design (verify current edition and amendment date in current revision).
> 3. Sanitary sewer service in Fishers is split between Fishers Department of Public Works and Hamilton Southeastern Utilities (HSE Utilities); confirm the service provider for the parcel before producing plans.

## Authorities

- **Department of Engineering** — construction plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.fishers.in.us/156/Engineering
- **Department of Planning and Zoning** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA. https://www.fishers.in.us/153/Planning-Zoning
- **Department of Public Works (DPW)** — sanitary sewer, stormwater operations, streets. https://www.fishers.in.us/199/Public-Works
- **Department of Code Enforcement / Building Permits** — improvement location and building permits. https://www.fishers.in.us/152/Code-Enforcement
- **Hamilton Southeastern Utilities (HSE Utilities)** — sanitary sewer over much of the south and east portions of the city. https://www.hseutilities.com/
- **Citizens Water and Indiana American Water** — water service depending on location (verify in current revision).
- **Fishers Fire Department** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, and emergency access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Fishers Code of Ordinances** — hosted on American Legal Publishing (verify current host in current revision).
- **Fishers Unified Development Ordinance (UDO)** — zoning, subdivision control, development standards.
- **Fishers Stormwater Technical Standards Manual** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Fishers Construction Standards and Standard Details** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA ramps, structures (verify in current revision).
- **HSE Utilities standards** — sanitary sewer extensions in their service territory.
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Planning and Engineering for substantive projects.
2. TAC review of concept submittal.
3. Plan Commission action on primary plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, the applicable sanitary utility, and Fire.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the Fishers manual.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and utilities.
8. Inspection, punch-list closeout, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Fishers manual.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Fishers standards, including subgrade and aggregate base.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from the applicable water and sanitary providers.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the UDO.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- Two sanitary providers within the city limits (Fishers DPW and HSE Utilities); confirm service area early because design standards and connection fees differ.
- Fishers MS4 program is operated by the city; the Hamilton County Surveyor still has jurisdiction over regulated drains that traverse the city.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply for development plans (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
- The UDO combines zoning and subdivision control; older Hamilton County zoning references no longer apply in the corporate limits.
