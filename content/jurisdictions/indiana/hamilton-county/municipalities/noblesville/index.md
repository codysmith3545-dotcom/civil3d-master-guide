> **TL;DR**
> 1. Noblesville is the Hamilton County seat and runs its own engineering, planning, and building review; expect a Technical Advisory Committee step on substantive submittals.
> 2. The City of Noblesville Stormwater Technical Standards Manual governs drainage design within the corporate limits (verify current edition in current revision).
> 3. Noblesville operates its own water and sanitary sewer utilities through the Department of Utilities; this is unusual for the metro area and avoids the multi-utility coordination seen in Carmel and Fishers.

## Authorities

- **Engineering Department** — construction plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.cityofnoblesville.org/department/?fDD=12-0
- **Department of Planning and Development** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA. https://www.cityofnoblesville.org/department/?fDD=21-0
- **Building Department** — improvement location, building permits, inspections.
- **Noblesville Department of Utilities** — water and wastewater service for most of the city. https://www.cityofnoblesville.org/department/?fDD=24-0
- **Noblesville Fire Department** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, and access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Noblesville Code of Ordinances** — hosted on American Legal Publishing (verify current host in current revision).
- **Noblesville Unified Development Ordinance (UDO)** — zoning, subdivision control, and development standards.
- **Noblesville Stormwater Technical Standards Manual** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Noblesville Standard Construction Details / Standard Specifications** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA, sanitary, water (verify in current revision).
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Planning and Engineering on substantive projects.
2. TAC review of the concept package.
3. Plan Commission action on primary plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Utilities, and Fire.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the city manual.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and Utilities.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Noblesville manual.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Noblesville standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letter from the Noblesville Department of Utilities.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the UDO.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- Single municipal utility for both water and sanitary simplifies will-serve and connection processes compared with neighboring cities.
- Noblesville MS4 program is operated by the city; the Hamilton County Surveyor still has jurisdiction over regulated drains.
- Downtown and historic areas have additional design overlay standards in the UDO (verify current overlays in current revision).
- Engineering review and inspection fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
