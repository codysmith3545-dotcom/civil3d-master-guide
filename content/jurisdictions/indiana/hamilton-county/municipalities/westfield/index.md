> **TL;DR**
> 1. Westfield runs its own engineering, planning, and building review; the city has grown rapidly and the development standards are actively maintained.
> 2. The City of Westfield Stormwater Technical Standards Manual governs drainage design within the corporate limits (verify current edition in current revision).
> 3. Westfield operates Westfield Public Works for stormwater and sanitary sewer; water service over much of the city is provided by Citizens Water (verify service area in current revision).

## Authorities

- **Engineering Department** — plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.westfield.in.gov/162/Engineering
- **Department of Community Development / Planning** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA. https://www.westfield.in.gov/161/Community-Development
- **Building Department** — improvement location, building permits, inspections.
- **Westfield Public Works** — sanitary sewer, stormwater operations, streets. https://www.westfield.in.gov/176/Public-Works
- **Citizens Water** — water service in much of the city. https://www.citizensenergygroup.com/
- **Westfield Fire Department** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Westfield Code of Ordinances** on American Legal / Municode (verify current host in current revision).
- **Westfield-Washington Township Unified Development Ordinance (UDO)** — zoning, subdivision control, development standards.
- **Westfield Stormwater Technical Standards Manual** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Westfield Standard Construction Details / Standard Specifications** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA, sanitary (verify in current revision).
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Community Development and Engineering.
2. TAC review of the concept submittal.
3. Plan Commission action on primary plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Public Works (sanitary), Citizens Water, and Fire.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the city manual.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and the relevant utilities.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Westfield manual.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Westfield standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from Westfield Public Works (sanitary) and Citizens Water (water).
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the UDO.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- Westfield's rapid growth means the standards manual and details are updated more often than peer cities; always check the manual revision date before producing plans.
- Westfield MS4 program is operated by the city; the Hamilton County Surveyor still has jurisdiction over regulated drains.
- Sanitary sewer is provided by the city, but water is typically Citizens Water; coordinate two will-serves.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
