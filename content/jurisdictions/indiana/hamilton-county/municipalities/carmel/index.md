> **TL;DR**
> 1. Carmel runs its own engineering and stormwater plan review; expect a multi-department TAC process before construction plans are released.
> 2. The City of Carmel Stormwater Technical Standards Manual governs drainage design within corporate limits (verify current edition in current revision).
> 3. Carmel operates Carmel Utilities for water; sanitary sewer service in Carmel is provided by Clay Township Regional Waste District (CTRWD) over most of the city, with limited exceptions (verify in current revision).

## Authorities

- **City Engineer / Engineering Department** — plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.carmel.in.gov/department-services/engineering
- **Plan Commission and Department of Community Services (DOCS)** — zoning, planning, plats, development plan review. https://www.carmel.in.gov/department-services/community-services-/-planning-zoning
- **Building and Code Services** — improvement location permits, building permits, inspections. https://www.carmel.in.gov/department-services/community-services-/-building-and-code-services
- **Carmel Utilities (water)** — water main extensions, will-serve, meter sizing. https://www.carmel.in.gov/department-services/utilities
- **Clay Township Regional Waste District (CTRWD)** — sanitary sewer for most Carmel parcels. https://www.ctrwd.org/
- **Carmel Fire Department** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Carmel Code of Ordinances** on American Legal / Municode (verify current host in current revision).
- **Carmel Unified Development Ordinance (UDO)** — zoning, subdivision control, development standards, administered by DOCS.
- **Carmel Stormwater Technical Standards Manual** — design criteria for water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP selection (verify adopted edition and amendment date in current revision).
- **Carmel Standard Construction Details / Standard Drawings** — pavement sections, curb, sidewalk, ADA ramps, structures (verify in current revision).
- **CTRWD design standards** — applicable to sanitary sewer extensions in their service area.
- **Carmel Utilities water standards** — water main material, separation, hydrants.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with DOCS and Engineering for substantive projects.
2. Concept review and Technical Advisory Committee (TAC) submittal.
3. Plan Commission action on primary plat or development plan as applicable.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Carmel Utilities, CTRWD, and Fire.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI processed concurrently; SWPPP reviewed against the city manual.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering before recording or construction release.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and the affected utilities.
8. Inspections, punch list, as-built submittal, and acceptance for public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Carmel manual.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP, with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per the city standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from Carmel Utilities and CTRWD where applicable.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the UDO.
- Traffic study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).
- Boundary and topographic survey on Indiana State Plane East, NAD83, with NAVD88 elevations.

## Notable quirks

- Carmel reviews its own stormwater plans and does not defer to the Hamilton County Surveyor for non-regulated drainage; coordination with the County Surveyor is still required where a regulated drain is touched.
- CTRWD is a separate political subdivision with its own fees, plan review, and inspection process; budget separate review time.
- Engineering escrow and inspection deposit practices apply for public-infrastructure projects (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
- Carmel's UDO consolidates zoning and subdivision control; do not assume the Hamilton County zoning ordinance applies inside corporate limits.
