> **TL;DR**
> 1. Plainfield is a town with substantial industrial and logistics development; its engineering review is mature and assumes large-site stormwater complexity.
> 2. The Plainfield Stormwater Technical Standards govern drainage design within the corporate limits (verify current title and edition in current revision).
> 3. Plainfield operates its own water and wastewater utilities; this consolidates utility coordination but adds plant-capacity review for large industrial users.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Planning and Engineering for substantive projects.
2. TAC review of the concept package.
3. Plan Commission or Town Council action on plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Water and Wastewater, and Fire Territory.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the town standards.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and the utilities.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Plainfield standards.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Plainfield standards, sized for the actual truck loading on industrial sites.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from Plainfield Water and Wastewater, including capacity confirmation for large users.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the zoning ordinance.
- Traffic impact study for warehouse, distribution, and large retail uses (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- Heavy industrial / logistics base means Engineering routinely reviews large-site SWPPPs and oversize sanitary loads; build extra coordination time into the schedule.
- The Hendricks County Surveyor retains jurisdiction over regulated drains that cross the town.
- Plainfield MS4 program is operated by the town.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
