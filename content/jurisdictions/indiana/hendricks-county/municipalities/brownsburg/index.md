> **TL;DR**
> 1. Brownsburg is a town with active engineering and planning departments; expect a TAC step on subdivision and commercial submittals.
> 2. The Brownsburg stormwater technical standards govern drainage design within the corporate limits (verify current title and edition in current revision).
> 3. Brownsburg operates its own water and sanitary sewer utilities through the Department of Public Works, simplifying utility coordination compared with neighboring towns.

## Authorities

- **Engineering Department** — construction plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.brownsburg.org/162/Engineering
- **Planning and Building Department** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA, permits. https://www.brownsburg.org/176/Planning-Building
- **Department of Public Works** — water, sanitary, streets, stormwater operations. https://www.brownsburg.org/170/Public-Works
- **Brownsburg Fire Territory** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Brownsburg Code of Ordinances** — hosted on American Legal / Municode (verify current host in current revision).
- **Brownsburg Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Control Ordinance** — administered by Planning (verify combined or separate documents in current revision).
- **Brownsburg Stormwater Technical Standards** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Brownsburg Standard Construction Details / Standard Specifications** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA, water, sanitary (verify in current revision).
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Planning and Engineering.
2. TAC review of the concept package.
3. Plan Commission action on plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Public Works (water and sanitary), and Fire Territory.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the town standards.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and DPW.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Brownsburg standards.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Brownsburg standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from Brownsburg DPW for water and sanitary.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the zoning ordinance.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- Single municipal utility for both water and sanitary, which is unusual among Hendricks County towns.
- Brownsburg MS4 program is operated by the town; the Hendricks County Surveyor still has jurisdiction over regulated drains.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
- Brownsburg's growth corridor along US 136 / SR 267 means INDOT permitting often runs in parallel with town review.
