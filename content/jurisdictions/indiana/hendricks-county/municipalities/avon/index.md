> **TL;DR**
> 1. Avon is a town that runs its own engineering and planning review; expect a TAC step on subdivisions and commercial development plans.
> 2. The Avon stormwater technical standards govern drainage design within the corporate limits (verify current title and edition in current revision).
> 3. Avon does not provide water service directly; water is typically Indiana American Water, while sanitary is handled by the Town of Avon Sewer Utility (verify provider for the parcel in current revision).

## Authorities

- **Engineering Department** — construction plan review, ROW, drainage, surety. https://www.avongov.org/government/engineering_department/index.php
- **Planning and Zoning Department** — zoning, plats, development plan, BZA. https://www.avongov.org/government/planning_and_zoning/index.php
- **Building Department** — improvement location, building permits, inspections.
- **Town of Avon Sewer Utility** — sanitary in much of the town (verify service area in current revision).
- **Indiana American Water** — water service in much of the area. https://www.amwater.com/inaw
- **Washington Township Avon Fire Territory** — fire-flow, hydrant spacing, access review.

## Codes and adopted standards

- **Avon Code of Ordinances** — hosted on American Legal / Municode (verify current host in current revision).
- **Avon Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Control Ordinance** — administered by Planning (verify combined or separate documents in current revision).
- **Avon Stormwater Technical Standards** — water-quality, water-quantity, conveyance, and BMP design criteria.
- **Avon Standard Construction Details / Standard Specifications** — pavement, curb, sidewalk, ADA, sanitary (verify in current revision).
- Indiana State Plane East (NAD83) and NAVD88 are the expected horizontal and vertical references.

## Plan-review process (typical sequence)

1. Pre-application meeting with Planning and Engineering.
2. TAC review of the concept package.
3. Plan Commission action on plat, development plan, or PUD.
4. Construction plan review by Engineering, Sewer Utility, the water provider, and Fire Territory.
5. IDEM Rule 5 NOI submittal; SWPPP reviewed against the town standards.
6. Performance and maintenance surety posted with Engineering.
7. Pre-construction meeting with Engineering and the utilities.
8. Inspections, as-built submittal, and acceptance of public infrastructure.

## Submittal items the reviewer always asks about

- Stormwater management plan, drainage report, and hydrology summary tied to the Avon standards.
- Erosion control plan and SWPPP with IDEM Rule 5 NOI for disturbances of one acre or more.
- Pavement section per Avon standards, including subgrade treatment.
- ADA compliance documentation for sidewalks, ramps, and crossings.
- Will-serve letters from the sewer utility and the water provider.
- Landscape and tree-preservation plan per the zoning ordinance.
- Traffic impact study when trip-generation thresholds are exceeded (verify current threshold in current revision).

## Notable quirks

- Multiple utility providers within the town; do not assume a single will-serve covers both water and sewer.
- The Hendricks County Surveyor retains jurisdiction over regulated drains, which is common in Avon's flatter watersheds.
- Avon MS4 program is operated by the town; coordinate post-construction BMP inspections accordingly.
- Engineering escrow and review fees apply (verify current fee schedule in current revision).
