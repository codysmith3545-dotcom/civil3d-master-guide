## Surveying fundamentals

### **Acquiescence**

A boundary doctrine recognizing a line that adjoining owners have mutually treated as the boundary for a long period (in Indiana, generally 20 years), typically evidenced by a fence or other physical marker, even if it differs from the deed line.

*Related:* adverse possession, practical location, color of title
*Sources:* *Herrell v. Casey*, 609 N.E.2d 1145 (Ind. Ct. App. 1993); see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **Angle of elevation**

The vertical angle measured upward from a horizontal reference plane to a sighted point. Its complement to 90 degrees is the zenith angle returned by most total stations.

*Related:* zenith angle, vertical angle, refraction
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, 15th ed., Ch. 4

### **ATR**

Automatic Target Recognition. A robotic total station feature that locks onto a prism using image processing or coaxial sensors, enabling one-person operation and faster sighting.

*Related:* robotic total station, MMS, total station
*Sources:* Leica/Trimble product documentation; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **Azimuth**

A horizontal direction measured clockwise from a reference meridian (most commonly grid north or geodetic north), expressed in degrees from 0 to 360.

*Related:* bearing, basis of bearings
*Sources:* NGS Geodetic Glossary; Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 7

### **Backsight**

(1) In leveling, the rod reading taken on a point of known elevation to determine the instrument height. (2) In angle measurement, the sight to a known reference point used to orient the instrument before turning a foresight angle.

*Related:* foresight, instrument height, turning point
*Sources:* FGCS *Standards and Specifications for Geodetic Control Networks*, 1984

### **Base station**

A stationary GNSS receiver occupying a known coordinate that broadcasts carrier-phase corrections to one or more rovers, enabling RTK or PPK positioning.

*Related:* rover, RTK, RTN, NTRIP, CORS
*Sources:* NGS *User Guidelines for Single Base Real Time GNSS Positioning*, 2014

### **Bearing**

A horizontal direction expressed as an acute angle from north or south toward east or west (e.g., N 45 deg 30 min E). A *forward* bearing is the direction of travel; the *back* bearing is the reverse direction (180 deg flip).

*Related:* azimuth, basis of bearings, metes and bounds
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 7

### **Benchmark (BM / TBM)**

A monumented point of known elevation used as the reference for vertical measurements. A *temporary benchmark* (TBM) is set for a specific project; a published BM is part of NGS or a local agency network.

*Related:* level loop, NAVD88, NGS, turning point
*Sources:* NGS data sheet conventions; FGCC *Standards and Specifications*, 1984

### **Collimation**

The condition of a survey instrument's line of sight or measurement axis being properly aligned with the mechanical axes. Periodic *collimation checks* (e.g., the peg test for levels, two-face checks for total stations) detect and quantify error.

*Related:* total station, EDM, plumb
*Sources:* Manufacturer field manuals; Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed.; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **Compass rule**

A traverse-adjustment method (also called the Bowditch rule) that distributes misclosure in latitudes and departures in proportion to the length of each course. Suitable when angular and linear precision are roughly equal.

*Related:* transit rule, least-squares adjustment, traverse
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed., Ch. 8

### **CORS**

Continuously Operating Reference Station. A permanent GNSS receiver that streams or archives observations for use in differential and post-processed positioning. The U.S. NGS CORS network supports OPUS and many state RTNs (e.g., InCORS in Indiana).

*Related:* OPUS, RTN, NTRIP, NSRS
*Sources:* NGS, [CORS Network](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/CORS/); see [Control Networks](field-and-boundary/control-networks/index.md)

### **Curvature correction**

An adjustment applied to long sights to account for the Earth's curvature, which causes a level line to depart from a horizontal line by approximately 0.667D^2 feet, where D is the sight distance in miles.

*Related:* refraction, level loop
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 4

### **Datum**

A reference frame for measurement: a horizontal datum (e.g., NAD83) for latitudes/longitudes, a vertical datum (e.g., NAVD88) for elevations. Datums are realized by control monuments or geodetic models.

*Related:* NAD83, NAVD88, NSRS, projection, geoid
*Sources:* NGS Geodetic Glossary

### **DMD**

Double Meridian Distance. A method for computing the area enclosed by a closed traverse using the sum of meridian distances and the departures of each course; equivalent to the coordinate (shoelace) method.

*Related:* compass rule, traverse, latitude/departure
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 12

### **EDM**

Electronic Distance Measurement. The measurement of slope distance using modulated infrared or visible light (or microwave) reflected from a prism or surface. Modern EDMs achieve millimeter-level precision over kilometers.

*Related:* total station, prism constant, robotic total station
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 6

### **Ellipsoid**

A mathematically defined oblate spheroid (e.g., GRS80) approximating the Earth's shape, used as the reference surface for geodetic latitudes, longitudes, and ellipsoid heights.

*Related:* geoid, datum, NAD83
*Sources:* NGS Geodetic Glossary; Snyder, *Map Projections - A Working Manual*, USGS PP 1395

### **Fix / float**

GNSS solution states. A *fixed* solution has resolved integer carrier-phase ambiguities and provides centimeter-level accuracy; a *float* solution has not, with decimeter accuracy. Always wait for fix before recording RTK shots.

*Related:* RTK, RTN, base station
*Sources:* NGS *User Guidelines for Single Base RTK*, 2014

### **Foresight**

(1) In leveling, the rod reading on a turning point or unknown elevation, used to compute its elevation. (2) In angle measurement, the sight to the new (unknown) point after backsighting a known reference.

*Related:* backsight, intermediate sight, turning point
*Sources:* FGCC *Standards and Specifications*, 1984

### **Geodetic survey**

A survey that accounts for the Earth's curvature and uses an ellipsoidal reference surface. Required for large areas, high precision, or when results must integrate with national datums.

*Related:* plane survey, datum, ellipsoid, NSRS
*Sources:* NGS Geodetic Glossary; FGCS *Standards*, 1984

### **Geoid**

An equipotential surface of the Earth's gravity field that closely approximates mean sea level. Orthometric heights (NAVD88, NAPGD2022) are measured from the geoid; the difference between geoid and ellipsoid height is the geoid undulation.

*Related:* ellipsoid, NAVD88, NAPGD2022, geoid model
*Sources:* NGS, [Geoid Models](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/GEOID/)

### **Geoid model (12B, 18, 2022)**

A grid of geoid undulations (geoid - ellipsoid separation) used to convert ellipsoid heights to orthometric heights. *Geoid12B* (NAD83(2011)/NAVD88), *Geoid18* (current NAD83(2011)/NAVD88 in CONUS), and *GEOID2022* (forthcoming, paired with NAPGD2022).

*Related:* geoid, NAVD88, NAPGD2022, NSRS 2022
*Sources:* NGS, [Geoid18](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/GEOID/GEOID18/); NGS Modernization roadmap, 2024

### **Instrument height (HI)**

In leveling, the elevation of the instrument's line of sight, computed as benchmark elevation + backsight reading. Subtracting a foresight from HI yields the new point's elevation.

*Related:* backsight, foresight, level loop
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 5

### **Intermediate sight**

A rod reading taken between a backsight and the next foresight to obtain elevations on intermediate points without changing instrument setup. Not used to advance the level loop.

*Related:* backsight, foresight, level loop
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 5

### **Latitude/departure**

The components of a traverse course resolved into north-south (*latitude*) and east-west (*departure*) directions. Latitude = length x cos(bearing); departure = length x sin(bearing). The sums around a closed traverse should be zero.

*Related:* traverse, compass rule, DMD
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 10

### **Least-squares adjustment**

A statistically rigorous method for distributing observation residuals so that the sum of squared, weighted residuals is minimized. The standard for adjusting modern survey networks; produces error ellipses and statistical metrics.

*Related:* compass rule, transit rule, traverse, control network
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed.; see [Control Networks](field-and-boundary/control-networks/index.md)

### **Level loop**

A leveling circuit that begins and ends on the same benchmark (or pair of benchmarks of known elevation difference). Misclosure is compared to allowable tolerances based on order of accuracy and loop length.

*Related:* benchmark, instrument height, NAVD88
*Sources:* FGCS *Standards and Specifications*, 1984; NGS *Bluebook* for vertical observations

### **MMS**

Mobile Mapping System. A vehicle-mounted sensor platform combining GNSS, IMU, and lidar/cameras to capture geo-referenced data while moving. Distinct from static terrestrial laser scanning.

*Related:* total station, ATR
*Sources:* ASPRS *Manual of Mobile Mapping Technology*, 2018; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **NTRIP**

Networked Transport of RTCM via Internet Protocol. The standard for streaming GNSS correction data over the internet from a CORS or RTN to field rovers.

*Related:* RTN, RTK, base station, CORS
*Sources:* RTCM Standard 10410.1 (NTRIP); see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **OPUS**

Online Positioning User Service. NGS's free post-processing service that computes a NAD83(2011) position from a static dual-frequency GNSS observation file by tying it to surrounding CORS.

*Related:* OPUS-S, OPUS-RS, OPUS-Projects, CORS, NSRS
*Sources:* NGS, [OPUS](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/OPUS/)

### **OPUS-Projects**

A web-based extension of OPUS for adjusting multiple static GNSS sessions as a network, including session weighting, vector computation, and integration with project marks.

*Related:* OPUS, OPUS-S, least-squares adjustment, control network
*Sources:* NGS, [OPUS-Projects](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/OPUS-Projects/)

### **OPUS-S / OPUS-RS**

*OPUS-Static* requires 2-48 hours of dual-frequency observation. *OPUS-Rapid Static* (OPUS-RS) requires only 15 minutes to 2 hours but is limited to areas with dense CORS coverage and degraded reliability.

*Related:* OPUS, OPUS-Projects, CORS
*Sources:* NGS, [OPUS user guidance](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/OPUS/about.jsp)

### **Plane survey**

A survey that treats the Earth as locally flat, ignoring curvature. Adequate for small projects (typically less than 12 mi or 20 km in extent) and for sites where coordinates are not tied to a national datum.

*Related:* geodetic survey, projection, state plane
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 1

### **Plumb**

The direction of gravity at a point; the line a freely hanging plumb bob takes. Distinct from the ellipsoid normal because gravity vectors deflect from the normal due to mass anomalies.

*Related:* geoid, level loop
*Sources:* NGS Geodetic Glossary

### **PPK**

Post-Processed Kinematic. A GNSS technique in which rover and base observations are recorded and processed after fieldwork, often producing better solutions than RTK in marginal conditions.

*Related:* RTK, base station, OPUS
*Sources:* NGS *Guidelines for RTK/RTN GNSS Surveying*, 2014; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **PPP**

Precise Point Positioning. A GNSS technique using a single receiver and precise satellite orbit/clock products to compute a position without a local base, typically requiring 30+ minutes of convergence.

*Related:* OPUS, PPK, RTK
*Sources:* NGS overview of GNSS positioning techniques; IGS precise products

### **Prism constant**

The systematic distance offset between the geometric center of a retroreflector prism and the EDM's measured reference point, typically -30 mm or 0 mm. Must be set in the total station to produce correct slope distances.

*Related:* EDM, total station, robotic total station
*Sources:* Manufacturer prism specifications (Leica, Trimble, Topcon)

### **Projection**

A mathematical transformation from the curved Earth (or ellipsoid) to a flat plane. Common projections include Lambert Conformal Conic and Transverse Mercator (the basis of state plane and UTM).

*Related:* state plane, datum, combined scale factor
*Sources:* Snyder, *Map Projections - A Working Manual*, USGS PP 1395

### **Refraction**

The bending of a sightline as it passes through air of varying density, causing apparent elevations to be too high. Combined curvature-and-refraction correction is approximately 0.574D^2 feet, where D is the sight distance in miles.

*Related:* curvature correction, level loop
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 4

### **Robotic total station**

A motorized total station that automatically tracks a prism, allowing one-person operation. Combined with ATR and a data collector, it underpins most modern construction-staking workflows.

*Related:* total station, ATR, EDM, prism constant
*Sources:* Manufacturer documentation; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **Rover**

The mobile GNSS receiver in an RTK or PPK configuration, occupying observed points and applying corrections from a base or RTN to compute fixed-quality positions.

*Related:* base station, RTK, RTN, NTRIP
*Sources:* NGS *User Guidelines for RTK*, 2014

### **RTK**

Real-Time Kinematic. A differential GNSS technique that uses carrier-phase observations to deliver centimeter-level positions in real time, given a fixed integer-ambiguity solution.

*Related:* RTN, PPK, base station, fix/float
*Sources:* NGS *User Guidelines for Single Base RTK*, 2014; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **RTN**

Real-Time Network. A regional set of CORS that broadcasts modeled corrections (VRS, MAC, FKP) so that a rover anywhere in the network area receives a tailored RTK stream without operating a local base. Indiana's network is *InCORS*.

*Related:* RTK, CORS, NTRIP
*Sources:* NGS *Guidelines for RTN GNSS Surveying*, 2014; INDOT InCORS program

### **Total station**

An instrument combining electronic theodolite and EDM, with onboard processor and data storage, used for measuring horizontal angles, vertical angles, and slope distances.

*Related:* EDM, robotic total station, prism constant, collimation
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 8; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **Transit rule**

A traverse-adjustment method that distributes misclosure in latitudes and departures in proportion to each course's latitude or departure, respectively. Appropriate when angular precision exceeds linear precision.

*Related:* compass rule, least-squares adjustment, traverse
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed., Ch. 8

### **Traverse**

A series of connected lines whose angles and distances have been measured. *Open*: starts and ends on different known points (or one unknown). *Closed* (loop): begins and ends on the same point. *Closed-link*: begins and ends on different known points, allowing both angular and positional misclosure to be evaluated.

*Related:* latitude/departure, compass rule, transit rule, control network
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 9

### **Turning point**

A temporary, stable point used in leveling to transfer elevations between instrument setups. Must be a well-defined object that does not move while the rod is moved from foresight to backsight.

*Related:* level loop, backsight, foresight
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 5

### **UCS**

User Coordinate System. In AutoCAD/Civil 3D, a redefinable local coordinate system used for drawing in non-standard orientations. Distinct from the WCS, which is fixed.

*Related:* WCS, drawing settings
*Sources:* Autodesk, *AutoCAD User's Guide* (Coordinate Systems chapter)

### **Vertical angle**

The angle measured in a vertical plane between the line of sight and a horizontal reference (positive up, negative down). Most modern total stations report zenith angles instead.

*Related:* zenith angle, angle of elevation, total station
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 8

### **WCS**

World Coordinate System. The fixed Cartesian reference system in AutoCAD/Civil 3D against which all UCSs are defined. Civil 3D coordinate geometry is stored in WCS.

*Related:* UCS, drawing settings
*Sources:* Autodesk, *AutoCAD User's Guide* (Coordinate Systems chapter)

### **Zenith angle**

The vertical angle measured downward from the local zenith (straight up) to the line of sight. Horizontal sights produce a zenith angle of 90 deg; sights below horizontal exceed 90 deg.

*Related:* vertical angle, angle of elevation
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 8

### **ASPRS**

American Society for Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing. Publishes positional accuracy standards for digital geospatial data, lidar, and orthoimagery. Current standard: *Positional Accuracy Standards for Digital Geospatial Data*, Edition 2 (2023).

*Related:* FGDC, ASPRS lidar specs
*Sources:* ASPRS, *Positional Accuracy Standards*, Ed. 2, 2023

### **FGDC**

Federal Geographic Data Committee. Coordinates federal geospatial data activities and publishes accuracy standards including FGDC-STD-007 (*Geospatial Positioning Accuracy Standards*).

*Related:* ASPRS, FGCS, NSRS
*Sources:* FGDC-STD-007.1-1998 (NSSDA); see [Control Networks](field-and-boundary/control-networks/index.md)

### **Localization**

A site calibration that transforms GNSS-derived coordinates to match local project control by computing a translation, rotation, scale, and inclined-plane fit. Produces a project-specific combined scale factor.

*Related:* combined scale factor, ground-to-grid, base station
*Sources:* Trimble Access / Leica Captivate documentation; see [Control Networks](field-and-boundary/control-networks/index.md)

## Civil 3D objects and concepts

### **Alignment**

A horizontal centerline composed of lines, curves, and (optionally) spirals, carrying stationing and serving as the basis for profiles, corridors, and pipe networks.

*Related:* profile, corridor, baseline (corridor), region
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Alignments*; see [Alignments](civil3d/alignments/index.md)

### **Ambient setting**

A drawing-level default that propagates to objects unless overridden at the object or style level (e.g., units, sign convention, precision). Set in *Drawing Settings* > *Ambient Settings*.

*Related:* drawing settings, expression
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Ambient Settings*

### **Assembly**

The cross-section template, made of subassemblies, that a corridor sweeps along its baseline to generate a model.

*Related:* subassembly, corridor, baseline (corridor), target
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Assemblies*; see [Corridors](civil3d/corridors/index.md)

### **Baseline (corridor)**

The combination of an alignment and a profile that a corridor follows. A corridor may have multiple baselines (e.g., main alignment plus side roads).

*Related:* corridor, region, frequency, target
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Corridor Baselines*

### **Breakline**

A 3D linear feature added to a TIN to enforce ridges, swales, edges, or other discontinuities. Triangle edges cannot cross a breakline.

*Related:* surface (TIN/grid), feature line, figure
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Adding Breaklines*; see [Surfaces](civil3d/surfaces/index.md)

### **Civil view**

In *Drawing Settings*, the choice between Civil 3D viewing of objects (full styling) and AutoCAD-only viewing (simplified). Affects how non-Civil 3D applications display the drawing.

*Related:* drawing settings, label style, point style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Object Layers and Object Display*

### **COGO point**

A Civil 3D point with coordinate-geometry attributes (number, northing, easting, elevation, raw description, full description). Differs from an AutoCAD point by carrying these properties and being controlled by point/label styles.

*Related:* point group, point style, description key, PNEZD
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About COGO Points*; see [Points](civil3d/points/index.md)

### **Corridor**

The 3D model produced by sweeping an assembly along an alignment+profile (baseline) at a specified frequency, optionally constrained by targets such as offset alignments and surfaces.

*Related:* assembly, subassembly, baseline (corridor), region, target
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Corridors*; see [Corridors](civil3d/corridors/index.md)

### **Data shortcut**

A lightweight cross-drawing reference to a Civil 3D object (alignment, profile, surface, pipe network, view frame group, corridor) stored as XML in a working folder. Allows multiple drawings to consume the same source object.

*Related:* vault reference
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Data Shortcuts*; see [Data shortcuts](civil3d/data-shortcuts/index.md)

### **Daylight**

The point where a graded slope intersects existing ground. *Daylight* subassemblies extend a grading slope until it meets the target surface.

*Related:* feature line, grading group, subassembly, target
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Subassemblies Reference*; see [Grading](civil3d/grading/index.md)

### **Description key**

A rule that maps a COGO point's *raw description* to a layer, full description, point style, and label style at import. Description-key sets are searched in order; the first match wins.

*Related:* COGO point, point group, point style, label style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Description Keys*; see [Description keys](civil3d/points/description-keys.md)

### **Drawing settings**

Civil 3D-specific drawing settings (units & zone, transformation, object layers, abbreviations, ambient settings) accessed from the Toolspace *Settings* tab. Distinct from AutoCAD UNITS.

*Related:* ambient setting, civil view
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Drawing Settings*

### **DWT**

AutoCAD/Civil 3D drawing template (.dwt). Carries default styles, layers, settings, and (in Civil 3D) coordinate zone. Country kits ship as DWTs plus supporting style/parts files.

*Related:* drawing settings, parts list
*Sources:* Autodesk, *Country Kits* documentation; see [Templates](customization/templates-and-kits/index.md)

### **Expression**

A formula that computes a value used in a label component, point style, or table cell. Supports drawing properties, object properties, and math functions; evaluated each time the label refreshes.

*Related:* label style, table style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Property Expressions*

### **FBK**

Field Book file (.fbk). A text format that Civil 3D Survey imports into a survey database, containing observations, setups, and figure commands. Generated from raw data collector files (e.g., RW5) by Survey Link or third-party translators.

*Related:* figure, COGO point
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Field Book File Format*; see [Survey](civil3d/survey/index.md)

### **Feature line**

A 3D polyline-like object with elevation at every vertex; the backbone of grading. Can be created from objects, drawn, or extracted from corridors. Behaves as a rule-based object that can be assigned to a site.

*Related:* grading group, daylight, site (Civil 3D), breakline
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Feature Lines*; see [Grading](civil3d/grading/index.md)

### **Figure**

A linework feature defined in the survey database (e.g., edge of pavement, building corner). Created by figure-prefix commands in the FBK and often used as a breakline source for surfaces.

*Related:* FBK, breakline, COGO point
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Survey Figures*; see [Survey](civil3d/survey/index.md)

### **Frequency**

The interval at which a corridor samples its assembly along the baseline (e.g., every 25 ft along tangents, every 5 ft on curves). Set per region; finer frequencies give smoother corridor surfaces at the cost of computation time.

*Related:* corridor, region, assembly
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Corridor Frequency*

### **Grading group**

A container for grading objects that share a common surface output and elevation reference. A grading group can be set to *automatic surface creation* and *project elevations*.

*Related:* feature line, daylight
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Grading Groups*

### **Invert**

The inside-bottom elevation of a pipe at a structure. Distinct from rim (top of structure) and from sump (storage volume below the lowest invert).

*Related:* pipe network, structure, parts list
*Sources:* ASCE/EWRI 24-05; Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Pipe Network Properties*

### **Label style**

A reusable definition controlling how labels display object data: components (text, lines, blocks), layout, anchors, and visibility/dragged-state behavior.

*Related:* table style, point style, marker style, expression
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Label Styles*

### **LandXML**

An open XML schema for civil/survey data exchange (alignments, surfaces, parcels, pipe networks). Enables interoperability between Civil 3D, OpenRoads, 12d, Trimble Business Center, and others.

*Related:* data shortcut, alignment, surface (TIN/grid)
*Sources:* [landxml.org](http://www.landxml.org/); Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Importing LandXML*

### **Layout profile**

A designed (drawn) profile, as opposed to a *surface profile* sampled from an existing surface. Authored in a profile view using PVI-based geometry.

*Related:* profile view, alignment, vertical curve, K-value
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Profiles*; see [Profiles](civil3d/profiles/index.md)

### **Marker style**

A style that controls how a point marker (or other vertex marker) draws: AutoCAD point style, custom marker, or block reference.

*Related:* point style, label style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Point Styles*

### **Match line**

The line on a plan sheet that indicates where the next sheet picks up; created and labeled by Plan Production tools.

*Related:* view frame, plan-profile sheet, sheet set
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Match Lines*; see [Plan Production](civil3d/plan-production/index.md)

### **Parcel**

A 2D enclosed area on a site. In Civil 3D, parcels are topologically maintained within a site; in surveying, *parcel* is the legally defined property tract whose boundary is described in a deed or plat.

*Related:* parcel area, site (Civil 3D), metes and bounds
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Parcels*; see [Parcels](civil3d/parcels/index.md)

### **Parcel area**

The Civil 3D-computed enclosed area of a parcel object, derived from its segment geometry. Reported in label tables and the *Parcel Properties* dialog.

*Related:* parcel, label style, table style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Parcel Properties*

### **Parts list**

A Civil 3D collection of pipe and structure parts available to a pipe network, with their styles, rules, and default sizes. Stored in the drawing; managed via the Toolspace *Settings* tab.

*Related:* pipe network, structure, invert
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Parts Lists*; see [Pipe networks](civil3d/pipe-networks/index.md)

### **Pipe network**

A Civil 3D object representing a system of gravity (or pressure) pipes and structures, with rules-based connectivity, inverts, and sizing. Drives plan and profile labeling and exports to interference-check/hydraulic tools.

*Related:* parts list, structure, invert
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Pipe Networks*; see [Pipe networks](civil3d/pipe-networks/index.md)

### **Plan-profile sheet**

A standard production sheet showing a plan view above a profile view of the same alignment segment. Generated by Plan Production tools using view frames.

*Related:* view frame, sheet set, match line
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Plan Production*; see [Plan Production](civil3d/plan-production/index.md)

### **PNEZD**

A point file format with columns Point, Northing, Easting, Z (elevation), Description (comma- or space-delimited). The Civil 3D default; many vendors emit a PNEZD-compatible export.

*Related:* COGO point, description key
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Point File Formats*; see [Points](civil3d/points/index.md)

### **Point group**

A filter that selects COGO points by criteria (number, name, raw description, elevation, etc.) and applies a point style and point-label style to the matched points. Drives visibility and surface inclusion.

*Related:* COGO point, point style, label style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Point Groups*

### **Point style**

A reusable style controlling a COGO point's marker (size, rotation, source) and 3D geometry. Distinct from the point label style, which controls the displayed text.

*Related:* point group, marker style, label style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Point Styles*

### **Profile view**

The graph (the "window") that displays one or more profiles for an alignment, with stationing along X and elevation along Y. Hosts layout profiles, surface profiles, and pipe-network profiles.

*Related:* alignment, layout profile, vertical curve
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Profile Views*

### **Region (corridor)**

A station range along a corridor baseline within which a single assembly applies at a chosen frequency. Use multiple regions to switch assemblies (e.g., typical, transition, intersection).

*Related:* corridor, baseline (corridor), assembly, frequency
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Corridor Regions*

### **Sheet set**

An AutoCAD-managed collection of sheets (.dst file) used to organize, plot, and publish a project's drawings. Plan Production builds sheet sets automatically.

*Related:* plan-profile sheet, view frame
*Sources:* Autodesk, *Sheet Set Manager*

### **Site (Civil 3D)**

A topology container for parcels and feature lines that share connectivity rules. Objects in different sites do not interact topologically; place feature lines in a no-parcel site to avoid accidental splits.

*Related:* parcel, feature line
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Sites Collection*

### **Structure**

A pipe-network node (manhole, inlet, junction, end structure). Has a rim, sump, geometry, and zero or more connected pipes.

*Related:* pipe network, parts list, invert
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Structures*

### **Subassembly**

A piece of a corridor assembly (lane, curb, sidewalk, daylight, ditch). Civil 3D ships hundreds; custom subassemblies authored via Subassembly Composer or .NET.

*Related:* assembly, corridor, daylight
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Subassemblies Reference*; see [Corridors](civil3d/corridors/index.md)

### **Surface (TIN/grid)**

A digital terrain model in Civil 3D. *TIN* surfaces store an irregular triangle mesh; *grid* surfaces store elevations on a regular grid. TIN is the default and supports breaklines, contours, and analyses.

*Related:* TIN, breakline, feature line
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Surfaces*; see [Surfaces](civil3d/surfaces/index.md)

### **Surveyor's report**

In Indiana, a statutory report (IC 36-2-12-14) that the registered land surveyor files with the county recorder summarizing a retracement survey, monuments found and set, evidence relied on, and measurements.

*Related:* retracement, monuments, original survey
*Sources:* Ind. Code 36-2-12-14; 865 IAC 1-12; see [Monuments and Evidence](field-and-boundary/monuments-and-evidence/index.md)

### **Table style**

A reusable definition controlling how a Civil 3D table draws: columns, headers, formats, sort order, split behavior. Tables can list points, segments, parcels, structures, pipes, and more.

*Related:* label style, expression
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Table Styles*

### **Target (corridor)**

An object a subassembly follows: a target *surface* for daylighting, a target *alignment* for offsets, a target *profile* for elevations. Set per region in *Corridor Properties* > *Targets*.

*Related:* corridor, subassembly, daylight
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Corridor Targets*

### **TIN**

Triangulated Irregular Network. A surface representation built from a Delaunay triangulation of input points and breaklines. The default Civil 3D surface type.

*Related:* surface (TIN/grid), breakline
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About TIN Surfaces*; Mark, *Computer Methods in the Geosciences* (1996)

### **Vault reference**

In Autodesk Vault Pro, a managed reference to a Civil 3D object that supports check-in/check-out and version history. Heavier than a data shortcut but provides true source control.

*Related:* data shortcut
*Sources:* Autodesk Vault documentation; see [Data shortcuts](civil3d/data-shortcuts/index.md)

### **Vertical curve**

A parabolic curve connecting two profile tangents. Quantified by *L* (length) and *A* (algebraic difference of grades), with K = L / |A|.

*Related:* K-value, layout profile, profile view, sight distance
*Sources:* AASHTO, *A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets* (Green Book), 7th ed., 2018; see [Vertical curve design](engineering/roadway-design/vertical-curve-design.md)

### **View frame**

A rectangle along an alignment that defines the extent of a plan/profile sheet, including overlap. Generated as a *view frame group* by the Plan Production wizard.

*Related:* plan-profile sheet, match line, sheet set
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Plan Production*

## Boundary and legal

### **Adverse possession**

A method of acquiring title to land by occupying it openly, notoriously, exclusively, hostilely, and continuously for a statutory period. In Indiana the period is 10 years (IC 32-21-7-1) and the claimant must also have paid taxes during that period (IC 32-21-7-2).

*Related:* prescriptive easement, color of title, acquiescence
*Sources:* Ind. Code 32-21-7-1, 32-21-7-2; *Fraley v. Minger*, 829 N.E.2d 476 (Ind. 2005); see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **Aliquot part**

A subdivision of a PLSS section into halves, quarters, quarter-quarters, etc. (e.g., NW1/4 SE1/4 = 40-acre tract). Aliquot descriptions are simpler and senior to most metes-and-bounds carve-outs within the same section.

*Related:* PLSS, section, government lot, BLM Manual
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 3; see [Legal descriptions](field-and-boundary/legal-descriptions/index.md)

### **ALTA/NSPS**

National survey standard, jointly published by the American Land Title Association and the National Society of Professional Surveyors, for title-insurance-grade boundary surveys. Current version: 2021.

*Related:* NSPS, boundary survey
*Sources:* ALTA/NSPS, *2021 Minimum Standard Detail Requirements for ALTA/NSPS Land Title Surveys*; see [Boundary and ALTA](field-and-boundary/boundary-and-alta/index.md)

### **Avulsion**

A sudden, perceptible loss or addition of land by water action (e.g., a river cutting a new channel in a flood). Unlike accretion, avulsion does not change the boundary; the original line remains.

*Related:* accretion, reliction, riparian, meander line
*Sources:* *Nebraska v. Iowa*, 143 U.S. 359 (1892); Robillard, Wilson, Brown, *Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **Basis of bearings**

The reference frame for the directions in a survey or description: grid (state plane), geodetic (true north), magnetic, astronomic, or assumed. Must be stated on every plat and in every legal description.

*Related:* azimuth, bearing, state plane
*Sources:* ALTA/NSPS 2021, Section 6.B.iv; see [Legal descriptions](field-and-boundary/legal-descriptions/index.md)

### **BLM Manual**

The Bureau of Land Management's *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, the federal standard for cadastral surveying of public lands and for restoring lost or obliterated PLSS corners. Current edition: 2009.

*Related:* PLSS, proportioning, section, original survey
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions for the Survey of the Public Lands of the United States*, 2009

### **Color of title**

A claim to land based on a written instrument that appears to convey title but is in fact defective. Often shortens or eases the proof requirements for adverse possession.

*Related:* adverse possession, acquiescence, junior/senior right
*Sources:* Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.; see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **Easement**

A non-possessory right to use another's land for a specific purpose. *Appurtenant*: benefits a particular parcel. *In gross*: benefits a person or entity (e.g., utility easement).

*Related:* right-of-way, prescriptive easement, encroachment
*Sources:* Restatement (Third) of Property: Servitudes; see [Easements and ROW](field-and-boundary/easements-and-row/index.md)

### **Encroachment**

A physical intrusion of an improvement onto adjoining land or onto an easement. ALTA Table A item 4 requires identification.

*Related:* easement, ALTA/NSPS, riparian
*Sources:* ALTA/NSPS 2021, Table A; see [Boundary and ALTA](field-and-boundary/boundary-and-alta/index.md)

### **Exception (deed)**

In a deed, a clause that withdraws something from the granted estate (e.g., excepting a strip previously conveyed). Distinct from a *reservation*, which creates a new right in the grantor.

*Related:* reservation, metes and bounds, point of beginning
*Sources:* Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **Government lot**

A PLSS subdivision, usually irregular, created where a normal aliquot subdivision is impossible (e.g., abutting a meandered water body, or in fractional sections at township boundaries). Numbered consecutively within a section.

*Related:* aliquot part, section, meander line, PLSS
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 3

### **Indiana 865 IAC**

Title 865 of the Indiana Administrative Code, the rules of the Indiana State Board of Registration for Professional Surveyors, governing licensure and survey practice (notably 865 IAC 1-12, *Standards for the Competent Practice of Land Surveying in Indiana*).

*Related:* PE/PS/LS, surveyor's report
*Sources:* 865 IAC 1-12; see [Indiana state](jurisdictions/indiana/state/index.md)

### **Junior/senior right**

When a parcel is created out of a larger tract, the earlier (senior) deed prevails over later (junior) deeds in case of conflict. Critical when interpreting overlapping descriptions in a sequential conveyance.

*Related:* original survey, retracement, color of title
*Sources:* Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **Littoral**

Pertaining to the shore of a sea or large lake (as opposed to *riparian*, which is for rivers and streams). Littoral rights and the ordinary high water mark govern lakefront boundaries.

*Related:* riparian, accretion, meander line
*Sources:* Cole, *Water Boundaries*, 1997 ed.; see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **Meander line**

A line of approximate boundary between upland and a navigable water body, run during the original PLSS survey along the ordinary high water mark. Not the legal boundary, which is the water itself, subject to accretion/avulsion/reliction.

*Related:* government lot, accretion, avulsion, reliction
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 3

### **Metes and bounds**

A description method using bearings and distances ("metes") and physical references ("bounds") starting at a point of beginning and traversing the parcel perimeter back to the POB.

*Related:* point of beginning, point of commencement, bearing
*Sources:* Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.; see [Legal descriptions](field-and-boundary/legal-descriptions/index.md)

### **Original survey**

The survey that first created a parcel boundary on the ground. Its monuments, when found, are senior to subsequent measurements; later surveyors *retrace* rather than re-create.

*Related:* retracement, junior/senior right, monuments
*Sources:* *Diehl v. Zanger*, 39 Mich. 601 (1878); Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **PLSS**

Public Land Survey System. The federal cadastral grid established by the Land Ordinance of 1785 to subdivide public lands into 6-mile-square townships, 1-mile-square sections, and aliquot parts.

*Related:* township, range, section, aliquot part, BLM Manual
*Sources:* Land Ordinance of 1785; BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009

### **Point of beginning (POB)**

The starting point of the parcel description; the perimeter must close back to this point. Not necessarily the same as the *point of commencement*, which is a remote reference.

*Related:* point of commencement, metes and bounds
*Sources:* Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **Point of commencement**

A remote, well-defined reference point (often a section corner) from which the description proceeds by tie courses to the actual point of beginning. Used when the POB is not directly recoverable from public monuments.

*Related:* point of beginning, metes and bounds
*Sources:* Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **Practical location**

A boundary doctrine recognizing a line that adjoining owners have located on the ground and treated as the boundary, even when the deed is ambiguous or the original line was lost.

*Related:* acquiescence, adverse possession
*Sources:* *Wood v. Kuper*, 150 Ind. App. 230 (1972); Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **Prescriptive easement**

An easement acquired by adverse use, generally with the same elements as adverse possession but conferring only a use right, not title. In Indiana, generally 20 years of adverse use.

*Related:* adverse possession, easement, acquiescence
*Sources:* *Wilfong v. Cessna Corp.*, 838 N.E.2d 403 (Ind. 2005); see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **Range**

In the PLSS, a north-south column of townships, numbered east or west of the principal meridian (e.g., R3E = third range east). Combined with the township row to identify a 36-square-mile township.

*Related:* township, section, PLSS
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 3

### **Reliction**

The gradual recession of a water body that exposes new land. Like accretion, the new land belongs to the upland owner; like accretion, the boundary moves.

*Related:* accretion, avulsion, riparian, littoral
*Sources:* *Nebraska v. Iowa*, 143 U.S. 359 (1892); Cole, *Water Boundaries*, 1997 ed.

### **Reservation**

In a deed, a clause that creates a new right (often an easement or estate) in favor of the grantor over the conveyed land. Distinct from an *exception*, which carves something out of the grant.

*Related:* exception (deed), easement
*Sources:* Restatement (Third) of Property: Servitudes; Robillard et al., *Brown's Boundary Control and Legal Principles*, 7th ed.

### **Retracement**

A survey that recovers the original boundary by following the steps of the original surveyor, honoring found monuments and original calls. Distinct from creating a new boundary.

*Related:* original survey, surveyor's report, junior/senior right
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 6; *Diehl v. Zanger*, 39 Mich. 601 (1878)

### **Right-of-way (ROW)**

(1) A specific kind of easement, usually for transit (road, rail, utility). (2) The land area within the boundaries of a public road, railroad, or utility corridor.

*Related:* easement, encroachment
*Sources:* AASHTO, *A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets*; see [Easements and ROW](field-and-boundary/easements-and-row/index.md)

### **Riparian**

Pertaining to the bank of a flowing watercourse (river or stream). Riparian rights and boundaries follow the water's ordinary high water mark and shift with accretion, reliction, and avulsion.

*Related:* littoral, accretion, avulsion, reliction, meander line
*Sources:* Cole, *Water Boundaries*, 1997 ed.; see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **Section**

In the PLSS, a one-square-mile (640-acre) subdivision of a township, numbered 1-36 starting in the NE corner and snaking westward and back. Subdivided by aliquot parts.

*Related:* township, range, aliquot part, PLSS
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 3

### **Township**

In the PLSS, (1) a 6-mile-square block of land containing 36 sections; or (2) a row of such blocks numbered north or south of the base line. *Township 16 North, Range 4 East* names both.

*Related:* range, section, aliquot part, PLSS
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 3

### **Proportioning**

The BLM method for restoring lost (not obliterated) PLSS corners by proportional measurement between existing original corners. *Single proportion* uses one line; *double proportion* (the standard for lost section corners) uses two intersecting lines.

*Related:* PLSS, section, retracement, BLM Manual
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 7; see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **As-built**

A survey of what was actually constructed, including dimensions, elevations, and deviations from the design. Often required by the regulating agency before final acceptance.

*Related:* construction staking
*Sources:* see [As-builts](field-and-boundary/as-builts/index.md)

## Engineering and drainage

### **Bioretention**

A structural BMP that captures stormwater in a vegetated, engineered-soil basin and infiltrates or filters it. Sometimes called a *rain garden* in residential applications.

*Related:* BMP, infiltration, retention, WQv
*Sources:* US EPA, *National Menu of Stormwater Best Management Practices*; see [Stormwater](engineering/stormwater/index.md)

### **BMP**

Best Management Practice. A structural or non-structural device, control, or practice used to prevent or reduce stormwater pollution or erosion.

*Related:* WQv, MS4, EPA CGP, bioretention
*Sources:* 40 CFR 122.2; US EPA NPDES program

### **Broad-crested weir**

A weir with a horizontal crest long enough (in the flow direction) for the flow to become nearly parallel to the crest, producing critical depth. Discharge: Q = C_d L H^{3/2}.

*Related:* weir, sharp-crested weir, headwater
*Sources:* Bos, *Discharge Measurement Structures*, 3rd ed., 1989; Chow, *Open-Channel Hydraulics*, 1959

### **Detention**

Temporary storage of stormwater that is released downstream at a controlled rate, typically to match pre-development peak discharge. Distinct from *retention*, which holds water indefinitely until evaporation or infiltration.

*Related:* retention, BMP, riser, low-flow orifice
*Sources:* US EPA, *Preliminary Data Summary of Urban Storm Water Best Management Practices*, 1999

### **EPA CGP**

EPA's Construction General Permit. The federal NPDES permit covering stormwater discharges from construction sites disturbing one or more acres. Requires a SWPPP and erosion-and-sediment controls.

*Related:* NPDES, MS4, SWPPP
*Sources:* US EPA, *2022 NPDES Construction General Permit*; see [Erosion and Sediment](engineering/erosion-and-sediment/index.md)

### **Energy grade line (EGL)**

The plot of total energy head (elevation + pressure + velocity) along a flow path. The EGL is always above the HGL by V^2/(2g).

*Related:* HGL, hydraulic radius, Manning's n
*Sources:* Chow, *Open-Channel Hydraulics*, 1959; FHWA HEC-22, 3rd ed., 2009

### **Freeboard**

The vertical distance between a design water surface (in a channel, basin, or pipe) and a controlling top, providing a safety margin. Common minimum: 1 ft for detention basins.

*Related:* HGL, detention, weir
*Sources:* INDOT *Design Manual* Ch. 30; FHWA HEC-22, 3rd ed., 2009

### **Headwater**

The depth of water immediately upstream of a hydraulic structure (typically a culvert), measured from the inlet invert. Compare to tailwater.

*Related:* tailwater, weir, broad-crested weir
*Sources:* FHWA HDS-5, *Hydraulic Design of Highway Culverts*, 3rd ed., 2012

### **HGL**

Hydraulic Grade Line. The elevation of the water surface in open-channel flow, or the piezometric head (elevation + pressure head) in pressurized flow.

*Related:* EGL, Manning's n, hydraulic radius
*Sources:* Chow, *Open-Channel Hydraulics*, 1959; see [Hydraulics](engineering/hydraulics/index.md)

### **Hydraulic radius**

The ratio of cross-sectional flow area to wetted perimeter, A/P. Appears in Manning's equation; for a full circular pipe, R = D/4.

*Related:* Manning's n, HGL
*Sources:* Chow, *Open-Channel Hydraulics*, 1959, Ch. 1

### **Infiltration**

The downward movement of water from the ground surface into soil. Quantified by infiltration rate (e.g., in/hr) and capacity. The basis of permeable pavement, infiltration trenches, and many bioretention designs.

*Related:* bioretention, NRCS curve number, retention
*Sources:* NRCS, *National Engineering Handbook* Part 630, Ch. 7

### **Manning's n**

A roughness coefficient for open-channel and partially full pipe flow. Appears in Manning's equation: V = (1.486/n) R^(2/3) S^(1/2) (US customary). Tabulated for common materials.

*Related:* hydraulic radius, HGL, EGL
*Sources:* Chow, *Open-Channel Hydraulics*, 1959, App. A; FHWA HDS-5, 3rd ed., 2012; see [Hydraulics](engineering/hydraulics/index.md)

### **MS4**

Municipal Separate Storm Sewer System. A publicly owned conveyance for stormwater that discharges to waters of the U.S., regulated under EPA NPDES Phase I (large MS4s) and Phase II (small MS4s).

*Related:* NPDES, EPA CGP, BMP
*Sources:* 40 CFR 122.26; US EPA NPDES Stormwater Program

### **NPDES**

National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System. The federal permitting program (Clean Water Act 402) that authorizes point-source discharges of pollutants to U.S. waters, including stormwater.

*Related:* MS4, EPA CGP
*Sources:* 33 USC 1342 (CWA Section 402); 40 CFR 122

### **NRCS curve number**

A dimensionless parameter (1-100) used in the NRCS runoff equation to convert rainfall depth to runoff depth, depending on land cover, soil hydrologic group, and antecedent moisture.

*Related:* TR-55, runoff coefficient, peak discharge
*Sources:* NRCS, *National Engineering Handbook* Part 630, Ch. 9

### **Peak discharge**

The maximum instantaneous flow rate at a point during a storm event. Computed by methods such as the rational equation (small areas) or NRCS TR-55 (medium areas).

*Related:* runoff coefficient, time of concentration, TR-55, NRCS curve number
*Sources:* NRCS, TR-55, 1986; see [Stormwater](engineering/stormwater/index.md)

### **Pipe friction coefficient**

A material- and flow-regime-specific factor that quantifies head loss due to wall friction. Manning's n is the open-channel form; Hazen-Williams C and Darcy-Weisbach f are pressurized-flow forms.

*Related:* Manning's n, HGL
*Sources:* AWWA M22, *Sizing Water Service Lines and Meters*; Chow, *Open-Channel Hydraulics*, 1959

### **Retention**

Storage of stormwater that does not discharge directly to the downstream system, with volume removed by evaporation, infiltration, or reuse. Distinct from detention.

*Related:* detention, infiltration, bioretention, WQv
*Sources:* US EPA, *Preliminary Data Summary of Urban Storm Water BMPs*, 1999

### **Riser**

A vertical pipe in a detention or retention outlet structure that controls the discharge through one or more orifices and a top weir; sized to meet release-rate requirements for multiple design storms.

*Related:* low-flow orifice, weir, detention
*Sources:* USDA NRCS *Pond Construction*, Ag Handbook 590

### **Low-flow orifice**

A small opening in a riser or outlet structure sized to release the water-quality volume slowly (often over 24-72 hours), independent of the larger storm-control orifice.

*Related:* riser, WQv, detention
*Sources:* US EPA, *National Menu of Stormwater BMPs*; INDOT *Design Manual*

### **Runoff coefficient (C)**

The dimensionless ratio of runoff to rainfall used in the rational method, depending on land cover, slope, and storm return period. Tabulated values range roughly from 0.10 (lawn) to 0.95 (asphalt).

*Related:* peak discharge, NRCS curve number, time of concentration
*Sources:* ASCE, *Design and Construction of Urban Stormwater Management Systems*, MOP 77, 1992

### **Sharp-crested weir**

A weir with a thin, square-edged crest that produces a fully ventilated nappe. Discharge for a rectangular sharp-crested weir: Q = C_d L H^{3/2}, with C_d depending on H/P.

*Related:* weir, broad-crested weir
*Sources:* Bos, *Discharge Measurement Structures*, 3rd ed., 1989

### **Sight distance**

The distance a driver can see ahead. *SSD* = stopping; *PSD* = passing; *DSD* = decision; *ISD* = intersection. Each governs design of horizontal/vertical curves and intersection layout.

*Related:* K-value, vertical curve
*Sources:* AASHTO, *A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets* (Green Book), 7th ed., 2018

### **K-value**

In vertical-curve design, K = L/|A|, the length of curve per unit algebraic difference of grades. Drives sight-distance and comfort criteria; design tables tabulate K-values for design speeds and sight-distance types.

*Related:* vertical curve, sight distance
*Sources:* AASHTO Green Book, 7th ed., 2018, Ch. 3; see [Vertical curve design](engineering/roadway-design/vertical-curve-design.md)

### **Tailwater**

The depth of water immediately downstream of a hydraulic structure. Critical to inlet/outlet control determination for culverts.

*Related:* headwater, weir
*Sources:* FHWA HDS-5, 3rd ed., 2012

### **Time of concentration (Tc)**

The time required for runoff to travel from the hydraulically most distant point in a watershed to the design point. Sum of sheet-flow, shallow-concentrated, and channel-flow travel times.

*Related:* peak discharge, NRCS curve number, TR-55
*Sources:* NRCS TR-55, 1986, Ch. 3; see [Stormwater](engineering/stormwater/index.md)

### **TR-55**

NRCS Technical Release 55, *Urban Hydrology for Small Watersheds*, the standard methodology for estimating runoff and peak discharge in watersheds up to about 25 mi^2.

*Related:* NRCS curve number, time of concentration, peak discharge
*Sources:* NRCS, *Urban Hydrology for Small Watersheds*, TR-55, 2nd ed., 1986

### **Ten States Standards**

The *Recommended Standards for Wastewater Facilities* (and corresponding *Water Works* document) published by the Great Lakes - Upper Mississippi River Board of State and Provincial Public Health and Environmental Managers. Widely adopted minimum standards for sanitary sewer and water utility design in the U.S. midwest.

*Related:* AWWA M22
*Sources:* GLUMRB, *Recommended Standards for Wastewater Facilities*, 2014; *Recommended Standards for Water Works*, 2018; see [Sanitary sewer](engineering/sanitary-sewer/index.md)

### **AWWA M22**

American Water Works Association Manual M22, *Sizing Water Service Lines and Meters*. Reference for hydraulic design of service lines and meters in potable water systems.

*Related:* Ten States Standards, pipe friction coefficient
*Sources:* AWWA, *Manual M22*, 3rd ed., 2014; see [Water distribution](engineering/water-distribution/index.md)

### **Weir**

A barrier across a channel that water flows over, used for flow control or measurement. *Sharp-crested* and *broad-crested* are the principal types.

*Related:* sharp-crested weir, broad-crested weir, riser
*Sources:* Bos, *Discharge Measurement Structures*, 3rd ed., 1989

### **WQv**

Water Quality Volume. The runoff volume from the design "first flush" event (commonly the first 1 in or 1.5 in of rainfall) that a BMP must capture and treat.

*Related:* BMP, bioretention, low-flow orifice
*Sources:* US EPA, *National Menu of Stormwater BMPs*; CWP, *Stormwater Manager's Resource Center*

### **PROWAG**

Public Right-of-Way Accessibility Guidelines. The U.S. Access Board's binding standards (final rule 2023) for accessibility of pedestrian facilities in the public right-of-way: cross slopes, ramp slopes, detectable warnings, etc.

*Related:* sight distance, right-of-way
*Sources:* U.S. Access Board, *PROWAG Final Rule*, 2023; see [ADA and accessibility](engineering/ada-and-accessibility/index.md)

## Coordinate systems and geodesy

### **NAD27**

North American Datum of 1927. A horizontal datum using the Clarke 1866 ellipsoid, fixed at Meades Ranch, Kansas. Superseded by NAD83 but still encountered in old plats and deeds.

*Related:* NAD83, datum, projection
*Sources:* NGS, *Geodetic Glossary*; see [Coordinate systems](field-and-boundary/coordinate-systems/index.md)

### **NAD83**

North American Datum of 1983. A horizontal/3D datum using the GRS80 ellipsoid, the basis of state plane and most modern U.S. mapping. Realized through several adjustments: NAD83(1986), (HARN), (CORS96), (NSRS2007), (2011).

*Related:* NAD83(2011) epoch 2010.0, NSRS, state plane, geoid
*Sources:* NGS, *NAD83 documentation*; see [Coordinate systems](field-and-boundary/coordinate-systems/index.md)

### **NAD83(2011) epoch 2010.0**

The current realization of NAD83 in the conterminous U.S., aligned to the 2010.0 epoch using CORS data through 2011. Most modern Indiana survey work is referenced to NAD83(2011).

*Related:* NAD83, NSRS, CORS
*Sources:* NGS, *NA2011 Adjustment of the NSRS*, 2012

### **NAVD88**

North American Vertical Datum of 1988. The current orthometric height datum for CONUS, fixed to a single benchmark in Quebec. To be replaced by NAPGD2022.

*Related:* NGVD29, NAPGD2022, geoid model, level loop
*Sources:* NGS, *NAVD88 documentation*

### **NGVD29**

National Geodetic Vertical Datum of 1929. Predecessor to NAVD88; widely encountered in older drawings and FEMA flood maps. Conversion to NAVD88 varies by location (typically -1.0 to +1.0 ft in Indiana).

*Related:* NAVD88, NGS
*Sources:* NGS, [VERTCON](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/TOOLS/Vertcon/vertcon.html)

### **NATRF2022**

North American Terrestrial Reference Frame of 2022. The plate-fixed (North American plate) successor to NAD83 in the modernized NSRS. Three additional plate-fixed frames will cover Pacific, Caribbean, and Mariana plates.

*Related:* NSRS 2022, NAPGD2022, NAD83
*Sources:* NGS, *Blueprint for the Modernized NSRS*

### **NAPGD2022**

North American-Pacific Geopotential Datum of 2022. The geopotential (height) component of the modernized NSRS, replacing NAVD88 in CONUS, Alaska, Hawaii, and U.S. territories.

*Related:* NSRS 2022, NAVD88, geoid model
*Sources:* NGS, *Blueprint for the Modernized NSRS*

### **State plane**

A U.S. national family of map projections, established for state-by-state high-accuracy mapping. Each state has one or more zones using Lambert Conformal Conic (east-west zones) or Transverse Mercator (north-south zones); Indiana has East and West zones.

*Related:* projection, NAD83, combined scale factor, ground-to-grid
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*; see [Coordinate systems](field-and-boundary/coordinate-systems/index.md)

### **Combined scale factor (CSF)**

The product of the grid scale factor (projection effect) and the elevation factor (ellipsoid-to-ground reduction): CSF = k_proj x (R / (R + h)). Multiplying ground distance by CSF yields grid distance.

*Related:* grid factor, ground-to-grid, state plane
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **Grid factor**

The projection scale factor (k_proj) at a given location, due to the conformal projection's distortion. Less than 1 along the standard parallels/secant lines, greater than 1 elsewhere.

*Related:* combined scale factor, state plane, projection
*Sources:* Snyder, *Map Projections - A Working Manual*, USGS PP 1395

### **Ground-to-grid**

The conversion from project (ground) distances and coordinates to state-plane (grid) values, typically by multiplying by a CSF and applying a translation. *Grid-to-ground* is the inverse.

*Related:* combined scale factor, localization, state plane
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

## Additional surveying terms

### **Accretion**

The gradual, imperceptible addition of soil to riparian or littoral land by water action. New land belongs to the upland owner; the boundary moves with the water.

*Related:* avulsion, reliction, riparian, littoral, meander line
*Sources:* *Nebraska v. Iowa*, 143 U.S. 359 (1892); see [Advanced Boundary Topics](field-and-boundary/advanced-boundary/index.md)

### **Astronomic observation**

A determination of azimuth, latitude, or longitude by sighting celestial bodies (sun or stars) using a theodolite. Largely supplanted by GNSS but still used to verify gyro-theodolites and as a check on geodetic azimuths.

*Related:* azimuth, basis of bearings, geodetic survey
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 19

### **Bowditch rule**

Synonym for the *compass rule*; a traverse-adjustment method distributing misclosure proportional to course length, named for Nathaniel Bowditch.

*Related:* compass rule, transit rule, traverse
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed., Ch. 8

### **Bubble level**

A vial containing fluid and an air bubble, used to indicate level (plate level) or plumb (circular bubble) on survey instruments. Periodically tested and adjusted via the peg or two-peg test.

*Related:* collimation, plumb, total station
*Sources:* Manufacturer field manuals; Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 4

### **Cadastral survey**

A survey that establishes or retraces property boundaries, especially the federal PLSS subdivision of the public domain. The BLM Cadastral Survey program is the federal authority.

*Related:* PLSS, BLM Manual, retracement
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009

### **Centerline (CL)**

A line representing the geometric or designed center of a road, channel, or other linear feature. In Civil 3D, an alignment usually represents a centerline.

*Related:* alignment, baseline (corridor)
*Sources:* AASHTO Green Book, 7th ed., 2018; see [Alignments](civil3d/alignments/index.md)

### **Chord**

A straight line connecting two points on a curve. The *long chord* of a horizontal curve connects the PC and PT; chord definition curves use the chord, not the arc, as the unit of measurement.

*Related:* horizontal curve, alignment, deflection
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 24

### **Construction staking**

The process of marking horizontal and vertical positions on the ground for the contractor to build to design. Includes layout, offsets, cut sheets, and grade stakes.

*Related:* as-built, robotic total station
*Sources:* see [Construction staking](field-and-boundary/construction-staking/index.md)

### **Control network**

A set of horizontal and vertical control points whose coordinates are established (and adjusted) to a stated accuracy and used as the basis for a project's geospatial work.

*Related:* least-squares adjustment, traverse, level loop, OPUS-Projects
*Sources:* FGCS *Standards and Specifications*, 1984; see [Control Networks](field-and-boundary/control-networks/index.md)

### **Cross-section**

A vertical slice through a feature (corridor, channel, structure) showing its profile transverse to the direction of travel or flow. In Civil 3D, sample-line groups generate cross-sections from corridors.

*Related:* corridor, assembly, profile view
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Sample Lines*

### **Deflection angle**

The angle at a survey station between the extension of the back course and the foreward course, signed left or right. Sum of deflections in a closed traverse equals 360 deg.

*Related:* traverse, bearing, alignment
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 9

### **Differential leveling**

A leveling method that determines elevation differences between two points by combining backsight and foresight rod readings on intervening turning points. The standard for high-precision vertical control.

*Related:* benchmark, level loop, instrument height, NAVD88
*Sources:* NGS, *Geodetic Leveling*, NOAA Manual NOS NGS 3, 1981

### **Direct/reverse (face left/face right)**

Observing a horizontal angle in two telescope orientations (D/R or FL/FR) and meaning them, eliminating most systematic instrument errors. Standard for high-precision angle work.

*Related:* total station, collimation
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 8

### **Eccentricity**

In a total station setup, the offset of the instrument's vertical axis from the centering point (e.g., due to plumbing error or use of an offset prism). Causes systematic angle and distance errors.

*Related:* collimation, total station
*Sources:* Manufacturer documentation; FGCS *Standards*, 1984

### **GNSS**

Global Navigation Satellite System. The umbrella term for satellite positioning systems: GPS (US), GLONASS (Russia), Galileo (EU), BeiDou (China), plus regional augmentations.

*Related:* RTK, RTN, OPUS, base station
*Sources:* NGS overview of GNSS; see [Survey Equipment](field-and-boundary/survey-equipment/index.md)

### **GPS**

Global Positioning System. The U.S. Department of Defense GNSS constellation, comprising 24+ satellites in medium Earth orbit broadcasting code and carrier signals on multiple frequencies.

*Related:* GNSS, RTK, OPUS
*Sources:* US Space Force, *gps.gov*; ICD-GPS-200

### **Gyro-theodolite**

A theodolite combined with a gyroscope that determines true (geographic) north independent of GNSS or stars. Used for tunnel and underground orientation.

*Related:* azimuth, astronomic observation
*Sources:* DMT *Gyromat* documentation; ASCE *Manual on Underground Survey*

### **HARN**

High Accuracy Reference Network. A 1990s-era state-by-state densification of NAD83, superseded by NAD83(NSRS2007) and (2011) but still found in older datasets.

*Related:* NAD83, NSRS, CORS
*Sources:* NGS, *HARN documentation*

### **Heads-up GNSS**

Informal term for using a GNSS receiver while watching live coordinates and quality flags on a data collector, as opposed to logging blind for post-processing.

*Related:* RTK, RTN, fix/float, rover
*Sources:* common surveying usage; manufacturer field guides

### **Height of instrument**

Synonym for instrument height (HI) in leveling, or the measured vertical distance from a setup point to the trunnion axis of a total station.

*Related:* instrument height, total station
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 5

### **Horizontal control**

The set of monumented points whose horizontal coordinates (typically state plane) are established for use as the planimetric basis of a survey or project.

*Related:* vertical control, control network, state plane
*Sources:* FGCS *Standards*, 1984

### **Horizontal curve**

A circular (or compound, reverse, or spiral) arc connecting two tangent lines in plan. Quantified by radius R, deflection delta, length L, and tangent T.

*Related:* alignment, chord, spiral, sight distance
*Sources:* AASHTO Green Book, 7th ed., 2018, Ch. 3

### **IMU**

Inertial Measurement Unit. A package of accelerometers and gyroscopes that measures acceleration and angular rate. Combined with GNSS in mobile mapping and UAV positioning.

*Related:* MMS, GNSS, RTK
*Sources:* Groves, *Principles of GNSS, Inertial, and Multisensor Integrated Navigation Systems*, 2nd ed., 2013

### **Inverse**

In COGO, the computation of bearing and distance between two known points (the inverse of "traverse," which computes a new point from a known one and a course).

*Related:* COGO point, latitude/departure, traverse
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 10

### **Lidar**

Light Detection And Ranging. A remote-sensing technology using laser pulses to measure distance, producing dense 3D point clouds. Deployed terrestrial (TLS), mobile (MLS), and airborne (ALS).

*Related:* MMS, ASPRS, surface (TIN/grid)
*Sources:* ASPRS, *Manual of Airborne Topographic Lidar*, 2012; *LAS Specification* v1.4

### **Magnetic declination**

The angle between magnetic north and true (geographic) north at a location, varying with location and time. Old deeds with magnetic bearings require declination correction to compare with modern bearings.

*Related:* basis of bearings, retracement
*Sources:* NOAA NCEI, *World Magnetic Model*; *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Mean sea level (MSL)**

The long-term average elevation of the sea surface, historically used as a vertical datum (e.g., NGVD29 was sometimes called *MSL Datum of 1929*). Modern orthometric datums (NAVD88) are no longer tied directly to MSL.

*Related:* NGVD29, NAVD88, geoid
*Sources:* NGS, *Geodetic Glossary*

### **Monument**

A physical marker (iron pin, capped pipe, brass disk, stone) set or recovered to mark a survey point, especially a property corner. Original monuments control retracements over later measurements.

*Related:* original survey, retracement, surveyor's report
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009; see [Monuments and Evidence](field-and-boundary/monuments-and-evidence/index.md)

### **Nadir**

The point on the celestial sphere directly below the observer (opposite the zenith). Used in calibration of plummet optics and for some scanning workflows.

*Related:* zenith angle, plumb
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 19

### **Nail and shiner**

A common monument: a nail driven into pavement with a metal washer ("shiner") stamped or punched with the surveyor's identifier. Suitable for non-corner control points; not durable as a property corner.

*Related:* monument, control network
*Sources:* common surveying practice; 865 IAC 1-12

### **Network adjustment**

The least-squares (or other) adjustment of a connected set of GNSS, terrestrial, or leveling observations to produce best-fit coordinates with associated statistics.

*Related:* least-squares adjustment, control network, OPUS-Projects
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed.; see [Control Networks](field-and-boundary/control-networks/index.md)

### **OHWM**

Ordinary High Water Mark. The line on the bank where the presence and action of water have left a distinct mark (vegetation change, scour, debris). Often the legal boundary of a navigable waterbody.

*Related:* riparian, littoral, meander line, accretion
*Sources:* 33 CFR 328.3(c); Cole, *Water Boundaries*, 1997 ed.

### **PI / PVI**

*Point of Intersection* (horizontal alignment) or *Point of Vertical Intersection* (profile). The theoretical apex where two tangents meet; alignment/profile geometry is dimensioned from it.

*Related:* alignment, layout profile, vertical curve, horizontal curve
*Sources:* AASHTO Green Book, 7th ed., 2018, Ch. 3

### **Plat**

A drawing of record showing the boundary of a parcel or subdivision, with monuments, dimensions, and certifications, recorded with the local recorder.

*Related:* surveyor's report, metes and bounds, original survey
*Sources:* Ind. Code 36-7-3 (subdivision platting); see [Boundary and ALTA](field-and-boundary/boundary-and-alta/index.md)

### **Precision vs accuracy**

*Precision* is the degree of repeatability of measurements; *accuracy* is the closeness of a measurement to its true value. A traverse can be precise but inaccurate (e.g., a tight loop on the wrong control).

*Related:* least-squares adjustment, control network
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed., Ch. 1

### **Random error**

An observation error due to many small, independent causes; assumed normally distributed and addressed by averaging and least-squares adjustment.

*Related:* systematic error, blunder, least-squares adjustment
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed., Ch. 2

### **Rod (level rod)**

A graduated staff held vertically on a benchmark or turning point and read through the level's telescope. Built from wood, fiberglass, or invar (high precision); modern digital levels read bar-coded rods.

*Related:* differential leveling, benchmark
*Sources:* Wolf and Ghilani, *Elementary Surveying*, Ch. 4

### **Setup error**

The combined effect of centering, leveling, and target-height errors at a single instrument station. Decreases with longer sights for the angular component.

*Related:* total station, collimation, eccentricity
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed., Ch. 6

### **Spiral curve (clothoid)**

A transition curve between a tangent and a circular curve (or between two circular curves of different radius) whose curvature varies linearly with arc length. Used for superelevation transitions on high-speed roads.

*Related:* horizontal curve, alignment, sight distance
*Sources:* AASHTO Green Book, 7th ed., 2018, Ch. 3

### **Static GNSS**

A GNSS observation method in which the receiver is set up over a point and logs data continuously for a planned session length, processed differentially against base or CORS stations.

*Related:* OPUS, OPUS-Projects, base station
*Sources:* NGS *Guidelines for Establishing GPS-Derived Heights*, 2008

### **Systematic error**

An observation error due to a consistent cause (e.g., uncorrected EDM scale, non-vertical rod), removable by calibration or by observing in pairs (FL/FR, paired backsights).

*Related:* random error, blunder, collimation
*Sources:* Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*, 6th ed., Ch. 2

### **Terrestrial laser scanning (TLS)**

Tripod-mounted lidar that captures dense point clouds of static scenes (buildings, infrastructure, terrain). Each scan is georeferenced via targets or cloud-to-cloud registration.

*Related:* lidar, MMS
*Sources:* ASPRS, *Manual of Mobile Mapping Technology*, 2018

### **Three-wire leveling**

A precise leveling method reading the upper, center, and lower stadia hairs at every backsight and foresight. The mean reduces random error and the spread provides a precision check.

*Related:* differential leveling, NAVD88
*Sources:* NGS, *Geodetic Leveling*, NOAA Manual NOS NGS 3, 1981

### **UAV/UAS photogrammetry**

Use of small uncrewed aircraft systems with cameras and (optionally) RTK GNSS to capture imagery for photogrammetric reconstruction of surfaces, orthomosaics, and 3D models.

*Related:* lidar, IMU, MMS, surface (TIN/grid)
*Sources:* ASPRS *Positional Accuracy Standards*, Ed. 2, 2023; FAA Part 107

### **Vertical control**

The set of monumented points whose elevations are established for use as the basis of project elevations.

*Related:* horizontal control, NAVD88, level loop, benchmark
*Sources:* FGCS *Standards*, 1984

### **VRS**

Virtual Reference Station. An RTN technique that synthesizes corrections for a non-existent base near the rover, transmitted via NTRIP. Used in many state RTNs.

*Related:* RTN, RTK, NTRIP, CORS
*Sources:* RTCM 10410.1; Trimble VRS Now documentation

### **Catchment**

### **Code set style**

### **Country kit**

### **Dynamic block**

An AutoCAD block containing parameters and actions for in-drawing manipulation. Common for North arrows, scale bars, and title-block fields.

### **External reference (xref)**

A file referenced into a host drawing without being inserted as a block. Used to share base mapping, corridor surfaces, and annotation between drawings without duplication.

*Related:* data shortcut
*Sources:* Autodesk, *AutoCAD External References*

### **Mass haul diagram**

A graph along an alignment showing cumulative cut-fill volume against station; used to plan earthwork and identify balance points and overhaul.

*Related:* corridor, surface (TIN/grid), earthwork
*Sources:* AASHTO, *Roadside Design Guide*; INDOT *Design Manual* Ch. 50

### **Object enabler**

A free Autodesk add-on that lets non-Civil 3D AutoCAD installations display and plot Civil 3D custom objects with full graphics.

*Related:* civil view, sheet set
*Sources:* Autodesk, *Civil 3D Object Enabler*

### **Object viewer**

A Civil 3D dialog that shows a selected object in an interactive 3D viewport without changing the drawing's current view; useful for inspecting corridors, surfaces, and pipe networks.

### **Property set**

An object property bag (defined via Style Manager or AECOM tools) attaching custom data to AutoCAD or Civil 3D objects, often used for tagging and scheduling.

*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Quantity Takeoff*

### **Surface analysis**

Built-in Civil 3D surface analyses including elevation banding, slope, slope arrows, contours, watershed, and water drop. Drives thematic surface displays.

*Related:* surface (TIN/grid), label style
*Sources:* Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *Surface Analyses*

## Additional boundary and legal terms

### **Calls (in a description)**

The bearings, distances, monuments, and adjoiners cited in a deed description. *Senior calls* (monuments, adjoiners) generally control over *junior calls* (course-and-distance) when in conflict.

*Related:* metes and bounds, monument, retracement
*Sources:* *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Chain of title**

The sequence of recorded conveyances of a parcel back to the sovereign or other root of title. A title examiner traces the chain and identifies exceptions, encumbrances, and clouds.

*Related:* original survey, color of title
*Sources:* *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Conflict resolution (priority of calls)**

In legal-description interpretation, the typical priority is: (1) senior rights, (2) intent, (3) calls for a survey, (4) monuments (natural then artificial), (5) bearings, (6) distances, (7) area. Local case law modifies the order.

*Related:* calls, retracement, junior/senior right
*Sources:* *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.; Robillard et al., *Evidence and Procedures*, 6th ed.

### **Dedication**

The voluntary transfer of an interest in land (typically right-of-way) from a private owner to a public agency, by deed or by recorded plat.

*Related:* right-of-way, plat
*Sources:* *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Easement by necessity**

An implied easement granted when a parcel is landlocked due to a conveyance that cut off access. Recognized in Indiana under common law.

*Related:* easement, right-of-way
*Sources:* *Whitt v. Ferris*, 596 N.E.2d 230 (Ind. Ct. App. 1992); see [Easements and ROW](field-and-boundary/easements-and-row/index.md)

### **Estoppel (boundary by)**

A doctrine preventing a party from disputing a boundary line they have, by conduct or representation, induced an adjoining owner to rely on.

*Related:* acquiescence, practical location
*Sources:* *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Fee simple**

The most complete ownership interest in real property, inheritable and free of conditions (apart from easements, governmental powers, and liens).

*Related:* easement, dedication
*Sources:* Restatement (First) of Property; *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Lost corner**

A PLSS corner whose original location cannot be recovered by competent evidence and must be restored by proportional measurement.

*Related:* obliterated corner, proportioning, BLM Manual
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 7

### **Obliterated corner**

A PLSS corner whose original monument is gone but whose location can still be recovered from collateral evidence (witness trees, fences, occupation, recorded ties). Distinct from *lost*.

*Related:* lost corner, proportioning, monument
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 7

### **Quitclaim deed**

A conveyance that transfers whatever interest the grantor has, without warranty. Common in boundary-line agreements and to clean up title clouds.

*Related:* fee simple, chain of title
*Sources:* *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Riparian boundary**

The boundary of a parcel that abuts a flowing watercourse, typically the ordinary high water mark or the thread of the stream depending on jurisdiction and navigability.

*Related:* riparian, OHWM, meander line
*Sources:* Cole, *Water Boundaries*, 1997 ed.

### **Subdivision plat**

A recorded plat that divides a parent parcel into lots and blocks for sale, with required monuments, easements, and dedications. Subject to local plat-approval ordinances and state statutes.

*Related:* plat, dedication, easement
*Sources:* Ind. Code 36-7-3; local subdivision control ordinances

### **Witness corner**

A reference monument set near, but not on, an inaccessible corner (e.g., in water, on a rock face). Its tie to the true corner is recorded so the corner can be reproduced.

*Related:* monument, original survey, lost corner
*Sources:* BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009, Ch. 4

## Additional engineering, drainage, and standards

### **Bypass capacity**

In stormwater inlet design, the flow that bypasses an inlet during a design event and continues to the next downstream inlet. Required for grate inlets in continuous gutters.

*Related:* peak discharge, hydraulic radius
*Sources:* FHWA HEC-22, 3rd ed., 2009

### **Critical depth**

The depth at which the specific energy of an open-channel flow is minimum for a given discharge. Flow depth equals critical depth at hydraulic structures such as broad-crested weirs and unsubmerged culvert outlets.

*Related:* HGL, broad-crested weir, headwater
*Sources:* Chow, *Open-Channel Hydraulics*, 1959, Ch. 3

### **Culvert**

A buried conduit that carries flow under a road or other obstruction. Hydraulic design considers inlet control vs outlet control, headwater depth, and outlet velocity.

*Related:* headwater, tailwater
*Sources:* FHWA HDS-5, *Hydraulic Design of Highway Culverts*, 3rd ed., 2012

### **Curb and gutter**

A combined curb (raised concrete edge) and gutter (depressed concrete swale) along a roadway edge. Geometry (height, width, slope) is set by jurisdiction; common section is INDOT Curb-and-Gutter Type II.

*Related:* runoff coefficient, peak discharge
*Sources:* INDOT *Standard Drawings* (current); AASHTO Green Book

### **Design storm**

The rainfall event used as the basis for hydraulic and drainage design, defined by return period (e.g., 10-year) and duration. Local jurisdictions specify which design storms govern which structures.

*Related:* peak discharge, NRCS curve number, TR-55
*Sources:* NOAA Atlas 14 precipitation frequency; INDOT *Design Manual* Ch. 30

### **Detention time**

The average residence time of water in a basin, computed as storage volume divided by outflow rate at a given stage. Drives water-quality treatment.

*Related:* detention, retention, WQv
*Sources:* US EPA, *Preliminary Data Summary of Urban Stormwater BMPs*, 1999

### **Erosion control blanket (ECB)**

A roll product (straw, wood, or coconut fiber) anchored to a slope or channel to protect bare soil while vegetation establishes. Selected by slope, flow conditions, and required service life.

*Related:* SWPPP, EPA CGP
*Sources:* AASHTO M 288 (geotextile classes); ECTC standards; see [Erosion and Sediment](engineering/erosion-and-sediment/index.md)

### **Hydrograph**

A plot of discharge (or stage) versus time at a point. Methods of generating include unit hydrograph, NRCS dimensionless, kinematic wave, and modeled (HEC-HMS, SWMM).

*Related:* peak discharge, time of concentration, NRCS curve number
*Sources:* NRCS *National Engineering Handbook* Part 630, Ch. 16

### **Inlet (drainage)**

A surface opening (curb inlet, grate inlet, combination, slotted) that captures stormwater from the ground surface and conveys it to a pipe network. Sizing per HEC-22.

*Related:* pipe network, peak discharge, bypass capacity
*Sources:* FHWA HEC-22, 3rd ed., 2009

### **Manhole**

A vertical access structure on a sanitary or storm sewer, typically precast concrete with a cast-iron frame and cover. Drop, transition, and junction variants exist.

*Related:* structure, pipe network, invert
*Sources:* Ten States Standards (sewer); INDOT *Standard Drawings*

### **Minimum slope (sewer)**

The minimum pipe slope required to maintain a target self-cleansing velocity (commonly 2 ft/s for sanitary sewers). Tabulated by pipe size.

*Related:* Manning's n, hydraulic radius, Ten States Standards
*Sources:* GLUMRB, *Recommended Standards for Wastewater Facilities*, 2014

### **Outlet structure**

A structure (riser, weir, orifice, or composite) that controls the discharge from a detention/retention basin to the receiving system.

*Related:* riser, low-flow orifice, weir
*Sources:* USDA NRCS *Pond*, Ag Handbook 590

### **Pavement design**

The process of selecting layer thicknesses and materials for a pavement based on traffic loading, subgrade strength, climate, and reliability. AASHTO 1993 and AASHTOWare Pavement ME are the dominant methods.

*Related:* AASHTO Green Book
*Sources:* AASHTO, *Guide for Design of Pavement Structures*, 1993; AASHTOWare Pavement ME documentation

### **Pre-development / post-development**

The condition of a site before and after the proposed project. Stormwater regulations typically require post-development peak discharge to not exceed pre-development for one or more design storms.

*Related:* peak discharge, detention, NRCS curve number
*Sources:* US EPA NPDES Phase II; IDEM Rule 5

### **Sheet flow**

Shallow, broad runoff over a planar surface (typically less than 100 ft of travel before becoming shallow concentrated). The first segment of TR-55 time-of-concentration analysis.

*Related:* time of concentration, NRCS curve number
*Sources:* NRCS TR-55, 1986, Ch. 3

### **SWPPP**

Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan. The site-specific document required by the EPA CGP and IDEM Rule 5 detailing erosion-and-sediment controls, BMPs, inspection schedule, and pollutant-source controls.

*Related:* EPA CGP, MS4, BMP
*Sources:* US EPA, *Developing Your Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan*, 2007; 327 IAC 15-5

### **Trench detail**

A standard cross-section for buried pipe installation specifying bedding, haunching, initial backfill, final backfill, and (optionally) compaction requirements. Material- and pipe-specific.

*Related:* pipe network, INDOT IDM
*Sources:* INDOT *Standard Specifications* Section 715

### **Velocity (self-cleansing)**

The minimum flow velocity in a sanitary or storm sewer required to keep solids in suspension; typically 2 ft/s for sanitary sewers and 3 ft/s for storm sewers.

*Related:* minimum slope, Manning's n
*Sources:* GLUMRB, *Recommended Standards for Wastewater Facilities*, 2014

## Additional coordinate systems and geodesy

### **Convergence (angle)**

The angle between grid north and geodetic north at a point on a state-plane projection. Required to convert between geodetic and grid azimuths.

*Related:* state plane, grid factor
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **Elevation factor**

The factor that scales a ground distance to its equivalent at the ellipsoid: R / (R + h), where R is mean Earth radius and h is the orthometric+geoid height. Component of CSF.

*Related:* combined scale factor, grid factor, ellipsoid
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **Epoch**

The reference time for a coordinate set, accounting for plate motion (e.g., NAD83(2011) epoch 2010.0). Critical when integrating modern GNSS data with ITRF/IGS frames.

*Related:* NAD83(2011) epoch 2010.0, NSRS 2022, NATRF2022
*Sources:* NGS, *Time-Dependent Positioning Tool (HTDP)*

### **Grid azimuth**

The azimuth measured from grid north, the direction parallel to the central meridian of a state-plane (or other) projection. Differs from geodetic azimuth by the convergence angle.

*Related:* convergence (angle), state plane, azimuth
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **Grid coordinates**

The northing and easting coordinates resulting from projection of geodetic coordinates to a plane (e.g., state plane northing, easting). Distinct from geographic (latitude, longitude) and ellipsoidal (X,Y,Z) coordinates.

*Related:* state plane, projection, NSRS
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **Grid-to-ground**

The conversion from state-plane (grid) values to project (ground) distances and coordinates by dividing by the CSF and (optionally) translating. Inverse of *ground-to-grid*.

*Related:* combined scale factor, ground-to-grid, localization
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **Helmert transformation (7-parameter)**

A similarity transformation between two 3D coordinate systems with three translations, three rotations, and a scale factor. Used to relate ITRF realizations and historical datums.

*Related:* datum, NSRS, NAD83
*Sources:* IERS Conventions (2010); NGS *HTDP* documentation

### **HTDP**

NGS's *Horizontal Time-Dependent Positioning* tool, which transforms coordinates between ITRF/NAD83 realizations and epochs, accounting for plate motion and post-seismic deformation.

*Related:* epoch, NAD83, NATRF2022, NSRS
*Sources:* NGS, [HTDP](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/TOOLS/Htdp/Htdp.shtml)

### **InCORS**

Indiana's continuously operating reference station network operated by INDOT. Provides RTK corrections via NTRIP and CORS data for OPUS-style post-processing within Indiana.

*Related:* CORS, RTN, NTRIP
*Sources:* INDOT InCORS program; see [Coordinate systems](field-and-boundary/coordinate-systems/index.md)

### **ITRF / IGS**

International Terrestrial Reference Frame (and the IGS realization). The global, plate-tectonic-aware reference frame against which GNSS satellite orbits and global geodesy are computed. NAD83 differs from ITRF by a 14-parameter transformation.

*Related:* NSRS, epoch, HTDP
*Sources:* IERS Conventions (2010); IGS, *igs.org*

### **Mean Earth radius**

A representative Earth radius (commonly 6,371,000 m or 20,906,000 ft) used in elevation-factor and curvature-correction formulas. Not a true constant; chosen for the local latitude and direction.

*Related:* elevation factor, curvature correction
*Sources:* Snyder, *Map Projections*, USGS PP 1395

### **State plane (Indiana East)**

Indiana East zone of the SPCS, FIPS zone 1301. Lambert Conformal Conic projection covering the eastern half of Indiana including Marion, Hamilton, Hancock, Shelby, Madison, and adjacent counties.

*Related:* state plane, projection, NAD83
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **State plane (Indiana West)**

Indiana West zone of the SPCS, FIPS zone 1302. Lambert Conformal Conic projection covering the western half of Indiana including Hendricks, Boone, Morgan, Putnam, and adjacent counties.

*Related:* state plane, projection, NAD83
*Sources:* NGS, *State Plane Coordinate System Manual*

### **VRS3-Net**

A specific RTN technology used in some commercial networks, generating tailored RTK corrections per rover. Synonym for the broader VRS concept.

*Related:* VRS, RTN, NTRIP
*Sources:* Trimble, *VRS Now* documentation

### **Vertical datum transformation**

The conversion between vertical datums (e.g., NGVD29 to NAVD88, or NAVD88 to NAPGD2022) using a model file (e.g., NGS *VERTCON* and forthcoming *VDatum*).

*Related:* NAVD88, NGVD29, NAPGD2022, geoid model
*Sources:* NGS, [VERTCON](https://geodesy.noaa.gov/TOOLS/Vertcon/vertcon.html); NGS *VDatum*

## Additional reference

### **Acre**

A unit of area equal to 43,560 square feet, 4,046.86 square meters, or 1/640 of a square mile. Standard parcel-area unit in the U.S. The PLSS quarter-quarter section is nominally 40 acres.

*Related:* aliquot part, parcel area, section
*Sources:* NIST Handbook 44; BLM, *Manual of Surveying Instructions*, 2009

### **Annexation**

The legal process by which a municipality extends its boundaries to include adjacent unincorporated land. In Indiana, annexation procedures are governed by IC 36-4-3.

*Related:* dedication, right-of-way
*Sources:* Ind. Code 36-4-3; see [Indiana state](jurisdictions/indiana/state/index.md)

### **Appurtenance**

A right or improvement that runs with the land and passes with a conveyance (e.g., an appurtenant easement, a fence, a driveway). Distinct from personal (in gross) interests.

*Related:* easement, fee simple
*Sources:* Restatement (Third) of Property: Servitudes; *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Beam clearance**

In bridge or roadway-clearance design, the minimum vertical clearance between the underside of a structure and the top of the surface or vehicle lane below. Federal interstate minimum: 16 ft (FHWA).

*Related:* AASHTO Green Book, sight distance
*Sources:* FHWA, *Federal-Aid Policy Guide* 23 CFR 625; AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications

### **Concrete strength (f'c)**

The 28-day compressive strength of concrete, in psi (US) or MPa (SI), specified for structural and pavement applications. Civil 3D pipe-network parts list selects materials by class.

*Related:* parts list, structure
*Sources:* ACI 318 (current edition); INDOT *Standard Specifications* Section 702

### **Datum shift**

The systematic difference between two horizontal or vertical datums at a location (e.g., NAD27 to NAD83 horizontal shift averages 25-100 m in CONUS; NGVD29 to NAVD88 vertical shift varies by location).

*Related:* datum, vertical datum transformation, NAD27, NAD83
*Sources:* NGS, *NADCON 5.0*; NGS *VERTCON*

### **Floodplain**

Land adjacent to a watercourse subject to inundation by a stated flood event (commonly 1% annual chance, the "100-year" floodplain). Mapped by FEMA on Flood Insurance Rate Maps (FIRMs).

*Related:* OHWM, peak discharge
*Sources:* FEMA, *Flood Insurance Study* methodology; 44 CFR Part 65

### **Liquidated damages**

A pre-agreed sum specified in a construction contract as compensation for delay or non-performance, replacing actual damages. Common in INDOT contracts.

*Related:* construction staking, as-built
*Sources:* INDOT *Standard Specifications* Section 108

### **NSSDA**

National Standard for Spatial Data Accuracy. The FGDC-STD-007.3 method for reporting geospatial positional accuracy at the 95% confidence level using check-point comparisons. Largely superseded by ASPRS Ed. 2 for digital geospatial data.

*Related:* ASPRS, FGDC, lidar
*Sources:* FGDC-STD-007.3-1998 (NSSDA)

### **Permeable pavement**

A pavement type (porous asphalt, pervious concrete, permeable interlocking pavers) that allows stormwater to infiltrate through the surface, reducing runoff. Counts toward MS4 stormwater credits in many jurisdictions.

*Related:* infiltration, BMP, MS4
*Sources:* US EPA, *National Menu of Stormwater BMPs*; ACI 522R

### **Sub-foundation (geotechnical)**

The natural or compacted material beneath a structure or pavement. In pavement design, characterized by resilient modulus, CBR, or k-value, all of which influence the required pavement thickness for the design traffic loading.

*Related:* pavement design
*Sources:* AASHTO, *Guide for Design of Pavement Structures*, 1993; AASHTOWare Pavement ME documentation

### **Cut sheet**

A tabular construction-staking document listing each stake with station, offset, design elevation, ground elevation, and cut/fill, organized for the equipment operator. Generated from corridor cross-sections or grading models.

*Related:* construction staking, as-built, corridor
*Sources:* see [Construction staking](field-and-boundary/construction-staking/index.md)

### **Geotextile**

A permeable fabric (woven or non-woven) used in civil engineering for separation, filtration, reinforcement, drainage, or erosion control. Specified by AASHTO M 288 classes.

*Related:* erosion control blanket, trench detail
*Sources:* AASHTO M 288, *Standard Specification for Geotextile Specification for Highway Applications*; INDOT *Standard Specifications* Section 918

### **Stationing**

The cumulative distance along an alignment, conventionally written as 100-ft segments separated by a plus (e.g., 12+34.56 in U.S. customary). The basis for locating profiles, sections, structures, and labels along the alignment.

*Related:* alignment, profile view, plan-profile sheet
*Sources:* AASHTO Green Book, 7th ed., 2018; Autodesk Civil 3D Help, *About Stations*

### **Surveyor's plat**

The graphic record of a survey, distinct from a recorded subdivision plat, that depicts found monuments, set monuments, measurements, calls, and certifications. Filed with the surveyor's report under Indiana practice.

*Related:* surveyor's report, plat, monument
*Sources:* 865 IAC 1-12; *Brown's Boundary Control*, 7th ed.

### **Witness post**

A signed post or fence-mounted marker placed at or near a survey monument to make it findable. In Indiana, recommended where monuments are set in agricultural fields or shoulder areas.

*Related:* monument, witness corner
*Sources:* common surveying practice; 865 IAC 1-12

### **Xref overlay vs attach**

In AutoCAD, *attach* causes nested xrefs to load when the host drawing is opened by another drawing; *overlay* prevents nesting, useful for reference-only attachments such as base maps.

*Related:* external reference, data shortcut
*Sources:* Autodesk, *AutoCAD External References*

### **NCHRP**

National Cooperative Highway Research Program. Funded by AASHTO via the FHWA, NCHRP publishes research reports (e.g., *Report 350*, superseded by MASH) that drive national highway design and safety practice.

*Related:* AASHTO MASH, AASHTO RDG
*Sources:* TRB, [NCHRP](https://www.trb.org/NCHRP/NCHRP.aspx)

### **Resident project representative (RPR)**

The owner's on-site representative during construction, often a licensed engineer or surveyor, who observes work, reviews submittals, and documents progress. Distinct from the general contractor's site superintendent.

*Related:* as-built, construction staking
*Sources:* EJCDC C-700; common engineering practice
