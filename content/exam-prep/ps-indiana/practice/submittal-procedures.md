> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana boundary survey plats are typically filed with the county recorder under the recording statutes; subdivision plats follow the local plat acts (IC 36-7-3 series) plus county and municipal subdivision control ordinances.
> 2. The surveyor's seal, signature, and license number must appear on the plat. Plats without a current seal cannot be recorded.
> 3. Many counties (including Marion, Hamilton, and others) require plat review by the county surveyor's office before recording. Verify each county's procedure.
> 4. Section corner perpetuation records ("tie cards") are filed with the county surveyor; this is mandatory for any PLSS corner the surveyor establishes or perpetuates.

## Plat submittal flow (typical Indiana boundary survey)

1. Prepare the plat to 865 IAC 1-12-11 content requirements.
2. Apply seal, signature, and date.
3. Deliver to client; recording is at the client's option for a boundary survey (subdivision plats are mandatorily recorded per the plat statutes).
4. If a section corner was perpetuated, file a tie card with the county surveyor under IC 36-2-12.

## Subdivision plats

Subdivision plats follow a multi-step approval process under the Indiana plat acts and local subdivision control ordinances:

1. Primary (preliminary) plat approval by the local plan commission.
2. Construction of required improvements or posting of a bond.
3. Secondary (final) plat approval.
4. Recording with the county recorder.

Each county and municipality customizes details. For Marion County subdivisions, refer to the Indianapolis-Marion County Subdivision Control Ordinance. For Hamilton County municipalities, follow the relevant city or town code.

## Section corner tie cards

Under IC 36-2-12, the county surveyor maintains a perpetual record of PLSS section corners. A licensed surveyor who finds, sets, or perpetuates a section corner monument must file a tie card describing:

- The corner identified (section, township, range, type of corner).
- The monument found or set (type, dimensions, marks).
- Accessories (bearing trees, reference monuments) with measurements.
- Date, surveyor's name, license number, and seal.
