> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana monumentation rules are in 865 IAC 1-12-10. Minimum monument is iron, at least 1/2 in. diameter and at least 24 in. long. Cap with surveyor's license number is required.
> 2. Monuments must be set at every boundary corner, every angle point, and every PC/PT of curved boundaries. Where the true corner cannot be set (in a roadway, in water), a reference monument is set offset, and the offset is shown on the plat.
> 3. Existing qualifying monuments may be adopted — but the surveyor must evaluate whether they are original, in position, and undisturbed.
> 4. The cap stamping is the surveyor's identification; lost or illegible caps invalidate the monument's evidentiary value as an "identified" monument.

## What 865 IAC 1-12-10 requires

- Monument material: iron rod or pipe, at least 1/2 in. diameter, at least 24 in. long. Other materials (concrete monuments, brass discs in concrete) are permitted for specific applications.
- Identification: a cap with the surveyor's name or firm and license number. Plastic caps must be durable; aluminum or brass caps are preferred for permanent corners.
- Location: every corner, every angle point on the boundary, every PC and PT on a curved boundary. Reference monuments where true corner cannot be set.

## Reference monuments

When a true boundary corner falls in a paved road, watercourse, or other unsuitable location, the surveyor sets a reference monument on an extension line or perpendicular offset and reports:

- The bearing and distance from the reference monument to the true corner.
- The reasoning for not setting at the true corner.

## Adopting existing monuments

A surveyor may adopt an existing monument as the corner only after evaluating:

- Type and condition of the monument.
- Stamping or marks identifying who set it.
- Position relative to record dimensions, occupation, and adjoiner evidence.
- Whether the monument is original to the controlling survey.

If adopted, the plat describes the monument (type, size, cap stamping) and notes its origin if known.

## Plat reporting

The plat must show, for each boundary corner:

- Monument found, with description.
- Monument set, with description.
- Reference monuments and offsets.

The surveyor's report, when prepared, narrates the monumentation decisions.
