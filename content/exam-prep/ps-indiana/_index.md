> **TL;DR**
> 1. Indiana licensure requires the NCEES Fundamentals of Surveying (FS) exam, the Principles and Practice of Surveying (PS) exam, plus an Indiana state-specific component covering 865 IAC and Indiana boundary law.
> 2. This study track covers four areas: fundamentals (math, measurements, coordinates, error theory), legal principles (boundary law, PLSS, riparian, 865 IAC), practice (monumentation, recording, plat content), and a 50-question practice bank.
> 3. Use the spaced-repetition study UI at `/study` to drill the question bank. Cards interleave by due date using a simple SM-2 schedule and persist in your browser via localStorage.
> 4. Questions in this bank are original to this repository and cite the page slug used to write them. They are study aids only — the actual NCEES and Indiana exams are administered separately.

## Exam structure

The NCEES PS exam is an 8-hour computer-based test covering eight knowledge areas:

1. Project management and legal principles
2. Standards and specifications
3. Business concepts
4. Field data acquisition and reduction
5. Office data processing
6. Subdivision and development
7. Boundary, control, and topographic surveys
8. Survey design and procedures

Indiana adds a state-specific component covering 865 IAC (standards of practice), IC 25-21.5 (licensing statute), IC 36-2-12 (county surveyor), IC 32-21-7 (adverse possession), and Indiana case law on boundaries.
