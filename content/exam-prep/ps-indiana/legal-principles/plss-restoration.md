> **TL;DR**
> 1. The Public Land Survey System (PLSS) divides land into 6 mile by 6 mile townships, each containing 36 sections of nominally 1 square mile. Indiana is entirely within the PLSS.
> 2. Original corners control. A retracing surveyor uses, in order: existent corner (found undisturbed), obliterated corner (position recoverable from evidence), lost corner (position must be reconstructed).
> 3. Lost corner reconstruction methods: single proportionate measurement (for line corners and standard corners) and double proportionate measurement (for section corners on both a township and a range line).
> 4. Indiana county surveyors have statutory authority and duty under IC 36-2-12 to perpetuate PLSS corners.

## PLSS basics

- **Township.** A 6-mile-square area of 36 sections, identified by a township and range number.
- **Section.** Nominally 1 square mile (640 acres). Numbered 1 to 36 in a boustrophedon ("plowing") pattern starting in the NE corner.
- **Aliquot parts.** Sections subdivide into quarters (160 acres), quarter-quarters (40 acres), etc.

## Corner classifications (BLM Manual)

1. **Existent corner.** Original monument found undisturbed, identified by physical evidence and accessory descriptions in the original field notes (bearing trees, mounds, pits).
2. **Obliterated corner.** Original monument is gone, but the position is recoverable from collateral evidence: testimony, fences, occupation, lines of older surveyors, remnants of accessories.
3. **Lost corner.** Position cannot be recovered from any direct or collateral evidence. Must be reconstructed by proportionate measurement from the nearest controlling corners.

## Single proportionate measurement

Used for line corners (quarter corners, eighth corners along a single line), and for standard corners along a base line or standard parallel.

Procedure: measure the total distance between the two nearest existent controlling corners along the line. The lost corner is placed at the proportional record distance from each controlling corner.

## Double proportionate measurement

Used for interior section corners (where four sections meet) and for closing corners where two lines meet.

Procedure:
1. Measure the latitudinal distance between the controlling corners east and west of the lost corner.
2. Measure the longitudinal distance between the controlling corners north and south.
3. Compute a proportional east-west position and a proportional north-south position.
4. The lost corner is placed at the intersection of those proportions.

The BLM Manual gives the formulas; this is the only method authorized for federal PLSS retracement of interior section corners.

## Indiana county surveyor role

IC 36-2-12 establishes the county surveyor and assigns duties including:
- Maintaining a Section Corner Tie Card index for the county.
- Perpetuating original government corners by setting durable monuments and filing tie cards.
- Performing referee functions in boundary disputes when ordered by a court.

A licensed surveyor working in a PLSS-based subdivision must research the county surveyor's tie cards before retracing.
