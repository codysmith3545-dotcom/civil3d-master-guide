> **TL;DR**
> 1. 865 IAC 1-12 is the Indiana administrative code governing surveying practice. Every Indiana PS exam tests this material directly.
> 2. The key sections to know: 1-12-10 (monumentation), 1-12-11 (plat content), 1-12-12 (relative positional accuracy classes), 1-12-13 (record retention), and the definitions in 1-12-1.
> 3. The rule overlays federal PLSS practice and ALTA standards but does not replace them; Indiana surveyors must satisfy whichever standard is most stringent for the work at hand.

## Why this matters for the exam

The Indiana-specific component of the licensing exam asks rule-text questions. Memorize the numeric thresholds (monument dimensions, accuracy classes, record retention period) and the procedural requirements (surveyor's report, plat content, basis of bearings).

## Key sections

- **865 IAC 1-12-1.** Definitions. Know how the rule defines "original survey," "retracement survey," "route survey," "boundary survey," and "construction survey."
- **865 IAC 1-12-10.** Monumentation. Minimum monument dimensions (1/2 in. diameter, 24 in. length for iron). Cap required with license number. Reference monuments where the true corner cannot be set.
- **865 IAC 1-12-11.** Plat content. Required elements include legal description, bearings/distances, curve data, area, monuments found and set, basis of bearings, source citation, adjoiner names, improvements/encroachments/easements, north arrow, scale, dates, and surveyor's seal.
- **865 IAC 1-12-12.** Relative positional accuracy classes. Indiana classifies surveys by intended use (urban/suburban/rural/etc.) with associated minimum precision requirements.
- **865 IAC 1-12-13.** Record retention. Life of license plus 5 years. Records sufficient for retracement.

## Relationship to IC 25-21.5

The licensing statute (IC 25-21.5) is the legislative authority. It defines who must be licensed, the licensing board (Indiana Board of Registration for Professional Surveyors), the categories of conduct subject to discipline, and penalties. 865 IAC is the regulatory output of the Board under that statutory authority.
