> **TL;DR**
> 1. Table A is the optional-scope menu attached to the 2021 ALTA/NSPS standard. Pick the items the client actually needs and price accordingly.
> 2. Items 1–4 are nearly always selected (monuments, address, flood zone, area). Items 11 (utilities), 16 (recent earth moving), 17 (proposed changes), and 19 (offsite easements) are the most negotiated.
> 3. Anything you certify to in Table A must be reflected on the plat with sources and notes.

## How to use this page

The list below is a plain-English summary for scoping calls and proposals. **It is not the official text** — for binding wording, refer to Table A in the 2021 standard published by NSPS. Items are paraphrased and consolidated; a few items have multiple sub-options that you will negotiate (for example, item 6's zoning options). When in doubt, read the standard.

## Table A items, summarized

- **Item 1 — Monuments at corners.** Set or reference durable monuments at all major corners that are not already monumented. The standard prescribes minimum monument size and identification.
- **Item 2 — Site address.** Show the site street address as posted or as supplied by the client; cite the source.
- **Item 3 — Flood zone classification.** Show the FEMA flood zone, panel number, and effective date based on the current FIRM.
- **Item 4 — Gross land area.** Show the gross land area in square feet and acres, computed to a sensible precision. Net area (after right-of-way takings) is a common add.
- **Item 5 — Vertical relief.** Contours at the interval specified by the client, with the benchmark, datum, and source of elevation cited.
- **Item 6 — Zoning.** Two sub-options. (a) Show zoning classification as supplied in writing by the client or the zoning report. (b) Show setbacks, height, and density restrictions from the same source. Surveyors do not interpret zoning; they report what the report says.
- **Item 7 — Building exterior.** (a) Exterior dimensions of buildings at ground level. (b) Square footage of exterior footprint. (c) Measured height of buildings.
- **Item 8 — Substantial features.** Locate substantial features observed in the process of conducting the fieldwork (parking, signs, etc.).
- **Item 9 — Parking.** Number and type of parking spaces, including handicap-accessible.
- **Item 10 — Party walls.** Determination of party walls based on observation only, not legal opinion.
- **Item 11 — Utilities.** Plot utilities serving the property based on (a) plans furnished by the client, plus (b) markings from a utility locate request, plus observed evidence. List the sources used. The standard expressly does not require subsurface investigation.
- **Item 12 — Governmental agencies.** Notice from governmental agencies regarding the property, when supplied.
- **Item 13 — Adjoiner names.** Names of adjoining owners according to current public records.
- **Item 14 — Distance to nearest intersection.** From a designated point on the property to the nearest intersecting street.
- **Item 15 — Rectangular surveying system.** Section, township, and range data, where applicable.
- **Item 16 — Recent earth moving, building construction, or building additions.** Observed evidence of same.
- **Item 17 — Proposed changes in street right-of-way.** As shown in records the client provides.
- **Item 18 — Wetlands delineation.** Locate wetland markers set by a qualified specialist (the surveyor does not delineate).
- **Item 19 — Offsite (appurtenant) easements.** Plot easements that benefit the surveyed parcel but lie offsite, when documents are furnished.
- **Item 20 — Professional liability insurance.** Furnish a certificate of insurance to the client.

(Some editions have included additional discrete items; verify against the current Table A.)

## Practical scoping notes

- Item 11 drives more re-quotes than any other line. Confirm whether the client expects only a private locate or a full design-grade subsurface utility engineering effort. The standard does not require SUE; that is a separate scope.
- Item 6 is a research-and-report item. Most surveyors require a written zoning report; do not opine.
- Item 18 requires a qualified wetlands specialist. Build a sub on the team or exclude.
- Items quoted but not addressable in the field (because the client never delivered the utility plans, the zoning report, or the wetlands flags) should be noted as "not provided" rather than dropped silently.
