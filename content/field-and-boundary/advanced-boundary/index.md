> **TL;DR**
> 1. This section covers boundary topics that go beyond standard retracement: water boundaries, adverse possession, acquiescence, common disputes, and PLSS corner restoration.
> 2. These topics arise less frequently in routine practice but carry high stakes when they do — getting them wrong can mean litigation, license complaints, or both.
> 3. Indiana-specific statutes and case law are cited where applicable. The surveyor documents the facts; the court decides the legal questions.

## Section contents

### Possession and use doctrines

- [Adverse possession](adverse-possession.md) — elements under Indiana law (IC 32-21-7), field evidence, and the surveyor's role.
- [Acquiescence and practical location](acquiescence-and-practical-location.md) — boundary by long-standing mutual recognition, and how it differs from adverse possession.

### Disputes

- [Common boundary disputes](common-boundary-disputes.md) — fence-line vs deed-line conflicts, encroachments, gaps and overlaps, and the surveyor as fact-finder.
