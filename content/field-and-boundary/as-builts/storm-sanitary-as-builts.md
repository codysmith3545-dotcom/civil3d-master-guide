> **TL;DR**
> 1. For every structure, measure the **rim elevation** (top of casting) and **all invert elevations** (inside bottom of each pipe) — inverts are the critical grade data for the reviewing authority.
> 2. Verify **pipe size and material** visually at every structure; do not copy from the plan. As-built means measured, not assumed.
> 3. Manhole access requires **confined-space awareness** — never enter a manhole for as-built measurements without proper training, equipment, and a standby person. Most invert measurements can be taken from the surface with a drop tape or level rod.

## Equipment

- GNSS RTK rover or total station for rim elevations and structure locations.
- Drop tape (weighted steel tape) for measuring depth from rim to invert.
- Telescoping level rod for reaching inverts from the surface in shallow structures.
- Flashlight or headlamp to see pipe inverts inside the structure.
- Mirror on a pole (optional) for viewing inverts in deep structures without entering.
- Tape measure for pipe diameters.
- Camera for documenting structure conditions.
- Manhole hook or lifter for opening lids.
- Traffic cones and high-visibility vest for work in roadways.

## Rim elevation

Shoot the top of the casting (rim) with GNSS or total station. Place the rod on the highest point of the casting ring. If the casting is damaged or uneven, note the condition and shoot the best representative point.

Record the rim elevation to 0.01 ft. If the structure has an adjustment ring or is not yet at final grade, note "rim not at final grade" — the reviewing authority needs to know.

## Measuring inverts

The invert is the inside bottom of the pipe where it enters or exits the structure. For each pipe in the structure, measure:

1. **Drop measurement.** Lower a weighted tape (drop tape) from the rim to the invert. The invert elevation = rim elevation minus drop distance. This is the most common method and does not require entering the structure.
2. **Direct rod reading.** On shallow structures (less than 5 to 6 ft deep), a level rod can reach the invert directly. Place the rod foot on the invert and read the elevation.
3. **Through-the-pipe measurement.** See [measuring buried pipes](measuring-buried-pipes.md) for post-backfill pipe measurement techniques.

Measure each invert separately. A structure may have 2, 3, or 4 pipes at different inverts. Record the direction of each pipe (N, S, E, W, or bearing) and the invert elevation.

**Invert measurement accuracy.** A drop tape measurement is typically accurate to 0.02 to 0.05 ft, depending on the tape condition, the observer's technique, and whether the bottom of the structure is clean. Silt, gravel, or debris in the invert can add 0.05 ft or more of error. Clear debris from the invert before measuring if accessible from the surface.

## Pipe size and material verification

At each structure, visually verify:

- **Pipe diameter.** Measure with a tape if accessible. For round pipe, measure the inside diameter. Compare to the plan.
- **Pipe material.** Identify visually: RCP (gray concrete with reinforcing wire visible), PVC (white or green plastic), HDPE (black corrugated or smooth-wall plastic), CMP (corrugated metal), DIP (ductile iron, dark).
- **Pipe shape.** Most are round; note if elliptical, arch, or box.

If the pipe size or material differs from the plan, record the actual condition. This is the point of an as-built.

## Pipe length and slope

Pipe length between structures can be measured by:

- **Horizontal distance between structure centers** (surveyed positions). This is the most common method for as-builts.
- **Tape through the pipe** (only practical on short, straight runs in shallow structures).
- **Computed from the plan** if direct measurement is not practical — but note that the length is plan-derived, not measured.

Pipe slope is computed: (upstream invert - downstream invert) / pipe length. Express as a percentage or in ft/ft. Verify that the slope is positive in the flow direction. A negative slope (pipe running uphill) is a construction defect on gravity systems and must be reported.

## Sanitary-specific items

- **Laterals.** Locate and measure the invert of each lateral connection. Note the lateral direction and size. If the lateral is plugged (not yet connected), note that.
- **Drop manholes.** Measure the incoming pipe invert, the drop pipe invert at the bottom of the structure, and the outgoing pipe invert. Note the drop pipe material and size.
- **Cleanouts.** Shoot the rim (top of cleanout casting) and the invert. Note the size.
- **Force main connections.** Note the connection point, pipe size, material, and any valve or blow-off.

## Storm-specific items

- **Inlets and catch basins.** Measure the rim (top of grate or casting), the invert of each pipe, and the sump depth (bottom of structure below the lowest invert).
- **Headwalls and outfalls.** Shoot the horizontal position, the invert, and the top of the headwall or endwall. Photograph the outlet condition.
- **Detention/retention connections.** Measure the invert where the storm system enters or leaves the detention facility. Verify the outlet control structure orifice size and invert.

## Safety considerations

### Confined space

A manhole is a **permit-required confined space** under OSHA 29 CFR 1910.146. Do not enter a manhole unless:

- You have completed confined-space entry training.
- The atmosphere has been tested (oxygen, combustible gases, hydrogen sulfide).
- A standby person is at the surface.
- Retrieval equipment (tripod, harness, winch) is in place.
- A confined-space entry permit is completed.

For as-built measurements, **entry is almost never necessary.** Drop tapes, mirrors, flashlights, and cameras on poles can capture all required data from the surface. If a pipe invert cannot be measured from the surface, document the limitation rather than entering the structure.

### Traffic

Many structures are in roadways. Follow local traffic-control requirements. At minimum: high-visibility vest, cones around the open manhole, and a spotter watching traffic. On high-speed or high-volume roads, a formal traffic-control plan with signs and flaggers may be required.

### Lifting lids

Manhole lids weigh 80 to 250 lb. Use a manhole hook or mechanical lifter. Lift with your legs. Set the lid down on a stable surface away from the opening. Never leave a manhole open and unattended.
