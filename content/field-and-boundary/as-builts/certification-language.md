> **TL;DR**
> 1. The as-built certification states what the surveyor **measured, when, and to what accuracy** — it does not certify that the construction complies with the design; that is the engineer's role.
> 2. Include **limitation language** that defines the scope of the certification, the date of measurement, and the fact that conditions may have changed since the survey.
> 3. In Indiana, as-built surveys that include certifications must bear the **seal and signature** of the licensed professional surveyor who supervised the work, per IC 25-21.5 and 865 IAC 1-12.

## What the surveyor certifies

An as-built certification is a statement by a licensed professional surveyor (PS) that the as-built measurements shown on the document were performed under their supervision, using appropriate methods, and are accurate to the stated standard.

The surveyor certifies:

- The **measurements** are correct to the stated accuracy.
- The **datum and coordinate system** are as stated.
- The work was performed on the **date(s)** noted.
- The work was performed under the **supervision** of the signing surveyor.

The surveyor does **not** certify:

- That the construction complies with the approved plans (that is an engineering determination).
- That the construction meets code requirements (that is a building-official or engineering determination).
- That the infrastructure will perform as designed (that is an engineering and construction warranty issue).
- Conditions that exist below ground, behind walls, or at times other than the survey date.

This distinction matters for liability. The surveyor is responsible for measurement accuracy; the engineer or architect is responsible for design compliance.

## Sample certification language

The following is a representative certification. Adjust to match the reviewing authority's requirements and your firm's legal counsel's advice.

---

*I, [Name], Professional Surveyor, License No. [Number], State of Indiana, hereby certify that the as-built survey shown hereon was performed under my direct supervision on [Date(s)]. The measurements shown represent the conditions observed at the time of the survey and are accurate to the tolerances stated herein. Horizontal positions are referenced to [datum, projection, zone, realization]. Elevations are referenced to [vertical datum, geoid model].*

*This certification applies only to the features and conditions shown on this document as of the survey date. It does not constitute a certification of compliance with approved construction plans, applicable codes, or engineering design standards. Subsurface conditions, concealed construction, and conditions that may have changed since the survey date are excluded from this certification.*

---

Some jurisdictions provide mandatory certification language. If the reviewing authority provides specific wording, use it exactly.

## Indiana-specific requirements

Under Indiana Code IC 25-21.5 and the administrative rules at 865 IAC 1-12:

- As-built surveys that meet the definition of "surveying" (measurement of real property, natural or constructed features, or the determination of positions) must be performed by or under the supervision of a licensed professional surveyor (PS).
- Documents must bear the surveyor's **seal (stamp), signature, and date**.
- The seal must be the current, active seal issued by the Indiana Professional Licensing Agency (PLA).
- Electronic seals and signatures are acceptable on digital documents, per IC 25-1-19 (Uniform Electronic Transactions Act applicability).

If the as-built includes a boundary determination (e.g., showing the building position relative to property lines and easements), it is unambiguously a survey and requires a PS seal. If the as-built only shows infrastructure positions without reference to property boundaries, some firms treat it as construction documentation — but the conservative and recommended practice in Indiana is to seal all as-built survey documents.

## Stamping and signing

- Place the seal on every sheet of the as-built drawing.
- Sign and date across or adjacent to the seal.
- On multi-sheet sets, the cover sheet must be sealed and signed. Interior sheets should bear at least the seal.
- On digital (PDF) deliverables, apply the seal and signature electronically. Some jurisdictions require a wet signature on the document of record.
- Do not seal a document you did not supervise. If subcontractors performed the field work, the signing PS must have exercised responsible charge (direction, control, and review of the work).

## Professional liability considerations

As-built certifications create professional liability exposure. Manage it by:

- **Limiting the scope** in the certification language. Do not certify compliance, performance, or conditions outside your measurement.
- **Documenting limitations.** If you could not access a structure, could not measure an invert, or suspect a condition you cannot verify, note it on the document.
- **Retaining records.** Keep the field data, data collector raw files, computations, and correspondence for the statute of limitations period (in Indiana, 10 years for improvements to real property under IC 34-11-2-11, but consult your attorney).
- **Carrying insurance.** Professional liability (errors and omissions) insurance should cover as-built survey work. Verify with your carrier.
- **Reviewing before signing.** The signing PS should review the as-built document in detail before sealing. A seal represents responsible charge, not a rubber stamp.
