> **TL;DR**
> 1. Site as-builts confirm that **buildings, grading, and site features** were constructed in conformance with the approved plans — focusing on finished floor elevations, building location relative to setbacks, drainage patterns, and detention basin geometry.
> 2. Shoot **enough spot elevations** to demonstrate that surface water drains away from buildings and toward the stormwater management system as designed — the reviewing authority will check for positive drainage.
> 3. Detention basin as-builts are the most scrutinized element: measure the **bottom elevation, outlet invert, emergency overflow, and side slopes** to verify storage volume.

## Finished floor elevation (FFE)

Shoot the finished floor elevation at every primary entrance. Place the rod on the top of the finished floor surface (not the threshold, not the subfloor).

- If the building has multiple floor elevations (split level, stepped foundation), measure each level.
- Compare the as-built FFE to the design FFE. Many jurisdictions require the FFE to be at or above the design elevation (for flood protection). A lower-than-designed FFE may be a permit violation.
- Record the FFE to 0.01 ft.

## Building corners

Shoot horizontal positions at each exterior corner of the building. The primary purpose is to verify:

- **Setback compliance.** Compare the measured distance from each building face to the property line against the zoning setback requirements.
- **Building location.** Confirm the building is positioned where the site plan shows it. A shift of even 2 to 3 ft can violate a setback or encroach on an easement.
- **Building dimensions.** Compute the building footprint dimensions from the measured corners and compare to the plan. Note any discrepancies.

If the building footprint differs significantly from the plan (rotated, shifted, or dimensionally different), flag the discrepancy in the as-built report.

## Parking lot grades

Shoot spot elevations across the parking lot at a grid spacing sufficient to show the drainage pattern (typically 25 to 50 ft). Key shots:

- **High points** (crowns, ridges between drain areas).
- **Low points** (drain inlets, trench drains).
- **Accessible parking areas** (ADA-compliant slopes: 2.0% maximum in any direction for accessible stalls and access aisles).
- **Drive aisles** (verify cross slope for drainage).
- **Curb line** (flow line elevations to confirm water flows toward inlets).

The as-built should demonstrate that water flows from the parking lot surface to the inlet structures and does not pond in unintended locations.

## Detention basin as-built

Detention basins are the most critical as-built element for stormwater permit compliance. Jurisdictions use the as-built data to verify that the constructed basin provides the storage volume and discharge control shown in the approved stormwater report.

Measure:

| Feature | What to measure |
|---|---|
| Basin bottom | Spot elevations across the bottom at 25 to 50 ft grid. The lowest point must match the design bottom elevation. |
| Side slopes | Measure top of bank and toe of slope at multiple cross sections. Compute the slope and compare to the design (commonly 3:1 or 4:1). |
| Outlet structure | Rim elevation, invert of each pipe, orifice size and invert, weir crest elevation if applicable. |
| Emergency overflow | Spillway invert or overflow pipe invert. This sets the maximum water surface. |
| Forebay (if present) | Bottom elevation, berm elevation, pipe or weir between forebay and main basin. |
| Normal pool (if wet basin) | Water surface elevation at time of survey, outlet control elevation. |

Compute the as-built **stage-storage** curve from the measured geometry and compare to the design curve. If the as-built volume is less than the design volume by more than the jurisdiction's tolerance (commonly 5% to 10%), the basin may need to be regraded.

## Retaining wall as-built

Measure:

- **Top of wall** elevation and horizontal position at each end and at intermediate points (25 to 50 ft intervals).
- **Bottom of wall** (exposed face) elevation and position.
- **Wall length.**
- **Offset from property line or right-of-way** (retaining walls near property boundaries are common sources of encroachment disputes).

If the wall is a critical structural element (engineered wall with a permit), the structural engineer may also require documentation of embedment depth, drainage provisions, and backfill material. The surveyor's role is typically limited to the geometric as-built.

## Grading and drainage verification

Across the site, the as-built must demonstrate that surface water flows as intended:

- Shoot spot elevations along the drainage path from each building to the nearest inlet or basin.
- Verify that the ground slopes away from building foundations (minimum 5% grade for 10 ft, or per local code).
- Check swales: shoot the centerline and both edges to confirm the cross section drains toward the low point.
- Check grading along property lines to verify that the project does not direct additional runoff onto neighboring properties (a common complaint and code violation).

## Landscaping and erosion control (if required)

Some permits require documentation of:

- Permanent erosion-control measures (rip-rap at outlets, stabilized channels, permanent seed/sod).
- Tree preservation — verify that protected trees were not removed or damaged during construction.
- Landscape buffer compliance (setback plantings, screening).

The surveyor may be asked to locate these features; the landscape architect or environmental consultant typically certifies compliance.
