> **TL;DR**
> 1. 865 IAC 1-12 is the Indiana administrative code governing how licensed surveyors must conduct, document, and deliver surveys. Violations are grounds for disciplinary action by the Indiana Professional Licensing Agency (IPLA).
> 2. Key provisions cover monumentation (what types, when required), survey plat content, record-keeping obligations, and minimum accuracy standards.
> 3. Indiana's standards exceed ALTA/NSPS minimums in several areas, notably monumentation and reporting requirements.
> 4. The rule text is available through the Indiana Secretary of State's administrative code portal; every Indiana surveyor should have a current copy at hand.

## Overview

865 IAC 1-12, promulgated under the authority of IC 25-21.5 (the surveyor licensing statute), establishes the minimum standards of practice for licensed surveyors in Indiana. The rule applies to all survey work performed under an Indiana license, whether for private clients, government agencies, or development projects.

The Indiana Board of Registration for Professional Surveyors, operating through the Indiana Professional Licensing Agency (IPLA), enforces these standards. A surveyor found to have violated them faces disciplinary action ranging from a letter of reprimand to license revocation.

## Monumentation requirements

865 IAC 1-12-10 sets out Indiana's monumentation rules:

- **Boundary corners.** Every boundary corner established or re-established must be marked with a durable monument. Acceptable types include iron rods or pipes (minimum 1/2 in. diameter, minimum 24 in. length), survey discs set in concrete, or other permanent materials approved by local practice.
- **Cap or identification.** Every monument set must bear a cap or other permanent marking identifying the surveyor's license number or firm name.
- **When monumentation is required.** Monuments must be set at all corners of the surveyed parcel, at all angle points along the boundary, and at points of curvature/tangency along curved boundaries. If a monument cannot be set at the true corner (e.g., it falls in a roadway or waterway), a reference monument must be set and its offset noted on the plat.
- **Exceeding ALTA minimums.** The 2021 ALTA/NSPS standards require monumentation at corners "unless already marked by existing monuments," but do not mandate specific monument types or sizes. Indiana's 865 IAC is more prescriptive — it specifies minimum dimensions, requires identification caps, and requires monuments at all corners regardless of pre-existing markers (though an existing qualifying monument may be adopted).

## Survey plat content requirements

865 IAC 1-12-11 specifies what must appear on a survey plat:

- Legal description of the property (section, township, range, lot/block if platted).
- Bearings and distances for all boundary lines.
- Curve data for all curved boundaries (radius, arc length, chord bearing, chord distance, delta angle).
- Area in square feet or acres.
- Monuments found and set, with descriptions (type, size, cap stamping, condition).
- Basis of bearings and how it was established.
- Reference to the source of the legal description (deed book/page, instrument number).
- Names of adjoining owners or subdivisions along each boundary line.
- Visible improvements, encroachments, and easements within or crossing the boundary.
- North arrow, scale, date of survey, and date of plat.
- Surveyor's seal, signature, and license number.

## Record-keeping requirements

865 IAC 1-12-13 addresses the surveyor's obligation to maintain project records:

- The surveyor must retain complete records of every survey, including field notes, calculations, research documents, and the final plat.
- Records must be maintained for the life of the surveyor's license plus five years after the license expires, is surrendered, or the surveyor dies.
- Upon retirement, surrender of license, or death, the surveyor (or the estate) must arrange for transfer of records to another licensed surveyor or to the county surveyor's office.
- Records must be sufficient for another competent surveyor to understand and retrace the work without re-performing the research.

## Where Indiana exceeds ALTA minimums

| Topic | ALTA/NSPS 2021 | Indiana 865 IAC 1-12 |
|-------|----------------|----------------------|
| Monumentation type/size | Not specified | Min. 1/2 in. diameter, 24 in. length |
| Monument identification | Not required | Cap with license number required |
| Record retention | Not addressed | Life of license + 5 years |
| Surveyor's report | Not required (narrative optional) | Report required for original surveys; recommended for retracements |
| Adjoiner names on plat | "As disclosed" | Required on all boundary surveys |

## Enforcement

Complaints against a licensed surveyor are filed with IPLA. The Board investigates and may hold a hearing. Possible outcomes:

- Letter of reprimand.
- Probation with conditions (continuing education, supervision).
- Suspension of license.
- Revocation of license.
- Civil penalties up to $1,000 per violation.

Violations of 865 IAC 1-12 are also admissible in civil litigation as evidence that the surveyor failed to meet the standard of care.
