> **TL;DR**
> 1. A corridor sweeps an assembly along an alignment and profile at a chosen frequency to produce a 3D design model.
> 2. Subassemblies expose parameters and targets; targets connect the corridor to surfaces, alignments, and profiles.
> 3. Corridor problems almost always trace back to assembly geometry, target matching, or frequency density - rebuild diagnostics call out which.
