> **TL;DR**
> 1. Civil 3D is AutoCAD with the **Toolspace** (Prospector, Settings, Survey, Toolbox) as its central nervous system.
> 2. Every drawing carries **drawing settings** (units, coordinate system, abbreviations, ambient settings) and **object/label styles**. These come from the **DWT** template you start with.
> 3. Pick the right workspace (`Civil 3D` for design, `Planning and Analysis` rarely) and lock down a company DWT before doing real work.
