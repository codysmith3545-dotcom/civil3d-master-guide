> **TL;DR**
> 1. AASHTO publications are copyrighted and sold through the AASHTO Bookstore at store.transportation.org.
> 2. They are offered as print, single-user PDF, and corporate (multi-user) electronic licenses; pricing differs sharply between the three.
> 3. This repository never republishes AASHTO text — we cite section and edition only. Verbatim AASHTO content lives only inside a properly licensed copy.

## Where to buy

AASHTO sells its publications directly through the AASHTO Bookstore (store.transportation.org). The major design and survey titles a civil practice will need are all there:

- *A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets* (Green Book).
- *Roadside Design Guide* (RDG).
- *AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications.*
- *Manual for Bridge Evaluation.*
- *Guide for the Development of Bicycle Facilities.*
- *Guide for the Planning, Design, and Operation of Pedestrian Facilities.*
- *Mechanistic-Empirical Pavement Design Guide.*
- *Standard Specifications for Transportation Materials and Methods of Sampling and Testing* (the AASHTO materials standards, often paired with ASTM).

Federal Highway Administration manuals (FHWA HEC series, MUTCD, etc.) are not AASHTO publications and are typically free from the FHWA website. AASHTO and FHWA jointly sponsor a few items (notably MUTCD development), but the licensing model differs by document.

## License options

For most major titles AASHTO offers three formats. The exact options vary by title; check the listing.

- **Print.** Bound book, shipped. One physical copy. Allowed to be lent within an office under normal first-sale doctrine, but cannot be photocopied or scanned for distribution.
- **Single-user electronic (PDF).** A licensed PDF with a single-user end-user license agreement (EULA). Read-only or limited-print depending on the title. Cannot be installed on shared drives, intranets, or DMS systems where more than the licensed user has access.
- **Corporate / multi-user electronic.** A site or seat license that allows a defined number of named users or concurrent users in a company. Required if more than one person needs digital access. Pricing scales with seat count.

A few practical points:

- AASHTO has historically offered member discounts to state DOTs and certain associate members; private firms pay full retail.
- Subscription models exist for some titles (annual access, includes interims as published). For LRFD Bridge in particular, a subscription that delivers the interims is worth considering — interims revise the specification between full editions and a static PDF goes stale.
- Buying a single-user PDF and then placing it on a shared network folder is a EULA violation. Buy seats for the people who need it.

## Why we never republish here

This repository is public. AASHTO content is copyrighted. Republishing tables, figures, or text — even short excerpts — would violate the license and expose contributors to a takedown. The rule is simple:

- **Cite by edition and section.** "Green Book, 8th ed., §3.4" tells a reader exactly where to look.
- **Summarize in your own words.** Explaining how a K-table works is fine; reprinting the table is not.
- **Link to the AASHTO Bookstore listing,** not to a pirated PDF.

If your office has a licensed copy and you need internal verbatim notes, keep them in a private knowledge store behind authentication. The licensing buckets the project follows are documented in [CONTRIBUTING.md](../../../CONTRIBUTING.md). AASHTO falls into Bucket 2 — licensed.

## What about state-adopted AASHTO content?

When a state DOT adopts the Green Book by reference in a public manual, the state manual is a government work and may be public-domain (Indiana law treats INDOT publications as state government works). However, the underlying AASHTO text remains AASHTO's copyrighted work — the state's adoption does not put it into the public domain. Treat any AASHTO-derived passage in a state manual the same way: cite, do not copy.
