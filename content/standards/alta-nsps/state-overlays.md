> **TL;DR**
> 1. The 2021 ALTA/NSPS standards are a **national minimum**. Section 3.E states that the surveyor must also comply with the standards of practice in the state where the property is located, and that the **stricter** requirement controls when state and national standards conflict.
> 2. In Indiana, the controlling state standards are **865 IAC 1-12** (Standards of Practice for Professional Surveyors). Key Indiana-specific requirements include seal and signature format, monument-setting specifications, and minimum plat content.
> 3. Neighboring states (Ohio, Illinois, Kentucky) have their own overlays. Firms working across state lines must maintain separate checklists for each state.

## How state overlays work

The ALTA/NSPS standards explicitly defer to state law where state law is stricter. Section 3.E of the 2021 standards provides:

> "The survey shall be conducted in accordance with the then-current accuracy standards and specifications adopted by the state in which the survey is performed."

This means that an ALTA survey in Indiana must satisfy both:

1. Every requirement of the 2021 ALTA/NSPS standards.
2. Every requirement of 865 IAC 1-12 and any other applicable Indiana statutes or rules.

Where the two standards address the same topic and Indiana's requirement is stricter, the Indiana requirement governs. Where the ALTA standard addresses a topic that Indiana does not, the ALTA standard governs.

## Indiana-specific requirements (865 IAC 1-12)

### Seal and signature

- Every survey plat must bear the Indiana Professional Surveyor's **seal** (embossed or digital), **signature**, and **date**.
- The seal must include the surveyor's name, license number, and the words "Professional Surveyor" (or abbreviation "PS") and "State of Indiana."
- Indiana does not permit a firm seal in lieu of the individual surveyor's seal.

### Monument requirements

- Indiana requires that property corners be monumented with a **permanent marker** — typically a minimum 5/8-inch (16 mm) diameter iron rod or pipe, at least 24 inches (610 mm) long, driven flush or below grade, with a cap bearing the surveyor's license number.
- Where setting a monument is impractical (e.g., in pavement or at a corner of a building), the surveyor must set a reference monument and note the offset on the plat.
- The ALTA standards require the surveyor to "set monuments as necessary" but do not specify minimum size or material. Indiana's specification is stricter and controls.

### Plat content

865 IAC 1-12 requires the following on every survey plat:

- **Basis of bearings** declared (State Plane Indiana East or West Zone, NAD83(2011), or other stated basis).
- **Closure precision** stated on the plat.
- **Date of field work** (ALTA also requires this, so the requirements align).
- **Bar scale and north arrow.**
- **Legend** identifying monument types and symbols.
- **Boundary dimensions** (bearings to the nearest second of arc, distances to 0.01 foot).

Most of these align with the ALTA Section 6 requirements, but Indiana's bearing and distance precision specifications (nearest second, 0.01 foot) are explicit where the ALTA standards are stated in terms of "relative positional precision."

### Minimum accuracy

Indiana classifies surveys by type and assigns minimum accuracy. For boundary surveys (Class A), the minimum closure is typically 1:15,000 or better. The ALTA standards require a relative positional precision calculation rather than a closure ratio. Both must be satisfied — report both the Indiana closure and the ALTA relative positional precision on the plat.

## Comparison to neighboring states

Firms working in the Indianapolis metro area occasionally perform ALTA surveys in neighboring states, particularly on properties near the state line. Key differences:

### Ohio

- Ohio Administrative Code 4733-37 and 4733-38 govern survey standards.
- Ohio requires monuments with a cap bearing the surveyor's registration number.
- Ohio requires an "opinion of title" statement on boundary surveys — a requirement that goes beyond the ALTA standard.
- Ohio requires bearings referenced to a stated meridian and distances in U.S. survey feet.

### Illinois

- Illinois Professional Land Surveyor Act of 1989 (225 ILCS 330) and the Illinois Administrative Code (68 Ill. Adm. Code 1270) govern survey standards.
- Illinois requires a minimum 5/8-inch (16 mm) rebar monument with an identification cap.
- Illinois plat requirements include a basis-of-bearings statement and closure data, similar to Indiana.
- Illinois has specific requirements for surveys in Cook County (Chicago area) that do not apply to downstate counties.

### Kentucky

- 201 KAR 18:150 governs minimum standards for surveys in Kentucky.
- Kentucky requires a monument with a cap bearing the surveyor's license number.
- Kentucky requires a "monument record" filed with the county clerk for each survey.
- Kentucky recognizes both the metes-and-bounds system and a limited PLSS system (the Virginia Military District in the northern part of the state was not surveyed under PLSS).

## Practical workflow

For an ALTA survey in Indiana:

1. Start with the 2021 ALTA/NSPS checklist (see [ALTA traps](alta-traps.md)).
2. Overlay Indiana 865 IAC 1-12 requirements on each item.
3. Where Indiana is stricter, follow Indiana.
4. Where ALTA is stricter (e.g., addressing every Schedule B-II exception, which Indiana does not specifically require), follow ALTA.
5. Include both the ALTA certification language and the Indiana seal/signature/date requirements on the plat.
