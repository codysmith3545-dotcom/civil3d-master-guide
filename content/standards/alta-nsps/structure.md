> **TL;DR**
> 1. The ALTA/NSPS standards are the joint minimum standards of the American Land Title Association and the National Society of Professional Surveyors for "ALTA surveys" — the land-title surveys ordered when title insurance with extended coverage is being issued.
> 2. The current edition is the 2021 standards, effective February 23, 2021. They are organized in Sections 1 through 6 plus Table A.
> 3. This page describes Sections 1–6 in plain English; it does not republish the official text. Always work from a current, licensed copy of the standards.

## What an ALTA survey is

An *ALTA/NSPS Land Title Survey* is a survey performed to a national minimum standard adopted jointly by ALTA (the title-insurance industry) and NSPS (the surveying profession). Title insurers rely on it to issue extended coverage that removes the standard "survey exception" from the policy. Because the lender, the buyer, and the title insurer all rely on the same survey, the standards exist to make sure everyone gets a comparable product.

The 2021 standards superseded the 2016 standards. They are copyrighted by ALTA and NSPS and made available through NSPS — see the link in the sources block.

## Sections 1 through 6 in plain English

### Section 1 — Purpose

States that the standards establish the minimum requirements for an ALTA/NSPS Land Title Survey. The survey is a product, not a process; the specifications target what must appear on the plat and in the report rather than how the field work is conducted. Optional Table A items (see [Table A](table-a.md)) are part of the survey when negotiated.

### Section 2 — Request for Survey

Defines the inputs the surveyor needs from the client before work begins. The two universally required items are:

- A **written authorization** to proceed.
- A **current title commitment** (or, where unavailable, the documents that would otherwise have been listed in Schedule B).

Section 2 also lists the optional items the client may request from Table A. The surveyor needs the title commitment because the standards require the surveyor to address every Schedule B exception and identify whether each one can be plotted.

### Section 3 — Surveying Standards and Standards of Care

Describes the professional standard the survey must meet. Key concepts:

- **Boundary resolution.** The surveyor must perform research adequate to resolve the boundary, going beyond the title commitment when necessary (adjoiner deeds, prior surveys, recorded plats).
- **Field measurement.** Measurements must meet the relative positional precision that the standards specify (a 95-percent confidence interval calculation; the result is reported on the plat).
- **Standard of care.** The work must conform to the standard of care of a prudent surveyor licensed in the state in which the property lies; state-specific rules govern where they are stricter than the ALTA/NSPS standards.

### Section 4 — Records Research

Lists the records the surveyor must consult or obtain, including:

- The current title commitment with legible copies of every Schedule B-II item.
- Adjoiner deeds and plats sufficient to resolve the boundary.
- Records of any recorded easements, rights-of-way, and encumbrances burdening or benefiting the property.

If the title commitment is incomplete, the surveyor notes that limitation in the report.

### Section 5 — Field Work

Describes what must be observed and measured in the field. The list includes, at a minimum:

- Monuments found and set; their description and condition.
- Physical evidence of boundaries, including fences, walls, occupational lines.
- Buildings and significant improvements on the surveyed property, with ties to the boundary.
- Evidence of easements, rights of way, servitudes, and other matters affecting title that are observable from a careful inspection of the property.
- Evidence of cemeteries, burial grounds, and so on.
- Evidence of utilities serving or crossing the property (the depth and content of utility location is governed by Table A item 11 — see [Table A](table-a.md)).
- Water boundaries, where applicable.

### Section 6 — Plat or Map

Specifies what appears on the deliverable. Items include:

- Title block with property description, surveyor's name and license, date of fieldwork, and bar scale.
- North arrow with basis of bearings.
- Boundary lines with bearings and distances; curve data; area.
- Every Schedule B-II item that can be plotted, plotted; each item that cannot be plotted, addressed with a note.
- Adjoining ownerships shown.
- Improvements within the boundary and offsets to boundary lines.
- The standard ALTA/NSPS [certification](certification.md) language, modified only as the standards permit.

The 2021 edition tightened some requirements around showing offsite easements, addressing observed evidence of utilities, and improving the relationship between the field observation and the title-commitment review.
