> **TL;DR**
> 1. Table A is the menu of optional items a client can negotiate into an ALTA/NSPS survey beyond the baseline of Sections 1–6.
> 2. Items 1–21 cover monumentation, addresses, zoning, flood, contours, utilities, easements outside the parcel, wetlands, and offsite improvements.
> 3. The frequently requested items on a commercial transaction are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6(b), 7(a), 8, 11, 13, 16, 17, and 19; everything else is by negotiation.

This page summarizes Table A in plain English. It does not republish the official text; rely on a current licensed copy of the 2021 ALTA/NSPS standards for the binding language.

## How Table A works

The contract between the client and the surveyor enumerates the Table A items that apply. Items not listed are not part of the survey, even if their subject matter is observable. Some items have sub-options the client must elect — for example, item 6 distinguishes between current-zoning lookup and zoning-letter retrieval; item 7 separates exterior dimensions from gross floor area.

If a Table A item is requested but cannot be performed (for example, the local jurisdiction does not respond to a zoning-letter request), the surveyor reports the limitation rather than silently dropping the deliverable.

## The items, 1 through 21

1. **Monumentation.** Set monuments at corners required by the contract or by state law. *Commonly requested.*
2. **Addresses.** Show the addresses of the surveyed property and visible adjoining properties. *Commonly requested.*
3. **Flood-zone classification.** State the FEMA flood zone, panel, and effective date for the surveyed property. *Commonly requested.*
4. **Gross land area.** Calculate and show the area of the surveyed property; very large parcels can include acreage to a stated precision. *Commonly requested.*
5. **Vertical relief — contours.** Provide contours at a specified interval, tied to a stated vertical datum.
6. **Zoning.** (a) Provide current zoning information from a stated source. (b) Retrieve a zoning report or zoning letter and reflect the building setback, height, and floor-area limits on the survey. *Item 6(b) is commonly requested on commercial deals.*
7. **Building information.** (a) Exterior dimensions of buildings at ground level. (b) Square footage of exterior footprint. (c) Measured height of buildings to a defined point. *Item 7(a) is commonly requested.*
8. **Substantial features observed.** Locate substantial features such as parking areas (with striping count if requested), sidewalks, signs, and similar improvements within the surveyed property. *Commonly requested.*
9. **Parking.** Number of striped parking spaces; striping configuration; handicap-accessible spaces.
10. **Party walls.** Existence and location of party walls.
11. **Underground utilities.** Show evidence of underground utilities based on (a) plans furnished by utility companies and (b) markings from a utility-locate request such as Indiana 811. The standards are explicit that the surveyor is reporting *evidence*, not making warranties about location accuracy. *Commonly requested.*
12. **Governmental agency survey requirements.** Items required by a specific government program (for example, certain HUD or SBA forms).
13. **Names of adjoining owners.** Per current public records. *Commonly requested.*
14. **Distance to nearest intersecting street.** A measured tie from the surveyed property to the nearest intersection of streets.
15. **Rectangular survey system data.** Section, township, and range data, where applicable.
16. **Observed evidence of earth moving, building construction, or building additions** within a recent stated period (typically since a date in the contract). *Commonly requested.*
17. **Proposed changes in street right-of-way lines.** Information from controlling jurisdictions on planned ROW changes. *Commonly requested.*
18. **Observed evidence of wetlands as delineated by a qualified specialist.** The surveyor plots delineation flags placed by another professional.
19. **Plottable offsite easements.** Easements outside the surveyed property that benefit it, plotted to the extent the documents allow. *Commonly requested.*
20. **Professional liability insurance.** A statement that the surveyor carries professional liability insurance at a stated minimum. (Sub-items vary by edition.)
21. **Other.** A catch-all for items that do not fit elsewhere, negotiated explicitly in the contract.

The exact wording, sub-options, and item count have evolved across editions. The 2021 edition revised several items and added new sub-options (notably around offsite easements and underground utilities). Confirm against the current standards.

## Practical notes

- Always read the title commitment before scoping Table A. Some title-commitment exceptions only become plottable after a Table A item (for example, item 19) is added.
- For Indiana commercial transactions, item 6(b) is often the long-pole item — municipalities can take weeks to issue zoning letters. Start it the day the contract is signed.
- Item 11 utility locate evidence depends on Indiana 811 marks, which expire. Coordinate the locate ticket with the field schedule.
