> **TL;DR**
> 1. The Indiana Design Manual (IDM) is INDOT's comprehensive design guide, published as individual chapter PDFs on the INDOT website. It governs geometric design, drainage, pavement, structures, traffic, and environmental review for all INDOT-let projects.
> 2. Civil/survey practitioners use Chapters 40-46 (roadway geometric design), Chapters 28-29 and 203 (drainage and hydrology), Chapter 55 (quantities), and Chapter 81 (right-of-way) most frequently.
> 3. The IDM is a government-authored work and may be mirrored verbatim. Always reference the current edition — INDOT updates chapters periodically without a single annual release date.

## Chapter overview

The IDM is organized into numbered chapters grouped by topic area. The following table summarizes every chapter with the audience most likely to use it.

| 9 | Public Involvement | Project managers, environmental staff |

| 14 | Design Documentation | Design engineers, surveyors |
| 15 | Value Engineering | Project managers |
| 17 | Context Sensitive Solutions | Design engineers |
| 19 | Traffic Engineering Studies | Traffic engineers |

### Survey and Mapping (Chapters 20-25)

| Chapter | Title | Primary audience |
|---|---|---|
| 20 | Survey Procedures | **Land surveyors** |
| 21 | Photogrammetry | Surveyors, mapping |
| 22 | Subsurface Investigation | Geotechnical engineers |
| 23 | Mapping | Surveyors, designers |
| 24 | Right-of-Way Surveys | **Land surveyors** |
| 25 | Utilities | Utility coordinators |

Chapter 20 (Survey Procedures) is particularly important for Civil 3D survey workflows. It defines INDOT's requirements for horizontal and vertical control, GPS methods, traverse closure standards, and deliverable formats.

### Drainage (Chapters 28-29, 200-series)

| Chapter | Title | Primary audience |
|---|---|---|
| 28 | Open Channels and Ditches | Drainage engineers |
| 29 | Storm Drainage | **Civil engineers, site designers** |
| 200 | Hydrology | Drainage engineers |
| 201 | Hydraulics of Bridges | Bridge/hydraulic engineers |
| 202 | Hydraulics of Culverts | **Civil engineers** |
| 203 | Storm Water Design | **Site designers** |

Chapter 29 and 203 are the primary references for site-development stormwater design on INDOT projects. They define inlet spacing, pipe sizing criteria, and detention requirements.

### Geometric Design (Chapters 40-46)

| Chapter | Title | Primary audience |
|---|---|---|
| 40 | General Design Information | **All designers** |
| 41 | Design Speed and Functional Classification | **All designers** |
| 42 | Intersections | **Civil engineers** |
| 43 | Interchanges | Highway designers |
| 44 | Horizontal Alignment | **Civil engineers, surveyors** |
| 45 | Vertical Alignment | **Civil engineers** |
| 46 | Cross Section Elements | **Civil engineers** |

These chapters correspond directly to Civil 3D alignment, profile, and corridor design. Chapter 44 provides minimum curve radii, superelevation rates, and sight-distance criteria used in horizontal alignment design. Chapter 45 covers K-values for vertical curves.

### Pavement and Structures (Chapters 52-56, 400-series)

| Chapter | Title | Primary audience |

| 55 | Quantities and Estimates | **All designers** |

Chapter 56 (CADD Standards) defines INDOT's layer naming, linetype, and file-naming conventions for plan deliverables. It aligns with the National CAD Standard (NCS) with INDOT-specific modifications.

## How to access

All IDM chapters are published as free PDFs at [https://www.in.gov/indot/design-manual/](https://www.in.gov/indot/design-manual/). INDOT updates individual chapters on a rolling basis; check the revision date on the chapter's title page. There is no single annual edition — each chapter has its own revision history.

Bookmark the main page and check for updates quarterly, or subscribe to INDOT's email notification list for design manual revisions.

## Relationship to AASHTO

The IDM is based on AASHTO's *A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets* ("Green Book") and other AASHTO publications. Where the IDM is silent, AASHTO governs. Where the IDM specifies a stricter or different value, the IDM controls for INDOT projects. Always cite the IDM value for Indiana work, not the generic AASHTO value.
