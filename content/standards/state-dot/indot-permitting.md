> **TL;DR**
> 1. Any work within INDOT right-of-way requires a permit. The most common types are: **Access Permit** (driveways and new connections), **Utility Permit** (installing or maintaining utilities in the ROW), and **Oversize/Overweight Permit** (heavy or wide loads).
> 2. Access permits require a site plan showing the driveway location, sight-distance analysis, and compliance with IDM Chapter 42 (Intersections) and Indiana Administrative Code 105 IAC 7. Processing time is typically 30-60 days.
> 3. Utility permits require a plan showing the utility alignment within the ROW, crossing method (bore, open-cut, directional drill), and restoration details. INDOT reviews for conflicts with existing facilities and future projects.

## Access Permits (driveway permits)

### When required

An access permit is required for:

- New driveways connecting to a state highway.
- Modifications to existing driveways (widening, relocation, change of use from residential to commercial).
- Temporary construction access from a state highway.
- Field entrances on rural state highways.

### Requirements

The applicant must submit:

1. **Application form** (INDOT Access Permit Application, available online).
2. **Site plan** showing:
   - Driveway location relative to the nearest intersection, property lines, and existing driveways.
   - Driveway width, throat length, radii, and grade.
   - Sight-distance triangles (per IDM Chapter 42 and AASHTO).
   - Drainage provisions (the driveway must not obstruct the roadside ditch or storm drainage).
3. **Traffic data** for commercial driveways: ADT, peak-hour turning movements, and trip generation (per ITE Trip Generation Manual).
4. **Drainage analysis** if the driveway affects the roadside ditch or if a culvert pipe is required under the driveway.

### Design standards

Driveway design must comply with:

- **105 IAC 7** (Indiana Administrative Code, Article 7 — Access Control): defines driveway spacing, width, and operational requirements by highway classification.
- **IDM Chapter 42**: intersection design criteria including sight distance, turn-lane warrants, and geometric standards.
- **ADA**: driveway crossings of sidewalks must maintain sidewalk accessibility (detectable warning surfaces, cross-slope limits).

Typical dimensional requirements:

| Parameter | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| Driveway width | 12-24 ft | 24-36 ft (two-way) |
| Minimum radius | 5 ft | 25-50 ft |
| Maximum grade at ROW line | 8% | 5% |
| Sight distance (stopping) | Per IDM Table 42-3 (design speed dependent) | Per IDM Table 42-3 |
| Minimum spacing from intersection | 100-200 ft (varies by road class) | Per 105 IAC 7 tables |

### Processing

- Submit to the INDOT district office responsible for the highway segment.
- Review typically takes 30-60 days.
- INDOT may request modifications (relocate driveway, add a turn lane, install a culvert pipe).
- The permit, once issued, includes conditions (maintenance responsibility, drainage, future-widening provisions).
- Work must begin within one year of permit issuance; the permit expires if not used.

## Utility Permits

### When required

Any installation, maintenance, or modification of utility facilities (water, sewer, gas, electric, telecommunications, fiber optic) within INDOT right-of-way requires a utility permit.

### Requirements

1. **Application form** (INDOT Utility Permit Application).
2. **Utility plan** showing:
   - Alignment of the utility within the ROW (plan and profile).
   - Crossing method: open-cut, bore, directional drill, or aerial.
   - Depth of cover (INDOT requires minimum cover of 36 in. for underground crossings of state highways, 24 in. for longitudinal installations outside the pavement area — verify with current policy).
   - Existing utilities in the area (from utility locate data).
   - Restoration details (pavement repair, ditch restoration, seeding).
3. **Traffic control plan** if lane closures or flagging is required during installation.
4. **Liability insurance** documentation.

### Key policies

- **Longitudinal occupancy**: utility lines running parallel to the highway should be placed as close to the ROW line as practical, outside the clear zone. INDOT discourages longitudinal installation under the pavement.
- **Crossings**: perpendicular crossings are preferred. Bore or directional drill is required under pavement; open-cut is allowed only with INDOT approval and requires full-depth pavement restoration.
- **Attachments to structures**: utilities attached to bridges or retaining walls require a separate structural review and approval.
- **Future conflicts**: INDOT may require relocation at the utility owner's expense if a future highway project conflicts with the permitted installation. This is a standard condition of the permit.

### Processing

- Submit to the INDOT district utility coordinator.
- Review typically takes 30-45 days.
- INDOT coordinates with its district maintenance and project development offices to check for conflicts with planned projects.

## Oversize/Overweight Permits

### When required

Vehicles exceeding Indiana's legal dimensions or weight limits on state highways:

- **Oversize**: width > 8'6", height > 13'6", length > 53 ft (trailer) or 65 ft (combination).
- **Overweight**: gross weight > 80,000 lbs or axle weight exceeding per-axle limits (20,000 lbs single axle, 34,000 lbs tandem).

### Types

- **Single trip**: valid for one movement over a specified route.
- **Annual**: valid for multiple trips within a year, subject to weight and dimension limits.
- **Superload**: loads exceeding 120,000 lbs gross or 16 ft wide. Requires route survey, bridge analysis, and escort vehicles. Processing time: 2-4 weeks minimum.

### Application

INDOT processes oversize/overweight permits through the **Indiana Oversize/Overweight Permitting System** (online portal). The system checks the requested route against bridge load ratings and construction restrictions automatically for standard permits. Superloads require manual review.

## Railroad Crossing Permits

Work at or near railroad crossings on state highways requires coordination between INDOT, the railroad company, and the Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission (IURC):

- **New crossing**: rare; IURC petition required. INDOT and the railroad must both consent.
- **Modification of existing crossing**: signal upgrades, surface replacement, or geometric changes require an IURC order and INDOT permit.
- **Utility crossing of railroad ROW**: separate railroad permit (not INDOT) plus an INDOT utility permit if within the highway ROW.

Processing time for railroad coordination: 3-6 months or more due to IURC review.

## General tips

- **Start early.** INDOT permits have processing times that can affect project schedules. Submit as soon as the design is sufficiently complete.
- **Pre-application meeting.** For complex access permits (commercial developments with significant traffic impact), request a pre-application meeting with the INDOT district. This prevents surprises during formal review.
- **Check the district.** Indiana has six INDOT districts (Crawfordsville, Fort Wayne, Greenfield, LaPorte, Seymour, Vincennes). Submit to the district responsible for the project location.
- **Local permits too.** An INDOT permit covers the state ROW only. If the project is within a city or town, a separate municipal right-of-way or driveway permit may also be required.

## Common gotchas

- **Permit vs project plans.** An INDOT access permit for a commercial driveway does not substitute for a local site plan approval. The municipality's stormwater, zoning, and building permits are separate processes.
- **Sight distance at field entrances.** Rural field entrances on state highways still require adequate sight distance. INDOT can deny a field entrance permit if the sight line is obstructed.
- **Utility relocation cost.** If a permitted utility conflicts with a future INDOT project, the utility owner bears the relocation cost. This is a significant financial risk for utility companies and their clients.
- **Annual oversize permits and route restrictions.** Annual permits typically exclude interstates and certain restricted routes. Verify route availability in the permitting system before purchasing.
